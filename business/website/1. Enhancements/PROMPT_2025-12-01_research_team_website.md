# Research Team Prompt: Website Optimization Analysis

**Objective:** Conduct comprehensive research to identify and recommend improvements to TheGrantScout landing page (thegrantscout.com). Produce actionable deliverables that the Dev Team can implement directly.

---

## Context

You are the Research Team for TheGrantScout, a grant discovery service. Before beginning this analysis, read the companion document **THEGRANTSCOUT_SUMMARY.md** to understand:
- What our product does
- How our matching algorithm works
- Our key differentiators
- Who our beta users are
- Our competitive positioning

This context is essential—your recommendations must align with what we actually offer and what makes us different.

---

## Phase 1: Current State Audit

**Goal:** Document exactly what exists on thegrantscout.com today.

**Tasks:**
1. Visit thegrantscout.com and screenshot every section (hero, features, how it works, pricing, FAQ, footer)
2. Create a text inventory of all copy:
   - Headlines and subheads
   - Body copy
   - CTAs (button text)
   - Claims made (statistics, promises)
3. Note the visual hierarchy:
   - What's above the fold?
   - What order do sections appear?
   - Color usage and whitespace
4. Test on mobile—screenshot mobile view
5. Identify issues:
   - Unclear messaging
   - Missing information
   - Broken elements
   - Friction points in user flow

**Output:** `01_WEBSITE_AUDIT.md`
- Annotated screenshots
- Complete copy inventory
- Issue list with severity ratings (Critical/High/Medium/Low)

## Phase 2: Competitor Deep Dive

**Goal:** Understand how competitors position themselves and what works.

**Competitors to analyze:**

| Competitor | Type | URL |
|------------|------|-----|
| Instrumentl | Direct (premium) | instrumentl.com |
| GrantStation | Direct (mid-market) | grantstation.com |
| Foundation Directory Online | Direct (premium) | fdo.foundationcenter.org |
| GrantWatch | Direct (budget) | grantwatch.com |
| OpenGrants | Direct (emerging) | opengrants.io |
| Grantable | Adjacent (writing) | grantable.co |

**For each competitor, document:**

1. **Hero Section**
   - Exact headline text
   - Subhead/value proposition
   - Primary CTA
   - Visual style

2. **How They Explain Their Process**
   - Number of steps
   - Language used
   - Specificity level

3. **Social Proof**
   - Testimonials (how many, from whom)
   - Logos
   - Statistics cited
   - Case studies

4. **Pricing Presentation**
   - Tier names
   - Price points
   - What's highlighted
   - Free trial/freemium?

5. **Unique Differentiators**
   - What do they emphasize that others don't?
   - What language do they own?

6. **Visual Style**
   - Color palette
   - Photography vs illustrations
   - Tone (corporate, friendly, urgent)

**Output:** `02_COMPETITOR_ANALYSIS.md`
- Comparison matrix (spreadsheet format)
- Screenshot gallery organized by competitor
- Key takeaways: What works? What doesn't? What can we steal?

## Phase 3: Best Practices Research

**Goal:** Identify what converts nonprofit decision-makers.

**Research Areas:**

1. **Nonprofit Buyer Psychology**
   - What objections do grant seekers have about tools like this?
   - What motivates them to try a new service?
   - What language resonates (ROI? Time savings? Success rates?)

2. **SaaS Landing Page Best Practices**
   - What's the optimal structure?
   - How many sections?
   - Above-the-fold priorities

3. **Social Proof That Works for Nonprofits**
   - Do testimonials matter more than logos?
   - What metrics are most compelling?
   - Case study formats that convert

4. **Trust Signals for This Audience**
   - What makes nonprofits trust a new tool?
   - Security/privacy concerns?
   - Data source credibility

**Sources to check:**
- Nonprofit marketing blogs (Classy, Network for Good, Nonprofit Tech for Good)
- SaaS landing page teardowns (Julian Shapiro, Harry Dry's Marketing Examples)
- Reddit: r/nonprofit, r/grants, r/fundraising
- LinkedIn nonprofit groups
- Nonprofit Times, Chronicle of Philanthropy

**Output:** `03_BEST_PRACTICES.md`
- Summarized findings with sources
- Specific, actionable recommendations
- Examples of landing pages that do it well (with screenshots)

## Phase 4: Differentiation Analysis

**Goal:** Crystallize what makes TheGrantScout unique and how to communicate it.

**Questions to answer:**

1. **What do we do that competitors don't?**
   - Prior relationship weighting (67% vs 8% stat)
   - F990 depth (1.6M grants, 8 years)
   - Explainable matching (not black box)
   - Research-informed approach

2. **What statistics are most compelling?**
   - Rank our proof points by impact
   - Which claims need citation/proof?
   - What would make a skeptical nonprofit pay attention?

3. **How should we position against Instrumentl specifically?**
   - They're the 800-pound gorilla
   - Price positioning (we're 50-70% cheaper)
   - Feature positioning (relationship intelligence vs. keyword matching)

4. **What's our "only we" statement?**
   - "Only TheGrantScout..." — finish this sentence
   - What can we claim that no competitor can?

**Output:** `04_DIFFERENTIATION_POSITIONING.md`
- 3 headline/subhead variations
- Key differentiators ranked by importance
- Proof points with recommended placement
- "Only we" positioning statement
