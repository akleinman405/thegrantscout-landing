## Removed/Ineligible Opportunities (QA Validated)

### 1. ETP Healthcare Workforce Fund - REMOVED

**Reason:** EXPIRED - Funding depleted March 2023

**Details:** The Employment Training Panel (ETP) Healthcare Workforce Fund was a California state program that has exhausted its funding allocation. No new applications are being accepted.

**Alternative:** Consider HRSA Health Workforce grants for similar training-focused funding.

---

### 2. Andrew Sabin Foundation - REMOVED

**Reason:** NO PUBLIC ACCESS - Institutional partnerships only

**Details:** The Andrew Sabin Foundation operates through established institutional partnerships and does not accept unsolicited proposals from organizations outside their existing network.

**Alternative:** None identified. Focus on foundations with open application processes.

---

### 3. HCAI Grants - REMOVED

**Reason:** WRONG ELIGIBILITY - For educational institutions, not advocacy nonprofits

**Details:** The California Health Care Access and Information (HCAI) grants are designed for accredited educational institutions offering healthcare training programs. Patient safety advocacy nonprofits like PSMF do not meet the eligibility criteria.

**Alternative:** Partner with an accredited educational institution to access HCAI funding jointly.

## Limited Access Opportunities (Flagged)

### California Endowment - INVITATION-ONLY

**Access Status:** Does NOT accept unsolicited proposals

**Strategy for Access:**
1. Submit inquiry form at calendow.org/grant-inquiry-form/
2. Identify and partner with current TCE grantees (see calendow.org/all-grantees/)
3. Attend California health equity convenings where TCE staff participate
4. Build relationships over 6-12 month period
5. Frame all engagement around racial justice and health equity

**Recent Grantmaking:** $381 million in 2023 to 710 organizations

---

### Gordon Moore Foundation - INVITATION-ONLY

**Access Status:** Does NOT accept unsolicited proposals; "moonshot" for new grantseekers

**Strategy for Access:**
1. Submit 100-word inquiry to inquiry@moore.org
2. Build visibility through published patient safety research
3. Seek introductions through existing Moore grantees
4. Consider Moore Inventor Fellows program as alternative path (nominations due November annually)

**Key Intelligence:** Foundation phased out patient care program to create new programs including wildfire prevention. Patient care remains a focus area but with reduced new grantmaking.

## Funder Deep Dive Notes

### Josiah Macy Jr. Foundation

**Critical Change (2024):** Transitioned from open call to solicited approach. Now issues formal Requests for Applications (RFAs) based on their priorities.

**RFA Timing:** Typically issued in January; awards in Summer/Fall

**2024 Investment:** Over $2.2 million in health professions education

**Key Current Initiatives:**
- AI in Medical Education (up to $200K for demonstration projects)
- Disability Inclusion in Nursing (partnership with RWJF, $1.2M program)
- Catalyst Awards for GME ($50K each to 6 institutions)

**Priority Areas:**
1. Diversity, equity, and belonging in clinical learning environments
2. Interprofessional collaboration and high-performing teams
3. Ethical dilemmas navigation for health professionals

**Leadership Note:** President Holly J. Humphrey, MD passed away April 17, 2025. Leadership transition may affect grantmaking focus.

**PSMF Strategy:** Position patient safety curriculum development as addressing all three priority areas. Partner with medical/nursing school for eligibility.

### Commonwealth Fund

**Grant Statistics (FY 2024-25):**
- Average grant size: $178,699
- Average duration: 11 months
- Process: Rolling LOI submissions

**LOI Requirements:**
- 3 pages maximum (approximately 2,500 words)
- 12-point font, Word document
- No full budget required at LOI stage
- Response within 6 weeks

**Collaborative Process:** If LOI is identified as potential fit, program staff engage in collaborative proposal development.

**Key Focus Areas:**
- Healthcare quality improvement
- Health equity advancement
- Access and affordability
- International health system comparisons

**Recent Major Reports:**
- Mirror, Mirror 2024 (international comparison)
- 2025 State Health System Scorecard

**Trust-Based Philanthropy Note:** Reporting requirements waived for grantees under trust-based principles.

**PSMF Strategy:** Submit LOI focusing on data transparency and systems approach to patient harm reduction. Emphasize measurable outcomes and policy implications.

### AHRQ Patient Safety Grants

**Key Program:** R18 - Health Services Research Demonstration and Dissemination Grants

**Funding Limits:** Up to $500,000 total costs annually

**Important Eligibility Note:** AHRQ's authorizing legislation prohibits for-profit organizations from leading applications. Only nonprofit organizations may be lead applicants.

**Current Priority Areas:**
- Patient safety improvement
- Healthcare delivery and practice improvement
- Whole-person healthcare delivery
- Diagnostic safety
- AI and patient safety
- Healthcare-associated infection prevention

**Active Related Programs:**
- IDEAS Project: Diagnostic safety tools (recruiting through September 2025)
- HAI Prevention Program: CLABSI, CAUTI, VAP/VAE (rolling out 2025-2026)
- AI Safety Research: Active research priority

**Application Requirements (as of January 25, 2025):**
- New FORMS-I requirements apply
- Standard NIH due dates (February 5, June 5, October 5)

**PSMF Strategy:** Partner with healthcare delivery organization for implementation study. Focus on APSS toolkit dissemination and measurable safety outcomes.

### NIOSH R13 Conference Support

**FOA Details:** PAR-21-148
**Expiration:** December 15, 2025

**Award Limits:**
- Up to $20,000 total costs (grant mechanism)
- Up to $30,000 (cooperative agreement)
- Up to $75,000 with prior NIOSH approval

**Key Restrictions:**
- Domestic conferences only
- No training-only events
- No indirect costs allowed
- Award must precede conference date
- Submit at least 6 months before conference

**NORA Alignment Required:** Applications must address National Occupational Research Agenda priorities, including Healthcare and Social Assistance sector.

**PSMF Strategy:** Frame Summit sessions around healthcare worker safety (occupational health angle). Include sessions on burnout, second victim phenomenon, safe patient handling. Submit for 2026 Summit by December 12, 2025.

### HRSA Health Workforce

**Bureau:** Bureau of Health Workforce (BHW)

**Eligibility:** Nonprofits with 501(c)(3) status, community-based organizations, faith-based organizations, tribal organizations

**FY 2024 Awards:**
- Medical Student Education: $11 million to 3 grantees
- Opioid-Impacted Family Support: $63 million to 32 grantees
- Total BHW programs: $1.4 billion to 1,597 grantees

**Relevant Programs:**
- Health and Public Safety Workforce Resiliency Training Program
- Behavioral Health Workforce Development
- Regional Public Health Training Centers
- Nursing Workforce Development

**Partnership Requirement:** Many HRSA grants require partnership with clinical training sites or accredited health professions programs.

**PSMF Strategy:** Partner with clinical training institution in underserved area. Focus on workforce resiliency and patient safety training integration.

**Key Focus Areas:**
- Health equity and racial justice
- Social determinants of health
- Community resilience

- Disability justice partnership with WITH Foundation

**Application Features:**

**PSMF Strategy:** Frame patient safety as health equity issue. Emphasize disparities in preventable harm. Propose measurable equity outcomes.
