## About This Checklist

This preparation checklist was designed specifically for Senior Network Services' attendance at the San Mateo County Area Agency on Aging bidder's conference on January 14, 2025. As an incumbent service provider, you have significant competitive advantages that this checklist helps you leverage effectively. The checklist is organized into three phases: Pre-Conference Preparation (due by January 10), During Conference actions (January 14), and Post-Conference follow-up (by January 17). Use this tool to ensure you maximize the strategic value of your incumbent position and gather all critical information needed for a winning contract renewal proposal.

## Pre-Conference Preparation (Due by January 10)

### Research and Intelligence Gathering

- [ ] Review current AAA contract terms and performance metrics
  - Contract start/end dates
  - Service categories (I&A, HICAP, Ombudsman, Housing)
  - Budget amounts by service line
  - Performance benchmarks and targets

- [ ] Document all service delivery successes from current contract
  - Total seniors served annually
  - Call volume and response times
  - Successful HICAP counseling sessions completed
  - Ombudsman complaint resolutions achieved
  - Housing placements/assistance provided
  - Notable success stories (anonymized)

- [ ] Compile outcome data showing impact
  - Client satisfaction survey scores
  - Service unit totals by category
  - Year-over-year growth trends
  - Cost per unit of service metrics
  - Geographic coverage statistics

- [ ] Identify any service gaps or expansion opportunities
  - Unmet needs in service area
  - Geographic areas with limited coverage
  - New populations that could be served
  - Potential new service categories (Title III-E, III-D)

- [ ] Research competitor organizations (if any)
  - Other CBOs serving seniors in Santa Cruz/San Benito
  - Their service areas and capabilities
  - Any recent expansions or changes
  - Potential partnership vs. competition dynamics

- [ ] Prepare 2-3 strategic questions to ask at conference
  - Question 1: What changes in evaluation criteria should we expect from the previous RFP cycle?
  - Question 2: Are there new service priorities or populations of focus for the 2025-29 cycle?
  - Question 3: How will demonstrated incumbent performance be weighted in the evaluation process?

- [ ] Prepare 30-second "incumbent advantage" elevator pitch

Our established infrastructure, trained staff, and deep community relationships mean zero transition time and uninterrupted service delivery. We're ready to build on this foundation with expanded capacity for the 2025-29 cycle."

- [ ] Identify areas where SNS can expand services
  - Title III-E Family Caregiver Support (current fit score: 85/100)
  - Title III-D Disease Prevention programs (current fit score: 65/100)
  - Enhanced geographic coverage
  - New evidence-based program implementation
  - CalAIM/ECM integration opportunities

- [ ] Prepare response to potential "why should we renew" question

**Key points to emphasize:**
1. Proven track record with measurable outcomes
2. Established community trust and recognition
3. No learning curve or onboarding time required
4. Continuity of care for existing clients
5. Existing infrastructure and operational capacity
6. Staff expertise and training already in place
7. Strong relationships with AAA program officers
8. Understanding of reporting requirements and compliance
