# TheGrantScout Database Assessment - Executive Summary

**Date:** November 30, 2025
**Analyst:** Research Team
**Project Phase:** Beta Wave 1 → Round 2 Planning

---

## Bottom Line Up Front

TheGrantScout Beta Wave 1 successfully delivered **18 reports to 3 clients** using only **25% of the available database**. The system works, but relies on manual research that won't scale. **Round 2 needs 100 hours of development** to automate matching and complete data import.

---

## Key Findings

### What Worked ✅
- **IRS BMF Enrichment:** 2.1M records enriched in 35 minutes (200-400x faster than APIs)
- **Data Quality:** Excellent (8.5/10) - IRS source data is authoritative
- **Beta Delivery:** 18 high-quality reports delivered on time
- **Database Design:** Well-structured, meets Build Plan requirements

### What's Missing 🔴
- **Data Completeness:** Only 16-22% imported (5 of 31 zip files)
- **Matching Automation:** 100% manual research process
- **Current Grants:** Only 42 records (should be 500-1000)
- **Schema Bloat:** 54% of database is wasted/redundant records

## Round 2 Priorities (100 Hours)

**IMPACT:** 500-1000 active opportunities

### 4. Database Cleanup (6 hours)
**WHY:** 54% of database is wasted space
**WHAT:** Delete redundant partitions, archive staging tables
**IMPACT:** 50% storage reduction, faster queries

### 5. Schema Improvements (8 hours)
**WHY:** Array inconsistencies, missing indexes
**WHAT:** Migrate TEXT to ARRAY, add FK constraints, create indexes
**IMPACT:** Better performance, data integrity

## Remaining Data Gaps (Cannot Fix)

**28% of pf_grants without EINs will remain unmatchable:**
- 50-60% are individuals (no EIN exists)
- 15-20% are foreign organizations (not in US IRS database)
- 10-15% are government entities (don't have EINs)
- 5-10% are inactive/dissolved
- 5-10% are data quality issues (typos, abbreviations)

**This is NORMAL and expected.** 72% coverage is excellent for F990 data.
