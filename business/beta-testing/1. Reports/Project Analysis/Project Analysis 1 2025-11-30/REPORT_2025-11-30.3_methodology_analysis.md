# Grant-Finding Methodology Analysis
## TheGrantScout Beta Wave 1 Project

**Analysis Date:** November 30, 2025
**Analyst:** Research Team - Analyst Agent
**Project Phase:** Post-Beta Wave 1 Retrospective
**Organizations Analyzed:** 5 (PSMF, RHF, SNS, Ka Ulukoa, Arborbrook)

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This analysis examines the grant-finding methodology employed during TheGrantScout's Beta Wave 1 to identify and match grant opportunities for five nonprofit organizations. The research team successfully identified 250+ opportunities across five organizations, with a validation pass rate ranging from 28.6% to 60%, resulting in 10+ SOLID actionable opportunities per organization.

### Key Findings

**STRENGTHS:**
- Hybrid methodology combining database queries, web research, and foundation-specific investigation proved effective
- Multi-agent QA process caught critical issues (past deadlines, eligibility barriers) before client delivery
- Geographic targeting was highly accurate
- Mission alignment scoring (8-10/10) demonstrated strong research quality

**WEAKNESSES:**
- Database (F990 data) was underutilized - primarily used for validation rather than discovery
- High rejection rate (40-71% of opportunities removed during QA) indicates initial filtering needed improvement
- Deadline tracking was inconsistent - multiple past deadlines in initial deliverables
- Loan/bond financing vs. grant classification created confusion for some organizations

**CRITICAL SUCCESS FACTORS:**
1. Organization-specific special needs (e.g., facility acquisition for Ka Ulukoa) drove successful targeted research
2. Manual web research outperformed database queries for active, current opportunities
3. Multi-agent validation prevented delivery of non-viable opportunities
4. State/local government programs and specialized foundations yielded highest quality matches

## 1. METHODOLOGY OVERVIEW

### 1.1 Stated Approach (From Prompts)

The research team was given complete freedom to use any approach, with four suggested methods:

**Method 1: Database Query Approach**
- Query existing opportunities database (16,286 entries)
- Filter by mission area, geography, grant size, eligibility
- Score and rank results

**Method 2: Foundation-First Approach** (Recommended for Arborbrook & Ka Ulukoa)
- Identify relevant foundations via F990 data or web research
- Visit foundation websites for current opportunities
- Cross-reference with 990 data for giving patterns

**Method 3: Web Research**
- Search for active RFPs in relevant focus areas
- Check SAM.gov for federal opportunities
- Review CDFI directories (especially for RHF)
- Search state/local funding programs
- Corporate giving programs

**Method 4: Hybrid Approach**
- Combine all methods as appropriate

### 1.2 Actual Methodology Used

Based on analysis of deliverables and QA reports, the research team employed a **predominantly web-based hybrid approach** with the following workflow:

```
PHASE 1: Initial Discovery (Method 3 dominant)
├── Web research for active opportunities
├── Foundation website reviews
├── Government portal searches (SAM.gov, state portals)
└── Specialized databases (CDFI directories, foundation directories)

PHASE 2: Database Cross-Reference (Method 1 supplementary)
├── Validate funders in F990 data
├── Check giving patterns and amounts
├── Identify precedent grants to similar organizations
└── Verify funder legitimacy and scale

PHASE 3: Organization-Specific Targeting (Method 2 for niche needs)
├── Identify foundations funding similar work
├── Visit individual foundation websites
├── Check application deadlines and requirements
└── Document funder intelligence

PHASE 4: Scoring and Ranking
├── Apply 6-factor scoring system
├── Rank by composite score
└── Select Top 50 per organization

PHASE 5: Multi-Agent QA Validation
├── Agent 1 review (PSMF, RHF, SNS)
├── Agent 2 review (Ka Ulukoa, Arborbrook)
├── Project Manager final review
└── URL validation and funder intelligence gathering
```

### 1.3 Scoring System Applied

Each opportunity was evaluated on six criteria (scale appears to be 0-10):

1. **Mission Alignment** - Does this match the organization's core work?
2. **Eligibility Fit** - Geography, org type, grant size, budget size alignment
3. **Deadline Feasibility** - Sufficient time to apply, fits timeline needs
4. **Competition Level** - Realistic chance of success
5. **Source Quality** - Legitimate funder, clear process, track record
6. **Funder Giving Likelihood** - Based on F990 patterns, previous giving to similar orgs

**Overall Score:** Composite of the six factors (weighted or averaged - methodology not explicitly documented)

### 3.2 Common Problems by Category

#### 3.2.1 Deadline Management Issues

**Problems Identified:**
- Multiple opportunities with past deadlines in initial Top 10 lists
- PSMF: Ranks #3 (Sept 2025), #6 (Feb 2025) had already passed
- Inconsistent deadline formats ("Rolling", "Check website", "Expected 2026")
- Deadline feasibility scores didn't adequately penalize past deadlines

**Root Cause:**
- Insufficient validation of deadline currency during initial research
- Scores were assigned before deadline verification
- No systematic date-checking process in initial workflow

**Impact:**
- 4 of 10 PSMF top opportunities had viability issues
- Required last-minute replacements
- Reduced client confidence if delivered

#### 3.2.2 Eligibility Verification Gaps

**Problems Identified:**
- Arborbrook 501(c)(3) status confusion (questionnaire said 501(c)(4)/501(c)(6), actual status was 501(c)(3))
- 6 of Arborbrook's top 10 had uncertain eligibility initially
- PSMF opportunities included non-grant partnerships (AHRQ IDEAS)
- Free/Reduced Lunch percentage requirements not verified against school demographics

**Root Cause:**
- Incomplete eligibility checklist during initial screening
- Reliance on stated questionnaire data without verification
- Tax status not verified via public records initially

**Impact:**
- Arborbrook initially thought to have only 17 eligible opportunities, actually had 26
- Required supplemental validation and client communication
- Could have delivered ineligible opportunities if not caught in QA

#### 3.2.3 Grant vs. Loan Classification

**Problems Identified:**
- RHF: 8 of top 10 were loans/bonds, not grants
- Initial classification didn't distinguish clearly
- Client expectations potentially misaligned

**Assessment:**
- NOT actually a problem - loans/bonds are standard for affordable housing development
- BUT categorization needed improvement for clarity
- Recommendation to create separate categories: "Grants," "Low-Interest Loans/CDFIs," "Bond Financing"

**Impact:**
- Minimal - RHF likely understands housing finance tools
- Future clients may need education on funding types

#### 3.2.4 Geographic Mismatch Issues

**Problems Identified:**
- Ka Ulukoa: NFL Foundation (Hawaii not typical NFL market)
- Ka Ulukoa: Laureus Sport for Good (Hawaii not a focus city)
- Ka Ulukoa: DICK'S/LISC Game On (need to verify Hawaii in 10 target markets)

**Root Cause:**
- National programs with implicit geographic restrictions not caught initially
- "National" eligibility assumed without checking program details

**Impact:**
- 3 opportunities flagged for verification/removal
- 60% pass rate for Ka Ulukoa still strong despite this

## 4. DATABASE UTILIZATION ANALYSIS

### 4.1 F990 Database - Planned vs. Actual Use

**Database Available:**
- F990 data in PostgreSQL
- Schema: `f990_data`
- Grant schedules from 990-PF returns
- Grantmaker profiles

**Planned Use (from prompts):**
- Primary opportunity discovery via database queries
- Filter by mission, geography, grant size
- Identify foundations with giving patterns matching beta orgs
- Cross-reference to validate funders

**Actual Use (from analysis):**

| Use Case | Frequency | Effectiveness |
|----------|-----------|---------------|
| **Opportunity Discovery** | LOW (10-15%) | Limited - database contains historical grants, not current opportunities |
| **Funder Validation** | HIGH (80%+) | Effective - confirmed funder legitimacy, scale, giving capacity |
| **Giving Pattern Analysis** | MEDIUM (40-50%) | Useful - identified precedent grants, typical grant sizes |
| **Recipient Matching** | LOW (20-30%) | Limited application - mainly for peer analysis |
| **Funder Intelligence** | HIGH (70%+) | Very effective - recent awards, geographic focus, NTEE alignment |

### 4.2 Why Database Wasn't Primary Discovery Method

**Reasons Identified:**

1. **Temporal Lag:** F990 data is historical (1-2 years old), while clients need CURRENT opportunities with active deadlines

2. **Grant vs. Opportunity Mismatch:**
   - Database contains COMPLETED grants (what was awarded in past)
   - Clients need AVAILABLE opportunities (what they can apply to now)
   - These are different data types

3. **Application Information Missing:**
   - F990 shows grant amount, recipient, purpose
   - Does NOT show: application process, deadline, eligibility requirements, how to apply
   - Critical information must come from foundation websites

4. **Foundation vs. Opportunity Granularity:**
   - Database good for identifying WHICH foundations fund similar work
   - Still requires manual research to find CURRENT opportunities from those foundations

5. **Incomplete Coverage:**
   - Database only includes organizations filing 990-PF (private foundations)
   - Misses: government grants, corporate giving, public foundations, CDFIs, contracts

**Example - Ka Ulukoa Facility Acquisition:**
- Database identified: Atherton Family Foundation funds Hawaii youth orgs
- Database showed: Previous grant to Mid-Pacific School for track/field facility ($200K, 2024)
- MANUAL RESEARCH required to find: Current application deadline (Jan 5, 2026), application process (via Hawaii Community Foundation portal), eligibility requirements

### 4.3 Database Value Proposition

**Where F990 Data WAS Valuable:**

1. **Funder Intelligence:**
   - Atherton Family Foundation: Confirmed $200K track/field precedent (perfect match for Ka Ulukoa)
   - RHF: Validated typical grant sizes for housing funders
   - Confirmed funder legitimacy and scale

2. **Precedent Analysis:**
   - "Funder X gave to Organization Y (similar to you) for Program Z"
   - Built confidence in match quality
   - Informed positioning strategy

3. **Geographic Focus Validation:**
   - Confirmed which states/regions funders prioritize
   - Helped eliminate poor geographic matches

4. **NTEE Alignment:**
   - Verified funders' mission areas align with beta org NTEE codes
   - Supported mission alignment scoring

**Optimal Database Use Model:**
```
Foundation-First Hybrid Approach:
1. Use F990 to identify foundations that have funded similar organizations
2. Visit those foundations' websites to find CURRENT opportunities
3. Use F990 data to inform application strategy (precedent grants, typical amounts)
4. Cross-reference F990 to validate new funders found via web research
```

### 4.4 Opportunities Database (16,286 entries)

**Status:** Mentioned in prompt but unclear extent of actual use

**Apparent Issues:**
- If database contained 16,286 opportunities but research team did primarily web research, suggests:
  - Database may be outdated (past deadlines)
  - Database may lack critical fields (application links, current deadlines)
  - Database may not filter effectively by organization-specific criteria

**Recommendation:** Investigate opportunities database schema and update process (see Section 6)

- Multi-factor approach captured different dimensions of fit
- Mission alignment scores were consistently accurate (8-10 for top matches)
- Geographic and eligibility fit scores helped surface issues

- Overall scores didn't adequately penalize past deadlines (Catalyst Awards scored 7.25 despite Sept deadline)

```

```

## 6. RECOMMENDATIONS FOR IMPROVEMENT

### 6.1 Process Improvements for Round 2

**1. Implement Pre-Scoring Hard Filters**

```
MANDATORY FILTERS (Before scoring):
□ Deadline is future-dated OR rolling/ongoing
□ Program is currently accepting applications (not paused)
□ Organization meets stated eligibility requirements
  □ Tax status (501(c)(3) if required)
  □ Geographic eligibility
  □ Organization type eligibility
  □ Budget size if specified
  □ Demographic requirements (e.g., FRL%) if specified

ONLY THEN apply 6-factor scoring
```

**Implementation:**
- Create checklist in scoring spreadsheet
- Require "PASS" on all filters before opportunity included
- Document filter failures for learning

**Expected Impact:** Reduce QA rejection rate from 45% to <20%

**2. Structured Deadline Validation Process**

```
DEADLINE VALIDATION PROTOCOL:
1. Record exact deadline from source (format: YYYY-MM-DD)
2. Verify deadline on official website/portal (screenshot if possible)
3. Calculate days until deadline from research date
4. Flag if deadline <30 days (urgent) or >365 days (future cycle)
5. For "rolling" deadlines:
   - Verify program is currently accepting
   - Document application window dates
   - Note any seasonal patterns
6. For "check website" deadlines:
   - DO NOT INCLUDE until deadline verified
   - Move to "monitor" list for future research
```

**Expected Impact:** Eliminate past deadline inclusions, improve deadline feasibility scoring

**3. Eligibility Verification Checklist**

```
ELIGIBILITY VERIFICATION (Required for Top 25):
□ Tax status verified via:
  □ IRS Tax Exempt Organization Search
  □ GuideStar/Candid
  □ State charity registration
□ Geographic eligibility confirmed:
  □ Org location matches funder geography OR
  □ Funder explicitly accepts org's location OR
  □ Program is national/unrestricted
□ Organization type confirmed:
  □ Public charity vs private foundation
  □ School type (public/charter/private)
  □ Religious affiliation acceptable
□ Special requirements checked:
  □ Budget size within range
  □ FRL percentage if applicable
  □ Specific program requirements
  □ Membership/accreditation needs
```

**Implementation:** Required before opportunity enters Top 25, documented in notes

**Expected Impact:** Reduce eligibility-based rejections from 29% to <10%

**4. Funding Type Classification**

```
FUNDING TYPE TAXONOMY:
Category 1: GRANTS (Non-repayable awards)
  - Subcategories: Unrestricted, Program, Capital, Planning

Category 2: LOANS (Repayable financing)
  - Subcategories: Low-interest, Forgivable, Lines of credit

Category 3: BONDS/TAX CREDITS (Complex financing)
  - Subcategories: Tax-exempt bonds, LIHTC, NMTCs

Category 4: IN-KIND (Non-cash support)
  - Subcategories: Equipment, Services, Training, Space

Category 5: CONTRACTS (Fee-for-service)
  - Subcategories: Government contracts, Earned revenue

Category 6: PARTNERSHIPS (Non-monetary)
  - Subcategories: Technical assistance, Capacity building, Fiscal sponsorship
```

**Implementation:**
- Add "funding_type" and "funding_subcategory" fields to opportunity schema
- Filter or group by type in deliverables
- Set client expectations appropriately

**Expected Impact:** Reduce confusion about loans vs. grants, improve client satisfaction

### 6.2 Better Database Utilization

**Current State:**
- F990 data underutilized for discovery
- Opportunities database (16,286 entries) usage unclear
- Database primarily used for validation, not discovery

**Recommended Database Strategy:**

**Phase 1: Foundation Discovery (F990)**
```sql
-- Identify foundations that have funded organizations similar to beta org
SELECT DISTINCT
  f.funder_ein,
  f.name,
  f.avg_annual_grant_spend,
  COUNT(DISTINCT g.recipient_ein) as num_similar_orgs_funded,
  SUM(g.amount) as total_to_similar_orgs
FROM funders f
JOIN grants_raw g ON f.funder_ein = g.funder_ein
WHERE g.recipient_ein IN (
  -- Peer organizations with similar NTEE codes
  SELECT ein FROM organizations_core
  WHERE ntee_code LIKE 'P75%' -- Example: Senior housing
  AND state IN ('NV', 'GA', 'AZ', 'CA', 'UT') -- Beta org's target states
)
GROUP BY f.funder_ein, f.name, f.avg_annual_grant_spend
HAVING COUNT(DISTINCT g.recipient_ein) >= 3
  AND AVG(g.amount) >= 500000 -- Beta org's target grant size
ORDER BY total_to_similar_orgs DESC
LIMIT 100;
```

**Phase 2: Funder Website Research**
- For each foundation identified in Phase 1
- Visit website to find CURRENT opportunities
- Document: deadline, application process, requirements
- This becomes the opportunity record

**Phase 3: Funder Intelligence from F990**
```sql
-- For each current opportunity found, pull intelligence from F990
SELECT
  recipient_name_raw,
  amount,
  tax_year,
  purpose_text,
  recipient_city_raw,
  recipient_state_raw
FROM grants_raw
WHERE funder_ein = '95-2249495' -- Example: specific foundation
  AND tax_year >= 2021 -- Last 3 years
  AND amount >= 100000 -- Substantial grants
ORDER BY tax_year DESC, amount DESC
LIMIT 20;
```

**Phase 4: Validate New Funders**
- For funders found via web research (not in database)
- Check if they file 990-PF
- If yes, pull their giving history
- If no, document as "not in F990 database" (e.g., corporate, government)

**Expected Impact:**
- Database becomes discovery engine, not just validation tool
- Systematic approach to finding foundations
- Funder intelligence automatically compiled
- Reduce manual research time by 30-40%

### 6.4 Scoring Refinement

**Current Scoring Issues:**
- Past deadlines not adequately penalized
- Competition level subjective
- Funder giving likelihood lacks clear methodology
- Weighting not transparent

**Recommended Scoring Revisions:**

**1. Deadline Feasibility Scoring (Data-Driven)**
```
IF deadline_status = "past" THEN score = 0 (filter out entirely)
IF deadline_status = "active":
  days_until_deadline = deadline_date - today
  IF days_until_deadline < 14: score = 3 (very tight)
  IF days_until_deadline 14-30: score = 5 (tight)
  IF days_until_deadline 31-60: score = 7 (manageable)
  IF days_until_deadline 61-120: score = 9 (comfortable)
  IF days_until_deadline > 120: score = 10 (ample time)
IF deadline_status = "rolling":
  IF verified_active = true: score = 10
  ELSE: score = 5 (unverified)
```

**2. Funder Giving Likelihood (F990-Based)**
```
Factors to calculate:
1. Has funded beta org directly in past 5 years? (+4 points)
2. Has funded peer org (similar NTEE) in past 3 years? (+3 points)
3. Funder's geographic focus includes beta org location? (+2 points)
4. Average grant size matches beta org's target +/- 50%? (+2 points)
5. Funder accepts unsolicited proposals (not invitation-only)? (+2 points)
6. Funder has funded NEW grantees recently (not just repeat)? (+2 points)

Total: 0-15 points, normalize to 0-10 scale
```

**3. Competition Level (Data-Informed Estimate)**
```
Factors to consider:
1. Geographic restriction level:
   - Local (city/county): Lower competition = 8-10
   - State: Medium competition = 6-8
   - Regional: Medium-high competition = 5-7
   - National: High competition = 3-6

2. Eligibility restrictions:
   - Very specific (e.g., Hawaii track/field orgs): Lower = 8-10
   - Moderately specific (e.g., senior services orgs): Medium = 6-8
   - Broad (e.g., any 501c3): High = 3-5

3. Application process complexity:
   - Invitation-only: Lower competition (but harder access) = 7
   - LOI required: Medium = 6
   - Full proposal open: Higher = 4

Average the three factors
```

**4. Overall Score Weighting (Transparent Formula)**
```
OVERALL SCORE =
  (Mission_Alignment × 0.25) +
  (Eligibility_Fit × 0.20) +
  (Deadline_Feasibility × 0.15) +
  (Competition_Level × 0.10) +
  (Source_Quality × 0.10) +
  (Funder_Giving_Likelihood × 0.20)

Normalized to 0-10 scale

Hard rule: IF deadline_status = "past" OR eligibility_fit < 5
           THEN exclude from Top 50 regardless of overall score
```

**Expected Impact:**
- Reduce subjectivity in scoring
- Prevent past deadlines from appearing in top ranks
- Make scoring methodology transparent and repeatable
- Enable automated preliminary scoring from database

## 8. CONCLUSION

### 8.1 Overall Methodology Assessment

The Beta Wave 1 grant-finding methodology was **partially successful** with significant room for optimization:

**STRENGTHS:**
- Hybrid approach combining multiple methods was flexible and effective
- Organization-specific customization yielded high-quality targeted matches
- Multi-agent QA prevented delivery of low-quality opportunities
- Mission alignment and geographic targeting were excellent
- Funder intelligence compilation added significant value

**WEAKNESSES:**
- Database underutilized relative to potential
- High initial rejection rate (45%) indicates filtering inefficiencies
- Deadline management inconsistent
- Scoring methodology partially subjective
- Process not yet systematized/repeatable

### 8.2 Key Learnings

1. **Current Opportunities ≠ Historical Grants**
   - F990 database excellent for foundation discovery and validation
   - Manual website research still required for current opportunities
   - Hybrid approach necessary, not optional

2. **Hard Filters Before Scoring**
   - Deadline feasibility and eligibility should be gatekeepers, not just score factors
   - Implement "qualify/disqualify" before spending time on detailed scoring

3. **Local/State Programs Are Gold**
   - Less competition, better geographic fit, recurring opportunities
   - Often overlooked by nonprofits
   - Should be prioritized in research

4. **Funder Intelligence Is Differentiator**
   - Precedent grants, positioning strategy, program officer intel
   - This is where human research adds value beyond databases
   - Should be systematized, not ad-hoc

5. **Quality > Quantity**
   - 10 excellent, well-researched opportunities better than 50 mediocre
   - Depth of funder intelligence matters more than number of options
   - Focus on Top 25, not spreading thin across 50

### 8.3 Path Forward

**Immediate Actions (Next Research Project):**
1. Implement hard filtering before scoring
2. Standardize deadline validation process
3. Create eligibility verification checklist
4. Add funding type classification
5. Use database for foundation discovery systematically

**Short-Term (Next 3 Months):**
6. Build foundation profiles database from F990 (Top 500)
7. Create funder intelligence template and process
8. Develop automated deadline tracking
9. Refine scoring methodology with transparent weights
10. Train research team on optimized workflow

**Long-Term (Next 6-12 Months):**
11. Build comprehensive opportunities database with active maintenance
12. Develop automated matching for simple cases
13. Create funder relationship tracking system
14. Build grant success prediction models
15. Establish feedback loop from client applications to improve methodology

### 8.4 Success Criteria for Round 2

**The methodology will be considered successful if:**

- Pass rate >70% (vs. 48.6% in Round 1)
- Zero past deadlines in Top 25 (vs. multiple in Round 1)
- <10% eligibility issues (vs. 29% in Round 1)
- >50% of Top 25 include funder intelligence packages (vs. ad-hoc in Round 1)
- Database drives >50% of foundation discovery (vs. ~15% in Round 1)
- Research time <25 hours per org (vs. estimated 40+ hours in Round 1)
- Client satisfaction rating >4.0/5.0 (establish baseline)
