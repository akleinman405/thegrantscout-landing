# Professional Grant & Business Report Design Best Practices
## Comprehensive Guide to Visual Aesthetics & Readability

*Research compiled: December 2025*
*Sources: McKinsey, BCG, consulting design standards, Gates Foundation, SaaS design best practices*

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Professional reports must balance visual appeal with clarity and readability. This guide synthesizes best practices from leading consulting firms (McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte), major foundations (Gates Foundation), and SaaS companies. Key principle: **Simplicity, consistency, and hierarchy drive comprehension.**

---

## 1. TYPOGRAPHY & FONT HIERARCHY

### 1.1 Font Selection & Pairing

#### Recommended Font Families

**Best Overall Pairings for Professional Reports:**
1. **Headings: Arial / Body: Georgia** - Creates professional contrast between modern headers and traditional body text
2. **Headings: Roboto / Body: Georgia** - Modern, digital-friendly combination with clean aesthetics
3. **All Calibri** - Single font solution with warm, approachable feel while remaining professional
4. **Headings: Arial / Body: Times New Roman** - Classic professional combination
5. **Headings: Helvetica / Body: Garamond** - Premium, established look

#### Why These Work

| Font | Best For | Characteristics | Use Case |
|------|----------|-----------------|----------|
| **Arial** | Headings | Clean, modern, versatile sans-serif | Contemporary reports, digitally viewed |
| **Calibri** | Body text | Warm, approachable, rounded edges | Emails, reports, friendly tone |
| **Georgia** | Body text | Serif with excellent screen readability | Longer passages, elegant feel |
| **Roboto** | Digital reports | Modern geometric sans-serif | SaaS reports, contemporary look |
| **Times New Roman** | Academic/legal | Classic serif, trustworthy | Traditional documents, books |

#### Font Selection Rules

- **Stick to 2-3 font families maximum** across entire document
- Use **serif for body text** in printed reports (Georgia, Times New Roman, Garamond)
- Use **sans-serif for body text** in digital reports (Calibri, Arial, Roboto)
- Use **sans-serif for headings** regardless of medium (Arial, Helvetica, Roboto)
- Avoid script typefaces, decorative fonts, or more than 3 fonts total
- Fonts used by McKinsey: **Arial for body + Georgia/custom Bower font for titles**
- Fonts used by BCG: **Trebuchet MS exclusively**

#### Font Psychology

- **Sans-serif fonts** (Arial, Calibri) = Perceived as more competent and modern
- **Serif fonts** (Georgia, Times New Roman) = Perceived as more trustworthy and traditional
- **Warm fonts** (Calibri) = Feel friendlier and less formal
- **Clean fonts** (Arial, Helvetica) = Feel more corporate and decisive

### 1.2 Font Sizes

#### Recommended Type Sizes (in Points)

| Element | Size | Weight | Notes |
|---------|------|--------|-------|
| **Document Title/Cover** | 24-28 pt | Bold | Center-aligned, all caps optional |
| **H1 Headings** | 18-20 pt | Bold | Major section breaks |
| **H2 Subheadings** | 14-16 pt | Bold or Semi-bold | Subsection titles |
| **H3 Subheadings** | 12-13 pt | Bold | Tertiary divisions |
| **Body Text** | 10-12 pt | Regular | 11-12 pt recommended for easy reading |
| **Captions** | 8-9 pt | Regular or Italic | Figure/table captions |
| **Footnotes/References** | 8-10 pt | Regular | Smaller but still readable |
| **Table Headers** | 10-11 pt | Bold | Same as body or 1 pt larger |
| **Footer/Page Number** | 10 pt | Regular | Light color, subtle |

#### Font Size Rules

- **Minimum body text: 10pt** for printed reports, 11-12pt preferred
- **Maximum heading: 4 points larger than body** unless it's the document title
- **Subheadings should be at most 1 point larger than body** with bold/italic differentiation
- **Keep heading size within 14-16pt range** for most reports (except titles)
- Ensure sufficient contrast between levels for visual hierarchy

---

### 1.3 Line Spacing & Letter Spacing

#### Line Spacing (Vertical Space Between Lines)

| Context | Line Spacing | Use Case |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Body Text - Formal Reports** | 1.5 lines | Standard for professional documents |
| **Body Text - Readable** | 1.5 - 2.0 lines | Optimal for accessibility |
| **Dense Technical Content** | 1.15 - 1.5 lines | Acceptable for data-heavy sections |
| **Headings** | 1.0 - 1.15 lines | Can be tighter than body |
| **Bulleted Lists** | 1.25 - 1.5 lines | Better than single-space for scanning |

#### Line Spacing Best Practices

- **Default to 1.5x line height** for body text in printed reports
- Use "At Least" setting at **125-130% of point size** (e.g., 15-16pt leading for 12pt text)
- Can decrease line spacing between headings and text or within bulleted lists
- **Character line length: 60-70 characters** (approx 6.5" width at standard margins)
- More generous spacing between paragraphs (12-18pt) than between lines

#### Letter Spacing (Horizontal Space Between Characters)

- **Body text**: No adjustment (0 pt tracking)
- **Headings**: Slightly increased spacing (0.5-1.5 pt) for emphasis
- **All caps headings**: Increase letter spacing by 1-2pt for readability
- **Never compress** text to fit; adjust margins instead

---

### 1.4 Text Formatting & Emphasis

#### When to Use Bold, Italic, Color

| Format | Use For | Avoid |
|--------|---------|-------|
| **Bold** | Headings, key terms, important numbers | Avoid underlining (distraction) |
| **Italic** | Emphasis, variable names, foreign words | Extended passages (hard to read) |
| **SMALL CAPS** | Abbreviations, organization names | General emphasis (use bold instead) |
| **Color** | Strategic accent words, data insights | Overuse (max 1-2 accent colors) |
| **Underline** | Hyperlinks only | Never for emphasis in body text |

#### Best Practices for Emphasis

1. **Use bold for emphasis**, not underline
2. **Limit colored text** to strategic data points or call-outs
3. **Never bold and italicize** the same text
4. **Use consistent formatting** for similar elements (all section headings formatted identically)
5. **Avoid ALL CAPS** for body text (reduces readability by 5-10%)
6. **Use color strategically**: Red for urgent/negative, Green for success, Blue for neutral/trustworthy

## 2. TABLE DESIGN & DATA PRESENTATION

### 2.1 Header Row Styling

#### Header Styling Specifications

| Property | Recommendation | Hex Code | Notes |
|----------|-----------------|----------|-------|
| **Background Color** | Solid header color | #112E51 (Navy) or #0095A8 (Teal) | Use brand color, ensure contrast |
| **Text Color** | White or light text | #FFFFFF | Maintain 4.5:1 contrast ratio minimum |
| **Font Weight** | Bold | - | Always bold headers |
| **Text Size** | Same as body or 1pt larger | 10-11pt | Match or slightly larger than body |
| **Border Weight** | Slightly thicker stroke | 2-3pt | Distinguish header from body rows |
| **Padding** | Generous | 12-15px | More space in headers than body |
| **Text Alignment** | Left-aligned (default) | - | Right-align numbers only |
| **Font Style** | Regular (no italic) | - | Keep headers clean |

#### Header Design Rules

- Headers must **visually contrast** with body rows
- Use **darker background + white text** for clear separation
- Apply **thicker bottom border** (2-3pt) to distinguish from data rows
- **Fix headers to top** when document scrolls (if digital)
- Include **descriptive column labels** (no abbreviations if possible)
- Match text alignment with column content (left for text, right for numbers)

---

### 2.2 Alternating Row Colors (Zebra Striping)

#### Zebra Striping Guidelines

**When to Use:**
- Tables with **10+ rows** of data
- **Wide tables** (harder for eyes to track)
- **Data-heavy reports** requiring sustained reading
- Financial tables, survey results, comparison matrices

**When to Avoid:**
- Tables with **fewer than 8 rows** (unnecessary)
- Very simple lookup tables
- If alternating pattern creates confusion with other striping

#### Color Recommendations for Alternating Rows

| Row Type | Background Color | Text Color | Notes |
|----------|------------------|-----------|-------|
| **Odd Rows** | White or very light | #333333 (dark gray) | #FFFFFF or #F8F8F8 |
| **Even Rows** | Light gray | #333333 (dark gray) | #F0F0F0 or #E8E8E8 (5-10% gray) |
| **First Row** | Darker than even rows | White | Create contrast with header |

**Best Practice Color Values:**
- Odd rows: #FFFFFF (white)
- Even rows: #F5F5F5 or #F0F0F0 (light gray, 5-7% opacity)
- Avoid medium grays (too much contrast = stripe effect too obvious)

#### Zebra Striping Rules

1. Use **soft, light gray** backgrounds (not bold stripes)
2. **First row should contrast** with header color
3. Keep text readable on both light and striped rows
4. Use same stripe pattern consistently throughout document
5. **Minimum contrast** 3:1 between alternating rows
6. For accessibility, stripe pattern should be clear but not jarring

---

### 2.3 Cell Padding & Spacing

#### Cell Padding (Space Inside Cells)

| Element | Padding | Format | Notes |
|---------|---------|--------|-------|
| **Header Cells** | 12-15 px | Top/Right/Bottom/Left | More generous than data cells |
| **Data Cells** | 8-12 px | Top/Right/Bottom/Left | Minimum 8px recommended |
| **Dense Numerical Tables** | 10 px | All sides | Balance density with readability |
| **Spacious Data Tables** | 15 px | All sides | Comfortable reading of large datasets |

#### Border Spacing (Space Between Cells)

| Style | Spacing | CSS Property | Best For |
|-------|---------|--------------|----------|
| **Minimal Lines** | 2-3 px | border-spacing: 2px | Modern, clean look |
| **Collapsed** | 0 px | border-collapse: collapse | Professional, tight appearance |
| **Relaxed** | 4-6 px | border-spacing: 5px | Large tables, readability |

#### Padding & Spacing Rules

1. **Minimum padding: 8px** on all sides of cells
2. **Standard padding: 10-12px** (good balance)
3. **Generous padding: 15px** for executive tables with sparse data
4. **Row height**: Use "relaxed" (more white space) for large datasets
5. **Never compress** to fit more data; increase margins instead
6. **Border spacing: Keep at 2-3px** for collapsed look, or use CSS `border-collapse: collapse`

---

### 2.4 Border Styles

#### Border Style Options

| Style | Description | Best For | Spacing |
|-------|-------------|----------|---------|
| **Full Borders** | All cells have visible borders | Dense data, comparison tables | 1-2 pt |
| **Minimal (Grid)** | Light horizontal lines only | Modern reports, dashboards | 1 pt |
| **No Borders** | Clean, lines via alternating colors | Executive summaries | Rely on row colors |
| **Left Border Only** | Header row has left border | Data-heavy tables | 2-3 pt |
| **Top/Bottom Only** | Only top and bottom borders | Academic, traditional style | 2 pt |

#### Recommended Border Approach for Professional Reports

**Best Practice: Minimal Grid Style**
- Horizontal lines only (between rows and header)
- Very light gray color (#E8E8E8 or #CCCCCC)
- 1pt weight
- Paired with alternating row colors (optional)

**Alternative: Collapsed Borders**
- Uses alternating row colors for definition
- No visible borders (or very subtle)
- Relies on padding and spacing
- Modern, clean aesthetic

#### Border Specifications

- **Header border weight**: 2-3pt (bottom only)
- **Row border weight**: 0.5-1pt or none
- **Border color**: Light gray (#CCCCCC to #E8E8E8)
- **Never use**: Thick colored borders, decorative patterns

---

### 2.5 Best Practices for Data-Heavy Tables

1. **Text Alignment**:
   - Left-align text and labels
   - Right-align all numbers (decimals aligned)
   - Center-align categorical indicators

2. **Column Width**:
   - Apply width to header cells (`<th>`) to control column width
   - Avoid columns wider than 3 inches
   - Allow extra width for numerical data

3. **Readability Enhancements**:
   - Keep minimum row height at 24-28px
   - Use zebra striping for tables with 10+ rows
   - Left-align stub column (first column)
   - Use captions for all data tables

4. **Semantic Structure** (for digital reports):
   - Use `<caption>`, `<thead>`, `<tbody>`, `<tfoot>` elements
   - Use `<th>` for all headers
   - Use `<td>` for data cells only

5. **Handling Large Tables**:
   - Break into multiple smaller tables if >20 rows
   - Use page breaks in printed reports
   - Freeze header rows in digital tables
   - Consider rotating to landscape for very wide tables

## 3. COLOR USAGE IN PROFESSIONAL REPORTS

### 3.1 Color Palette Strategy

#### Optimal Color Palette Size

- **Minimum**: 2 colors (primary + neutral)
- **Standard**: 3-4 colors (primary, secondary, accent, neutral)
- **Maximum**: 5-6 colors before appearing cluttered
- **Avoid**: "Christmas tree effect" (too many colors = distraction)

#### Color Family Organization

Use colors from **same family** to show relationships:
- **Cool family**: Blues, teals, purples (trust, stability, analysis)
- **Warm family**: Reds, oranges, yellows (action, urgency, energy)
- **Neutral family**: Grays, blacks, whites (background, text)

**Never mix** warm and cool families without clear purpose (e.g., red and green for pass/fail).

---

### 3.2 Semantic Color Usage

#### Meaning-Based Color Assignments

| Status/Meaning | Color | Hex Code | Use For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Positive/Success** | Green | #27AE60 or #22C55E | Growth, completion, approved |
| **Negative/Decline** | Red | #E74C3C or #EF4444 | Decrease, warning, decline |
| **Neutral/Caution** | Orange | #E67E22 or #F59E0B | Warning, attention needed |
| **Informational** | Blue | #2980B9 or #0EA5E9 | Insights, data, neutral info |
| **Secondary** | Purple | #8E44AD or #A855F7 | Alternative metric, comparison |

#### Rules for Semantic Colors

1. **Consistency is critical**: Use same color for same meaning throughout report
2. **Accessibility required**: Ensure not relying ONLY on color for meaning (add text/icons)
3. **Avoid red/green only**: 8% of men have red/green color blindness
4. **Test for accessibility**: Use tools like ColorBrewer or Viz Palette
5. **Business rule alignment**: Follow company standards if they exist

### 3.4 Accent Color Strategy

#### When to Use Color vs. Grayscale

| Content Type | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| **Executive Summary** | Use 1 accent color for key metrics |
| **Data Visualizations** | Use palette of 3-4 colors for comparison |
| **Status Indicators** | Use semantic colors (red/yellow/green) |
| **Body Text** | Use black/dark gray, NO color |
| **Call-outs** | Use 1-2 accent colors |
| **Headings** | Use primary brand color or black |

#### Accent Color Rules

1. **Use color strategically**, not decoratively
2. **Limit to 1-2 accent colors** in body text (reserve third for data viz)
3. **Warm colors** (red, orange) for urgency → Use sparingly
4. **Cool colors** (blue, green) for routine data → Safe to use regularly
5. **Never use color alone** to convey meaning → Add text labels
6. **Contrast requirement**: 4.5:1 minimum contrast between text and background

---

### 3.5 Background Colors & Highlighting

#### Callout Box Backgrounds

| Intensity | Color | Use | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Subtle** | 5-10% gray (#F5F5F5 to #EEEEEE) | Information callouts | Notes, tips |
| **Moderate** | 10-15% tint (#E8E8E8) | Important info | Key findings |
| **Accent** | 20-30% tint of brand color | Emphasis | Critical insights |
| **Bold** | 50%+ tint | Warnings | Action items |

#### Highlight Color Guidelines

- **Yellowish highlight**: #FFFACD or #FFFFE0 (soft yellow)
- **Pink highlight**: #FFE4E1 or #FFF0F5 (soft pink)
- **Blue highlight**: #E3F2FD or #F0F8FF (sky blue)
- **Green highlight**: #E8F5E9 or #F1F8E9 (pale green)

**Rule**: Highlights should be **transparent (10-20% opacity)**, not solid colors.

## 4. CALLOUT BOXES & HIGHLIGHTS

### 4.1 Callout Box Design Patterns

#### Left Border Design Pattern (Most Professional)

**Specifications:**
- **Left border width**: 4-6pt (thick, visible)
- **Left border color**: Brand color or semantic color
- **Background**: Light tint of border color (5-15% opacity)
- **Padding**: 12-15px on all sides
- **Border radius**: 0-2px (sharp corners more professional)

**Example for "Important" callout:**
- Border-left: 5pt solid #E74C3C (red)
- Background: #FDE8E6 (light red, ~10%)
- Text color: #333333

#### Full Border Design Pattern

**Specifications:**
- **Border weight**: 1-2pt on all sides
- **Border color**: Match left-border or accent color
- **Background**: Light tint, 5-10%
- **Padding**: 15px all sides
- **Border radius**: 2-4px optional

**Less professional** than left-border; use for less critical information.

#### No Border Design Pattern

**Specifications:**
- **Border**: None
- **Background**: Light color or tint (10-15%)
- **Padding**: 12-15px
- **Top/bottom shadow**: Optional (subtle 1-2px shadow)

**Best for**: Subtle callouts that shouldn't interrupt flow.

---

### 4.2 Callout Box Types & Color Coding

| Type | Border Color | Background | Icon | Use Case |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Note** | #0095A8 (Teal) | #E0F7FA | (i) icon | Additional information |
| **Tip** | #27AE60 (Green) | #E8F5E9 | Lightbulb | Helpful hint |
| **Warning** | #E67E22 (Orange) | #FFF3E0 | ! icon | Attention needed |
| **Critical** | #E74C3C (Red) | #FADBD8 | X icon | Action required |
| **Success** | #27AE60 (Green) | #EAFAF1 | Checkmark | Completed item |

---

### 4.3 Icon Usage in Callouts

#### When to Use Icons

- **With callouts** containing important information
- **Not required** for simple text callouts
- **Always use consistently** (same icon type throughout report)

#### Icon Best Practices

| Element | Approach | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Icon size** | 16-24px | Matches font size or slightly larger |
| **Icon color** | Match border color | Teal icon for teal-bordered callout |
| **Icon position** | Left side, before text | Vertical center alignment |
| **Icon style** | Simple outline (not filled) | Line icons, not solid |
| **Icons to use** | Standard symbols | (i), !, lightbulb, checkmark, X |

#### Icons to Avoid

- Decorative/cute graphics
- Thick/heavy icons (appear clunky)
- Filled icons (can look blocky in small sizes)
- Custom illustrations (inconsistent with professional tone)

---

### 4.4 Positioning Within Document Flow

#### Placement Guidelines

- **Use after paragraph** explaining concept (not before)
- **Allow space above and below** (12-18pt spacing)
- **Don't nest** callouts within other callouts
- **Don't overlap** with images or tables
- **Page positioning**: Avoid placing at page break if possible

#### Text Wrapping

- **Text should wrap around callout** in some layouts (optional)
- **Better practice**: Full-width callout with text flowing below
- **Minimum width**: 3 inches (readable)
- **Maximum width**: Document width (don't exceed margins)

## 5. WHITE SPACE & MARGINS

### 5.1 Page Margins

#### Standard Margin Specifications

| Document Type | Top | Bottom | Left | Right | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Standard Report** | 1" | 1" | 1" | 1" | Default for most documents |
| **Printed w/Binding** | 1" | 1" | 1.5" | 1" | Extra space on left for binding |
| **Two-Sided Printing** | 1" | 1" | 1.25" | 1.25" | Inside margins = outer margins |
| **Digital Only** | 0.75" | 0.75" | 0.75" | 0.75" | Can be narrower |
| **Executive Summary** | 1.25" | 1.25" | 1.25" | 1.25" | More generous margins |

#### Margin Selection Rules

1. **Default to 1" all around** for most professional reports
2. **Increase left margin to 1.5"** if document will be bound/stapled
3. **Consider two-sided printing**: Mirror margins help balance
4. **For digital**: Can reduce to 0.75" but maintain balance
5. **Minimum margins**: 0.5" (printer limitation is 0.25")
6. **Line length consideration**: 1.5-2" side margins with 12pt font = comfortable 60-70 character line

---

### 5.2 Section Spacing

#### Space Between Sections

| Element | Above | Below | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **H1 Heading** | 18-24pt | 12-18pt | Major section break |
| **H2 Heading** | 12-18pt | 9-12pt | Subsection |
| **H3 Heading** | 9-12pt | 6-9pt | Minor subsection |
| **Paragraph Break** | 0pt | 12pt | Space after, not before |
| **Section Break** | 24pt | 24pt | Before and after |

#### Spacing Rules

- **Never have two headings** without text between
- **Space below** headings (not above) for cleaner appearance
- **Increase space** before new sections (24pt minimum)
- **Consistent spacing** throughout entire document
- **Use "space after"** in paragraph formatting (not manual line breaks)

---

### 5.3 Paragraph Spacing

#### Line and Paragraph Spacing

| Context | Line Spacing | Space After | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Body Text** | 1.5x | 12pt | Standard professional |
| **Body Text Dense** | 1.15x | 12pt | For data-heavy sections |
| **Bulleted Lists** | 1.25x | 6pt | Tighter within lists |
| **Lists Between Paragraphs** | 1.5x | 12pt | Match body spacing |
| **Quotes** | 1.5x | 12pt | Indented, same as body |

#### Paragraph Best Practices

1. **Keep paragraphs short** (3-5 sentences max)
2. **Vary length** to prevent monotonous rhythm
3. **Use 1.5x line spacing** as default (not single-space)
4. **Space after paragraphs: 12pt** (don't double-space)
5. **First line indent: 0.5"** OR space after paragraph (not both)
6. **Justify text**: Left-aligned preferred (not justified/full justify)

---

### 5.4 Table Spacing

#### Space Around Tables

| Position | Space | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Before Table** | 12-18pt | Create visual break |
| **After Table** | 18-24pt | More space below |
| **Caption (Below)** | 6pt | Tight spacing to table |
| **Text After Table** | 12pt | Resume normal spacing |

#### Table Internal Spacing

- **Header padding**: 12-15px
- **Cell padding**: 10-12px
- **Row height**: 24-28px minimum
- **Alternating row color**: 5-10% gray
- **Border weight**: 0.5-1pt (light gray)

---

### 5.5 White Space to Text Ratio

#### Optimal Balance

**Best Practice: 1 part white space to 3 parts content**

Calculation:
- If your page is 8.5" x 11" with 1" margins = 6.5" x 9" content area
- Optimal: 6.5" x 3" white space, 6.5" x 6" actual content
- This creates ~33% white space, 67% content

#### White Space Benefits

1. **Improves readability** by 5-10%
2. **Makes documents feel premium** (more expensive looking)
3. **Guides reader's eye** to important content
4. **Reduces cognitive load** (less overwhelming)
5. **Improves retention** of key information

#### White Space Placement

- **Margins**: 1" standard
- **Between sections**: 18-24pt
- **Between paragraphs**: 12pt
- **Left/right gutters**: 0.5-1"
- **Top/bottom gutters**: 0.5-1"

## 6. HEADERS & FOOTERS

### 6.1 Header Design

#### Header Content Options

| Element | Position | Size | Color | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Logo** | Left or centered | 0.5-0.75" height | Brand color | Every page or first page only |
| **Title/Section Name** | Right | 10-11pt | Dark gray (#333333) | Every page |
| **Date** | Right | 9pt | Gray (#666666) | Every page optional |
| **Company Name** | Center | 10pt | Dark gray | Every page optional |

#### Logo Placement Best Practices

- **Top-left** (most common, establishes brand)
- **Top-center** (formal, centered design)
- **Top-right** (less common, works for single-column reports)
- **Height**: 0.5-0.75 inches (don't overshadow content)
- **Padding**: 0.5" from top and side

#### Header Styling

- **Font**: Match body font (10-11pt)
- **Color**: Dark gray (#333333) or brand color, not black
- **Weight**: Regular (not bold unless emphasizing)
- **Border**: Optional light line (0.5-1pt gray) below header

---

### 6.2 Footer Design

#### Footer Content Options

| Element | Position | Size | Color | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Page Number** | Center or right | 10pt | Gray (#666666) | Every page |
| **Document Title** | Left | 9pt | Gray (#666666) | Every page optional |
| **Date Last Updated** | Right | 9pt | Gray (#666666) | Every page optional |
| **Company/Contact Info** | Center | 8-9pt | Gray | Every page optional |
| **Confidentiality Notice** | Center | 8pt | Gray | First page only or optional |

#### Page Number Formats

- **Simple**: "5" (right-aligned)
- **With context**: "Page 5 of 15" (right-aligned)
- **With title**: "5 | Report Title" (right-aligned)
- **Document reference**: "2025-11 Report | Page 5" (both sides)

#### Footer Styling

- **Font size**: 9-10pt (smaller than body)
- **Color**: Gray (#666666 or #808080), not black
- **Weight**: Regular (match body text)
- **Border**: Optional light line (0.5pt gray) above footer
- **Alignment**: Right for page numbers, centered for copyright

---

### 6.3 Advanced Header/Footer Features

#### Different First Page
- **Use for**: Title pages, executive summaries
- **Implementation**: Omit header/footer on cover page
- **Benefits**: Cleaner appearance for cover

#### Odd/Even Pages (Two-Sided Printing)
- **Odd pages**: Logo on left, title on right
- **Even pages**: Title on left, page number on right
- **Benefits**: Professional book-like appearance

#### Section Headers
- **Dynamic headers**: Show current section in header
- **Implementation**: Update header when section changes
- **Benefits**: Helps readers navigate long documents

---

### 6.4 Header/Footer Best Practices

1. **Keep headers and footers simple** (not cluttered)
2. **Use consistent styling** throughout document
3. **Logo should not overshadow** page content
4. **Use subtle colors** (gray, not bold colors)
5. **Leave 0.5" space** between footer and page bottom
6. **Avoid overlapping** header/footer with content
7. **Different first page** if using title page

## 8. DESIGN PRINCIPLES FROM LEADING FIRMS

### McKinsey Design Approach

**Key Principles:**
1. **Clarity is the currency** - Every design choice must serve comprehension
2. **Data-first design** - Graphics and layout highlight insights, not decorate
3. **Minimal design philosophy** - Remove any element that doesn't add value
4. **MECE principle** - Ideas organized as Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive
5. **Action titles** - Every section has clear, sentence-format headline conveying main message

**McKinsey Font Stack:**
- Headings: Custom Bower font (unavailable) → Use Arial or Helvetica
- Body: Arial (more recent) or Georgia (older templates)

**McKinsey Color Palette:**
- Navy (#003366) for headers and primary elements
- White background
- Minimal color use (1-2 accent colors max)

### BCG Design Approach

**Key Characteristics:**
1. **Single font discipline** - Trebuchet MS exclusively
2. **Clean grid structure** - Consistent alignment across all documents
3. **High-contrast design** - Dark backgrounds with white text for key sections
4. **Data visualization focus** - Charts and graphs take visual priority

**BCG Font Stack:**
- Exclusive: Trebuchet MS for all text

**BCG Color Usage:**
- Dark backgrounds for emphasis
- Strategic color for data visualization only

### General Consulting Standards (McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte)

**Universal Principles:**
1. Keep color palette simple (3-5 colors)
2. Use sans-serif fonts (Arial, Helvetica, Trebuchet)
3. Bold action titles on every section
4. Top-down approach (key messages first)
5. Uniform formatting across all slides/sections
6. Focus on clarity over decoration
7. Generous white space around content
8. Data-driven visuals, not decorative graphics

## 9. COMMON DESIGN MISTAKES TO AVOID

| Mistake | Why It's Bad | Solution |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Too many fonts** (>3) | Looks unprofessional, hard to read | Limit to 2-3 complementary fonts |
| **Small text** (<10pt) | Readers strain to read, reduce comprehension | Use 11-12pt minimum for body |
| **Thin line spacing** (single) | Text appears cramped, reduces readability | Use 1.5x line spacing |
| **Rainbow colors** (6+) | Distracting, "Christmas tree effect" | Limit palette to 3-5 colors |
| **Text underline for emphasis** | Reduces readability, looks old-fashioned | Use bold instead |
| **Justified text alignment** | Creates awkward spacing, harder to read | Use left-aligned text |
| **Narrow margins** (<0.75") | Looks cramped, hard to bind | Use 1" standard margins |
| **No alternating row colors** | Eyes struggle with data tables | Add zebra striping for 10+ rows |
| **Text in tables too small** | Tables become unreadable | Minimum 10pt, prefer 11pt |
| **Cluttered headers/footers** | Distracts from content | Keep simple, use gray colors |
| **No white space** | Overwhelming to read | Aim for 30-35% white space |
| **Overuse of bold/italic** | Loses emphasis effect, looks cluttered | Use sparingly and consistently |
| **Wrong font pairing** | Can appear unprofessional | Match serif+sans-serif deliberately |
| **Inconsistent formatting** | Looks sloppy, confuses readers | Use styles, not manual formatting |
