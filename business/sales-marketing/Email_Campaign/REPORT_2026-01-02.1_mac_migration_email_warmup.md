## Issue #3: Rolling 24h Limit Preventing Full Sends

### Problem
The system uses a **rolling 24-hour window**, not a calendar-day reset. If you sent 200 emails at 5pm yesterday, those don't "free up" until 5pm today.

### Current Behavior
- Send 150 emails at 3pm Monday
- Tuesday 9am: Only 250 capacity (150 still "in use" until 3pm)
- Tuesday 3pm: Full 400 capacity restored

### User's Desired Behavior
- Send at a consistent rate during all business hours
- Always use full daily capacity

### Solution Options

**Option A: Calendar-Day Reset (Simpler)**
- Reset counter at midnight EST each day
- 400 fresh emails every business day
- Risk: Could hit Gmail's actual rate limits if you send 400 at 5pm, then 400 at 9am (800 in 16 hours)

**Option B: True Rolling Window with Smart Pacing (Recommended)**
- Keep rolling 24h window for Gmail compliance
- Add "catch-up" logic: If capacity is available, send faster to use full 400/day
- Spread sends evenly across 10-hour business day (40/hour)

**Option C: Steady-State Pacing**
- Send exactly 40 emails/hour during business hours
- 10 hours × 40 = 400/day
- Never exceeds rolling 24h limit
- Most conservative, most consistent

## Email Warmup Plan

### Strategy: Subdomain Addressing

Instead of creating multiple accounts, use **subdomain addressing** (also called plus addressing or dot variations) to route all emails through a single mailbox while tracking different "personas":

**Option 1: Plus Addressing (Gmail native)**
- `outreach+1@thegrantscout.com`
- `outreach+2@thegrantscout.com`
- All deliver to `outreach@thegrantscout.com`
- ⚠️ Many spam filters recognize this as the same sender

**Option 2: Subdomain Strategy (Better for reputation)**
Create actual subdomains for sending:
- `outreach@mail1.thegrantscout.com`
- `outreach@mail2.thegrantscout.com`
- `outreach@mail3.thegrantscout.com`

**Benefits:**
- Each subdomain builds its own reputation
- If one gets flagged, others continue
- Main domain `thegrantscout.com` stays clean
- Can warm up in parallel

    capacity_remaining = get_total_capacity()
    return capacity_remaining / max(hours_remaining, 1)
