## Solution: Use Mission/Sector for Deeper Personalization

**Your suggestion is exactly right:**
> "Help finding grant opportunities for nonprofits who _____"

We CAN do this because the database has mission data:

| Prospect | Mission (from DB) | Subject Line |
|----------|-------------------|--------------|
| Erika's Lighthouse | "educating about adolescent depression" | "Grant opps for nonprofits fighting teen depression" |
| VISTE | "enable elderly to live independently" | "Grant opps for nonprofits serving seniors" |
| ACLU of KY | "defend individual rights and liberties" | "Grant opps for civil liberties orgs" |

### Implementation Approach

1. **Join email prospects to database by website**
2. **Extract key phrase from mission** (first 5-10 words or sector)
3. **Generate personalized subject:** "Grant opportunities for nonprofits who [mission phrase]"

**Sample Query:**
```sql
SELECT
    p.org_name,
    p.mission_statement,
    p.sector_desc,
    LEFT(p.mission_statement, 50) as mission_snippet
FROM f990_2025.prospects p
WHERE LOWER(p.website) LIKE '%' || LOWER([email_prospect_domain]) || '%';
```
