# ⭐ 1. State Capacity-Building Grant Programs  
Many states offer **small, low-barrier capacity-building grants ($2k–$50k)** that nonprofits can use for:

- Technology tools  
- Grant discovery software  
- Training  
- Strategic planning  
- Fundraising systems  
- Organizational development  

### Examples  
- California: CalNonprofits Capacity Grants  
- Texas: OneStar Foundation programs  
- New York: Nonprofit Infrastructure Capital Investment Program  
- Minnesota: Nonprofit Recovery Fund  
- Colorado: ARPA capacity-building grants  
- Massachusetts: Nonprofit Security & Capacity Grants  

These programs often **reimburse or directly pay for software tools** like TheGrantScout.

# ⭐ 2. Federal Pass-Through Capacity-Building Funds  
Federal agencies rarely directly fund nonprofits for capacity-building…  
BUT they send billions to states/localities who distribute it through:

- ARPA  
- CARES  
- HUD CDBG  
- CSBG (Community Services Block Grants)  
- SAMHSA capacity funds  
- HHS rural & community development funds  

These funds can cover:  
- software  
- data tools  
- grant-support systems  
- fundraising infrastructure  

**Meaning your product is eligible in many states without special approval.**

# ⭐ 3. SBA-Related Programs (Indirect but Real Benefits)

- SBA Small Business Development Center (SBDC) networks  
- SCORE nonprofit partner networks  

These organizations can **use SBA funds to pay for fundraising or grant-readiness tools**, indirectly paying you.

# ⭐ 10. Programs That Can Pay *You Directly* for Each Nonprofit Served

**“Is there a program that would fund me directly for each nonprofit I serve (so it’s free to the nonprofit)?”**

States maintain rosters of approved “Capacity-Building Providers.”

When a nonprofit requests support, **the state pays YOU**, not the nonprofit.

This is the closest match to “per nonprofit served = you get paid.”

### ✔ D. AmeriCorps VISTA Support Contracts  
Commissions contract vendors for capacity-building support.  
Paid per deliverable or per nonprofit.

### ✔ E. ARPA/CARES Local Nonprofit Support Initiatives  
Cities and counties have used federal funds to:

- pay TA providers directly  
- offer free capacity-building services to nonprofits
