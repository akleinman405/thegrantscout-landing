# State TA Provider Pools Research Report
## Get Paid by States/Foundations to Serve Nonprofits

**Date:** 2025-12-10
**Prepared for:** TheGrantScout Launch Strategy

---

## Executive Summary

This research identified **21 programs** across 15 states plus national platforms where capacity-building consultants can potentially get approved as vendors and receive payment from states, foundations, or government entities rather than directly from nonprofits.

**Key Finding:** True "TA Provider Pool" models where the state/funder maintains an approved list AND pays vendors directly are **rare**. Most programs fall into one of these categories:

| Model Type | How It Works | Count Found |
|------------|--------------|-------------|
| **Direct Payment Pools** | State/funder pays consultant directly | 3 |
| **Consultant Collaboratives** | Vetted list; funder subsidizes fees | 4 |
| **Directory Listings** | Visibility only; nonprofit pays consultant | 6 |
| **Grant Programs** | Nonprofit receives grant, chooses own consultant | 5 |
| **Internal Consulting** | Organization provides consulting directly | 3 |

**Top Recommendation:** Focus on the 7 programs that either pay consultants directly or maintain vetted lists that drive referrals. The others provide visibility but don't match the "automatic reimbursement" model sought.

## 1. King County, WA - Grant Application & Capacity Building (GACB) Program

**Administering Agency:** King County Department of Community & Human Services (DCHS)
**Website:** https://kingcounty.gov/en/dept/dchs/human-social-services/funding-opportunities-dchs/grant-application-capacity-building

### How It Works
King County contracts with consultants to provide free capacity-building services to nonprofits applying for or receiving DCHS funding. **County pays consultants directly** - nonprofits pay nothing.

### Services Covered
- Grant application consulting
- Capacity building consulting
- Organizational development
- Financial management
- Strategic planning

### Payment Structure
- County contracts directly with consultants
- Consultants bill King County, not nonprofits
- Multi-year contract opportunities

### How to Apply
- **Currently Open:** Check King County procurement portal
- **Deadline:** Periodic RFPs
- **Requirements:** Demonstrated expertise in nonprofit capacity building
- **Approval Timeline:** Contract procurement process

### Outreach Play After Approval
- **Recipient Lists Public:** County maintains list of funded nonprofits
- **Grant Cycle Timing:** DCHS funding cycles
- **Co-Marketing Available:** Yes - listed as contracted provider
- **How to Find Recipients:** Contact DCHS for referrals; check county funding announcements

### Fit Score: 9/10
**Rationale:** This is the closest match to the ideal model. County pays directly, and "grant application consulting" clearly covers grant readiness work. Geographic limitation (King County) is the only drawback.

### Recommended Next Steps
1. Monitor King County procurement portal for consultant RFPs
2. Contact DCHS directly to express interest: 206-263-9105
3. Request information on current consultant needs and procurement timeline
4. Prepare qualifications package highlighting grant readiness expertise

## 2. Pennsylvania - PANO Consultant Collaborative

**Administering Agency:** Pennsylvania Association of Nonprofit Organizations (PANO)
**Website:** https://pano.org/consulting/

### How It Works
PANO maintains a vetted "Consultant Collaborative" and has a limited pool of capacity-building funding (April-December 2025) to subsidize consulting fees for PANO members.

### Services Covered
- Strategic planning
- Governance training
- Board retreat facilitation
- Fundraising (includes grant readiness)
- Human resources
- Merger assistance

### Payment Structure
- PANO subsidizes consultant fees from grant pool
- Consultant is paid through PANO funds
- Some engagements may be partially nonprofit-paid

### How to Apply
- **Currently Open:** Yes (April-December 2025)
- **Deadline:** Rolling through December 2025
- **Requirements:** Submit application to join Consultant Collaborative; attend monthly Zoom calls
- **Approval Timeline:** 2-4 weeks for vetting

### Outreach Play After Approval
- **Recipient Lists Public:** No - but PANO connects consultants with requesting members
- **Grant Cycle Timing:** Current pool runs through Dec 2025
- **Co-Marketing Available:** Yes - listed in consultant directory; monthly networking
- **How to Find Recipients:** PANO makes introductions based on nonprofit requests

### Fit Score: 8/10
**Rationale:** Active program with funding through 2025. "Fundraising" category can include grant readiness. Low barrier to entry - just need to apply and get vetted. Monthly consultant collaborative meetings provide networking.

### Recommended Next Steps
1. Apply for PANO membership (required first step)
2. Complete "Request a Consultant" application to join Collaborative
3. Attend monthly Consultant Collaborative Zoom meetings
4. Request introduction to nonprofits seeking fundraising/grant support

**Website:** https://nonprofitbuilder.org/for-consultants

### How It Works
Nonprofit Builder connects foundations with their grantees and pre-vetted consultants. Foundations pay for grantee capacity-building through the platform. Consultants receive referrals and payment without billing nonprofits directly.

### Services Covered
- Full range of organizational development
- Strategic planning
- Fundraising and development
- Board governance
- Financial management
- Leadership development

### Payment Structure
- Foundations contract with Nonprofit Builder (minimum $50K/year)
- Foundation covers all grantee capacity-building costs
- Consultants are paid through platform
- 800+ consultants in network; 30+ foundations participate

### How to Apply
- **Currently Open:** By referral only
- **Deadline:** Rolling
- **Requirements:** Referral from existing Nonprofit Builder consultant OR member foundation
- **Approval Timeline:** Varies; vetting process required

### Outreach Play After Approval
- **Recipient Lists Public:** No - private platform
- **Grant Cycle Timing:** Ongoing throughout year
- **Co-Marketing Available:** Yes - vetted consultant network with foundation referrals
- **How to Find Recipients:** Platform handles matching

### Fit Score: 8/10
**Rationale:** Excellent model with significant scale (800+ consultants, 30+ foundations). Challenge is getting the initial referral. Worth cultivating relationships with existing consultants to get introduced.

### Recommended Next Steps

## Key Insights & Recommendations

### What We Learned

1. **True "voucher + approved vendor" models are rare.** Most states provide grants to nonprofits who then choose their own consultants. There's no centralized "approved list" that guarantees referrals.

2. **State nonprofit associations are the gatekeepers.** PANO (PA), Forefront (IL), MNA (MI), CNA (CO) all maintain consultant directories or networks. These are the primary pathway to visibility.

3. **County/local programs may be better than state programs.** King County's GACB program has the exact model sought - government pays consultants directly. Other counties may have similar programs worth investigating.

4. **Foundation platforms offer scale.** Nonprofit Builder connects to 30+ foundations with a single application. Worth the effort to get a referral and join.

5. **"Grant readiness" fits under multiple categories.** Position the service as:
   - "Fundraising capacity building"
   - "Fund development planning"
   - "Grant application consulting"
   - "Government funding preparation"

### Strategic Recommendations

1. **Start with PANO immediately.** Active funding, clear pathway, rolling acceptance. Pennsylvania is a large nonprofit market.

2. **Pursue King County as a model.** Even if geographic limitation applies, this validates the concept. Research whether other large counties have similar programs.

3. **Build toward Nonprofit Builder.** This is the long-term play with best scale. Start cultivating referral relationships now.

4. **Position as "fund development" not just "grant discovery."** Broader category fits more program criteria.

5. **Create a one-pager for program administrators.** When contacting programs, have a clear, professional summary of services that fits their language.
