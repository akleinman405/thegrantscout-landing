# Lessons Learned: TA Provider Pools Research
**Date:** 2025-12-10

---

## 1. What We Were Looking For

State-level Technical Assistance (TA) Provider Pools where Alec could get approved as a capacity-building vendor and receive payment directly from states or foundations - rather than billing nonprofits - creating an "automatic reimbursement" model for TheGrantScout's services.

---

## 2. What We Found

- **21 programs identified** across 15 states plus national platforms
- **Key finding:** True "direct payment" models are rare - only 3 of 21 programs pay consultants directly; most give grants to nonprofits who then choose their own consultants
- **Best opportunity:** King County GACB (WA) - county pays consultants directly for grant application support; exact match to target model
- **Most actionable:** PANO Consultant Collaborative (PA) - active funding through Dec 2025, low barrier to entry, rolling acceptance
- **Best scale:** Nonprofit Builder (national) - 30+ foundations, 800+ consultants, but requires referral to join

---

## 3. What Worked Well

- **Categorizing program models** (Direct Payment, Consultant Collaboratives, Directory Listings, Grant Programs, Internal Consulting) made the findings actionable - immediately clear which programs match the target model
- **Fit scoring system** (1-10 based on service coverage, enrollment status, recipient list access, payment clarity, state size, co-marketing) enabled quick prioritization
- **Producing tiered recommendations** (Pursue Immediately / Worth Pursuing / Monitor / Not Recommended) gave clear next steps

---

## 4. What to Improve Next Time

- **Search county-level, not just state-level** - King County (the best find) is a county program. Large metro counties (LA, Cook, Harris, Maricopa, Miami-Dade) likely have similar programs that state-level searches missed
- **Search by funding source, not program name** - CDBG, CSBG, AmeriCorps, and ARPA all fund TA programs but aren't labeled "capacity building." Searching these funding streams would uncover more opportunities
- **Mine procurement portals for past RFPs** - TA provider contracts renew every 2-5 years. Tracking past RFPs reveals upcoming opportunities before they're announced

---

## 5. Key Insight

**The "voucher + approved vendor list" model we envisioned is rare, but the "county/foundation contracts directly with consultants" model does exist and is replicable.** King County proves the concept works - the Research Team should now systematically search the top 25 US counties for similar programs.
