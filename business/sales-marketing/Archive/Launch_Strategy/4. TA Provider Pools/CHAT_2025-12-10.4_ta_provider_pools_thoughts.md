# Summary: How to Find More Bullseye TA Provider Opportunities  
### (Summary of Discussion Following the Research Team’s TA Provider Pools Report)

---

## ⭐ What This Summary Covers  
After reviewing the Research Team's TA Provider Pools report, we discussed **how you can systematically identify more “bullseye” programs** — meaning:

- Programs where **you get paid directly** by a government agency, foundation, or intermediary  
- Nonprofits receive TheGrantScout **at no cost**  
- Your work is funded as **capacity building or technical assistance (TA)**  

This document summarizes the insights, patterns, and search strategies we outlined.

---

# ⭐ The Bullseye Model (Anchor Example)  
The King County GACB program is the perfect blueprint:

- County runs nonprofit funding programs  
- County ALSO pays external consultants to support grantees  
- Consultants bill the county directly  
- Nonprofits receive the service free  

This “funder → consultant” payment model is rare but repeatable.

We use it as the **pattern** the Research Team can search for nationwide.

---

# ⭐ How to Find More Bullseye Opportunities

---

## 1️⃣ Pattern-Matching Against King County  
Look for programs with these attributes:

- Government or foundation administers nonprofit grants  
- Provides capacity-building or grant application support  
- Uses contracted consultants  
- Pays the consultant directly  
- Nonprofits do not pay  

Search for keywords like:

- “capacity building consultant”  
- “technical assistance provider”  
- “grant application support”  
- “organizational development consultant”  
- “consultant RFP”  
- “nonprofit TA provider pool”  

This pattern-based approach returns opportunities normal keyword searches miss.

---

## 2️⃣ Search the Largest Counties & Cities  
Programs like King County usually appear in large metro counties:

- Los Angeles County  
- Cook County (Chicago)  
- Harris County (Houston)  
- Maricopa County (Phoenix)  
- Miami-Dade County  
- Dallas County  
- Bexar County  
- Santa Clara / Silicon Valley  
- San Francisco / Alameda / San Diego  

Your Research Team can systematically:

- search procurement portals  
- review past RFPs  
- identify programs focused on nonprofit support

These are the highest-probability areas for bullseye models.

---

## 3️⃣ Search Foundation-Funded Intermediary Models  
Programs like **Nonprofit Builder** (where foundations pay you to serve their grantees) exist because:

- Foundations fund capacity-building intermediaries  
- Intermediaries maintain consultant pools  
- Consultants get paid directly  
- Nonprofits receive subsidized or free services  

Search for:

- “capacity building intermediary”  
- “foundation-funded TA provider”  
- “organizational effectiveness initiative”  
- “funder-sponsored consultant pool”  

Many major foundations (Ford, Kellogg, Kresge, Sherwood) run versions of these.

---

## 4️⃣ Explore State Service Commissions & AmeriCorps TA Programs  
Every state has a Service Commission that oversees AmeriCorps/VISTA capacity-building.

Often they:

- provide technical assistance to nonprofits  
- contract external consultants  
- pay consultants directly  

Search for:

- “AmeriCorps VISTA capacity building provider”  
- “state service commission consultant application”  
- “AmeriCorps TA provider RFP”  

These are a strong mid-tier source of opportunities.

---

## 5️⃣ Search HUD CDBG Technical Assistance Mechanisms  
HUD’s Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) program allows:

- nonprofit support  
- consultant contracting  
- organizational development services  

Cities and counties use CDBG funds to hire consultants.

Search terms:

- “CDBG nonprofit technical assistance”  
- “CDBG consultant RFP”  
- “capacity building services RFP”  

This uncovers opportunities that **don’t use the words “nonprofit” or “capacity building”** directly.

---

## 6️⃣ Mine ARPA/Recovery Plan Funding (Some Still Active Through 2026)  
Even though much ARPA money is obligated, derivatives exist:

- economic recovery TA  
- nonprofit stabilization contracts  
- consultant pools funded through local ARPA allocations  

Search:

- “ARPA nonprofit support consultant”  
- “SLFRF technical assistance”  
- “American Rescue Plan capacity building RFP”  

---

## 7️⃣ Include Arts & Culture Agency Programs  
Arts agencies surprisingly run many consultant pools:

- California Arts Council  
- New York State Council on the Arts  
- Chicago DCASE  

Common services funded:

- strategic planning  
- fundraising readiness  
- grant development support  

Search:

- “arts council consultant roster”  
- “capacity building consultant arts”  

---

## 8️⃣ Use Procurement Portals + Past RFP Mining  
For each city/county:

1. Register on procurement portal  
2. Search **past** RFPs for relevant terms  
3. Identify expired TA-provider contracts  
4. Track renewal cycles  
5. Set alerts for re-release  

TA-provider contracts often renew every **2–5 years**.

This method is extremely effective.

---

## 9️⃣ Use a Similarity Scoring Model  
Teach the Research Team to classify each discovered program by similarity to the King County model:

### Attributes:
- Administering agency  
- Purpose  
- Consultant involvement  
- Payment mechanism  
- Target population  
- Capacity-building focus  

Then score:

- **Similarity Score (0–100)**  
- **Likelihood of direct payment**  
- **Ease of application**  
- **Total opportunity size**  

This helps prioritize results.

---

## 🔟 Search by Funding Source (Not Program Name)  
Some funding sources consistently generate TA-provider opportunities:

- CDBG  
- CSBG  
- AmeriCorps  
- County human services budgets  
- Equity & empowerment offices  
- Foundation-organized capacity-building grants  

By searching for programs originating from these funding streams, the Research Team finds high-value opportunities that aren't labeled intuitively.

---

# ⭐ Final Summary  
The Research Team can reliably identify more “bullseye” TA provider opportunities by:

- Using King County as the anchor model  
- Searching major counties, cities, foundations, and service commissions  
- Mining procurement portals and past RFPs  
- Focusing on funding sources that regularly support TA  
- Building a similarity scoring model  
- Targeting both government-run and philanthropic intermediaries  

This uncovers rare but powerful programs where:

**You get paid directly, nonprofits pay nothing, and your service is treated as funded capacity building.**
