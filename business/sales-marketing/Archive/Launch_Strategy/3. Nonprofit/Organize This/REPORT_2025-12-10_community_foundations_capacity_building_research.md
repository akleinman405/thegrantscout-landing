## SUMMARY ANALYSIS

### Capacity Building Program Types Found:

**High Activity (Formal Training/Cohort Programs):**
- South Dakota Community Foundation - 15-month credential program (CFRM/CFRE)
- Oklahoma City Community Foundation - Quarterly endowment training workshops
- Community Foundation for Greater Buffalo - Cohort-based capacity building (arts-focused)
- Hawaii Community Foundation - Travel scholarships for professional development

**Active Support Services:**
- Tulsa Community Foundation - 501TechNet (25-50% cost reduction on tech services)
- Marin Community Foundation - Community Power Initiative with TA; on-site training
- Arkansas Community Foundation - Giving Tree grants for capacity building
- Community Foundation of Middle Tennessee - Technical assistance programs

**Grantmaker-Focused Programs:**
- Oklahoma City Community Foundation - Endowment training (for organizations with endowments)

**Related/Limited Programs:**
- Greater Houston Community Foundation - Foundation Services and educational programming
- Community Foundation of New Jersey - Grantmaking focus; related regional orgs provide TA

### Key Insights:

1. **Vendor Hiring**: All foundations showing capacity programs appear to contract external vendors/consultants for training delivery, technical assistance, and specialized services.

2. **Nonprofit Volume**: Large variations - from specific program cohorts (SD: 15-month program, HCF: 21+ multiyear FLEX recipients) to thousands served through grants (8,000+ for CFNJ).

3. **Most Structured Programs**: South Dakota (credential program), Oklahoma City (workshop series), and Hawaii (travel scholarships) have most formally defined training offerings.

4. **Emerging Trend**: Multiple foundations (especially Hawaii, Marin, Arkansas) combining grant funding with technical assistance rather than grants alone.

5. **Arts Sector Focus**: Buffalo's capacity building most explicitly detailed for arts nonprofits; others serve broader sectors.

6. **Endowment Focus**: Oklahoma City and Arkansas heavily emphasize endowment-building as sustainability capacity.
