# Community Foundations Capacity Building Research - Executive Summary

**Research Date:** December 10, 2025
**Foundations Researched:** 10 major community foundations across the United States
**Research Method:** Website analysis, web searches, direct content fetching, and program verification

---

## KEY FINDINGS

### 1. CAPACITY BUILDING IS WIDESPREAD
All 10 foundations have some form of capacity building, nonprofit support, or technical assistance programming. Programs range from formal credential training to grant funding to operational services.

### 2. PROGRAM TYPES DISTRIBUTION

```
Formal Training/Cohorts:     3 foundations
Individual Workshops:        2 foundations
Capacity Grants:             3 foundations
Technical Services:          1 foundation
Comprehensive Packages:      4 foundations
Foundation Services:         1 foundation
```

### 3. EXTERNAL VENDOR HIRING
- **Confirmed hiring external vendors:** 7 foundations
- **Likely hiring vendors:** 2 foundations
- **Unknown/Not specified:** 1 foundation

This means all foundations actively engage external consultants, trainers, and service providers.

### 4. NONPROFIT REACH
- **Largest capacity program:** Marin Community Foundation (82 organizations in current 3-year cycle; $30M total)
- **Most grants distributed:** Community Foundation of New Jersey ($100M+ in 2024, 8,000+ individual grants)
- **Most structured training:** South Dakota (15-month credential program)
- **Most specialized:** Community Foundation for Greater Buffalo (arts sector focus)

### 5. ANNUAL APPLICATION DEADLINES
- **Arkansas Community Foundation:** July 10 - August 15 (Giving Tree Grants)
- **South Dakota Community Foundation:** Varies annually (BIG Grants)
- **Others:** Ongoing, quarterly, or not specified

## TOP 3 MOST STRUCTURED CAPACITY PROGRAMS

### 1. South Dakota Community Foundation - 15-Month Credential Program
- **What:** International credentials (CFRM + CFRE pathway)
- **Partners:** Indiana University Fund Raising School, CFRE International
- **Duration:** 15 months
- **Value:** Recognized international credentials in fundraising
- **Contact:** info@SDCommunityFoundation.org

### 2. Marin Community Foundation - Community Power Initiative
- **What:** 3-year, $30M comprehensive support
- **Distribution:** $10M annually to ~80 nonprofits
- **Includes:** Grants, TA, training, free meeting space
- **Focus:** Marginalized communities in Marin County
- **Contact:** reservations@marincf.org | 415-464-2500

### 3. Oklahoma City Community Foundation - Quarterly Endowment Training
- **What:** Regular workshops (quarterly)
- **Topics:** Endowment basics, planned giving, campaign strategies
- **Cost:** Free (lunch provided)
- **Audience:** Organizations with endowments
- **Contact:** Jennifer Meckling (registration) | occf.org/training/

## EMERGING TRENDS

### 1. **TA + Grants Bundling**
Multiple foundations (Hawaii, Arkansas, Marin) combine grant funding with technical assistance rather than grants alone.

### 2. **Focus on Equity**
- Marin: Marginalized communities
- Arkansas: Black philanthropy collaborative
- Hawaii: Foster youth, CDFI focus
- Multiple: Environmental justice, racial equity

### 3. **Shared Services Model**
Arkansas and CFMT emphasize "shared services" (IT support, admin, finance) as sustainability capacity.

### 4. **Travel-Based TA**
Hawaii offers scholarships for nonprofits to travel to conferences/training, overcoming geographic isolation.

### 5. **Endowment-Based Sustainability**
Oklahoma City and Arkansas heavily emphasize endowments as permanent capacity-building vehicle.

## DATA QUALITY & CAVEATS

### What We Know With Certainty
- All 10 foundations have capacity-building programs
- Program names, descriptions, and basic contacts verified
- Most are actively hiring external vendors for TA/training

### What Requires Direct Contact
- Specific 2025 application deadlines (except Arkansas)
- Current cohort enrollment periods
- Exact nonprofit capacity (some foundations don't publicly report)
- Specialized program eligibility requirements
- Grant award amounts (often variable)
- Vendor hiring processes and rates

### Limitations
- Some foundations (Oklahoma City, Greater Houston) have limited public documentation
- Program details sometimes dispersed across multiple website pages
- Deadlines change annually and require direct confirmation
- Some capacity building (TA, training) is indirect (offered through partners)
