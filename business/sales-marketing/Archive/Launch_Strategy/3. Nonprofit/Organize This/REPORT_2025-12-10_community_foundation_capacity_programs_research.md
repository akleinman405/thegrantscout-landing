## SUMMARY FINDINGS

### Foundations with ROBUST Capacity Programs (Active, Detailed, Multiple Options)
1. **San Antonio Area Foundation** - Extensive training portfolio (certificates, workshops, cohorts)
2. **Columbus Foundation** - Comprehensive capacity-building infrastructure with multiple programs
3. **East Bay Community Foundation** - Specialized accelerator/stabilizer for Black-led organizations
4. **Community Foundation of Louisville** - Multiple programs (Alden Fellows, Fund for Louisville, Racial Justice Cohort)
5. **Central Indiana Community Foundation** - Growing capacity infrastructure with new director focus

### Foundations with MODERATE Capacity Programs (Some Programs, Less Detail)
6. **Rochester Area Community Foundation** - Workshop series and collaborative initiatives
7. **Lincoln Community Foundation** - "Beyond the Grants" support services (vague)
8. **Wichita Community Foundation** - Nonprofit Elevation initiative (limited detail)

### Foundations with LIMITED/UNKNOWN Capacity Programs
9. **Community Foundation of Greater Birmingham** - No detailed programs identified
10. **Community Foundation of Greater Chattanooga** - No detailed programs identified

### Key Patterns Observed

**Program Types Most Common:**
- Training/Certificates (SAAFDN, Columbus, CICF)
- Capacity-building grants (Louisville, EBCF, Columbus, CICF)
- Cohort-based learning (Louisville, EBCF, Columbus)
- Grantee support/resources (Most foundations)

**Vendor/Consultant Hiring:**
- Confirmed: SAAFDN, Columbus, EBCF, Louisville (CNPE partnerships), CICF (Fund Raising School)
- Unknown: Most others

**Geographic/Demographic Focus:**
- EBCF: Strong focus on Black-led organizations (ASCEND:BLO)
- Louisville: Racial justice focus (Racial Justice Cohort, Black-led organizations)
- CICF: Racial equity (Racial Equity Fund)

**Program Sizes:**
- Small cohorts: EBCF (5 orgs per cohort), Alden Fellows (5 per year)
- Larger capacity: Columbus Board Series (15 orgs), Louisville Racial Justice Cohort (12 orgs)
- Variable: SAAFDN (limited seats, not specified)

### Challenges in Research

1. **Limited Public Information:** Several foundations (Birmingham, Chattanooga) don't publicize capacity programs on main websites
2. **Vague Program Descriptions:** Some programs lack detail on contact, deadlines, cohort size
3. **2025 Deadlines:** Some programs' current deadlines not found; previous year info outdated
4. **Inconsistent Terminology:** "Capacity building," "grantee support," "technical assistance" used inconsistently
5. **Website Navigation:** Some programs buried in secondary pages or nonprofit portals

### Recommendations for Next Steps

1. **Direct Contact:** For foundations with limited info (Birmingham, Chattanooga, Lincoln, Wichita), direct contact required for complete information
2. **Program Deep Dives:** Top 5 foundations warrant detailed inquiry about:
   - Vendor/contractor procurement processes
   - Specific deadlines for 2025-2026
   - Participant satisfaction/outcomes
   - Capacity to hire external consultants
3. **Segmentation:** Consider separate approach for:
   - Large, structured programs (SAAFDN, Columbus, EBCF)
   - Resource-based support (CICF, Wichita, Lincoln)
   - Limited programs (Birmingham, Chattanooga)
