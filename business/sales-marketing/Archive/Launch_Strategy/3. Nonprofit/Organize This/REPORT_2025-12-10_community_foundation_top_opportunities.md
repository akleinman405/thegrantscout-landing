## NEXT STEPS FOR VENDOR OUTREACH

### Phase 1: Direct Contact (Week 1-2)
- Email key contacts with brief introduction
- Propose informational calls with program directors
- Request current vendor/partner lists
- Ask about RFP processes

### Phase 2: Intelligence Gathering (Week 2-3)
- Schedule calls with Erin Scott (Columbus), Diana Coyle (CICF), learning@saafdn.org
- Request vendor engagement details:
  - How do they procure consultants?
  - What budget levels available?
  - What are pain points/gaps?
  - What outcomes do they measure?
- Research existing vendor relationships (websites, LinkedIn)

### Phase 3: Strategic Positioning (Week 3-4)
- Develop targeted proposals for top 3 foundations
- Pilot program ideas aligned with their stated needs
- Build business case with outcomes data
- Position within their strategic priorities (equity, emerging leaders, sustainability)

## KEY INSIGHTS

1. **Capacity Building is Growing:** 8 of 10 foundations have some capacity-building activity, suggesting sector trend

2. **Partnership Model Wins:** Foundations hiring external vendors outpace those with all-internal programs (indicates openness to outsourcing)

3. **Equity Focus is Strong:** 5 foundations emphasize racial equity, DEI, or Black-led organizations in capacity work (opportunity for specialized expertise)

4. **Leadership Development Hot:** Executive coaching, board governance, leadership programs across all foundations with programs (consistent need)

5. **Affordability Matters:** SAAFDN's low-cost model ($980) suggests nonprofits want accessible training (opportunity for scaled solutions)

6. **Information Gap:** 2 of 10 foundations have limited public information on programs (suggests marketing/communication opportunity)
