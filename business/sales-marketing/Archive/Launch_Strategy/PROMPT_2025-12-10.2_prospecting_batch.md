## PROMPT 7: Government / SBA Programs + TA Provider Pools

### Mission
Research whether government programs exist that could fund or subsidize TheGrantScout for nonprofits, with special focus on Technical Assistance (TA) Provider Pools.

### What Are TA Provider Pools?
Many states maintain approved vendor lists of "Capacity-Building Providers" or "Technical Assistance Providers." When a nonprofit receives a capacity-building grant, they can hire vendors from this pre-approved list — and the STATE pays the vendor directly, not the nonprofit.

This is the closest model to "automatic reimbursement" in the nonprofit space.

### Research Questions
1. Which states have TA Provider Pools or approved vendor lists for nonprofit capacity building?
2. What's the application process to get on these lists?
3. What services are covered? (grant writing, fundraising strategy, technology tools?)
4. How do nonprofits access these services? (vouchers, referrals, direct contracts?)
5. What are the payment structures? (per hour, per engagement, per nonprofit served?)
6. Do SBDCs fund tools for nonprofits?
7. Are there state-level nonprofit infrastructure grants?
8. Do any federal programs fund "technical assistance" that could include grant discovery tools?

### Outreach Strategy Questions (Critical)
9. **Are voucher/grant recipient lists public?** Can we find out which nonprofits received capacity-building grants?
10. **When are capacity grant cycles announced?** What's the timing so we can outreach right after awards?
11. **Will agencies share recipient lists with approved providers?** Can we request the list directly?
12. **Do agencies send "available providers" info to recipients?** Can we get included in that communication?
13. **Are there press releases or announcements when grants are awarded?** Where to monitor?
14. **Can approved providers co-market with the administering agency?** (joint emails, inclusion in materials)

### Research Sources
- State nonprofit association websites (often list TA resources)
- State economic development agencies
- State community development offices
- AmeriCorps state commissions
- SBA.gov and SBDC network
- SAM.gov (search for nonprofit technical assistance contracts)
- Corporation for National and Community Service (AmeriCorps programs)
- Search: "[State] nonprofit technical assistance provider list"
- Search: "[State] capacity building approved vendors"

### Priority States to Research
1. California
2. Texas
3. New York
4. Florida
5. Illinois
6. Pennsylvania
7. Ohio

9. North Carolina
10. Michigan
