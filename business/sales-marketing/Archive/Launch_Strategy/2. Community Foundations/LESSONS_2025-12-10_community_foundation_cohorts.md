# Lessons Learned: Community Foundation Cohorts Research

**Date:** 2025-12-10
**Project:** B2B Partnership Opportunities with Community Foundations

---

## 1. What We Were Looking For

Community foundations running capacity-building cohorts or grantee support programs where TheGrantScout could pitch as a vendor serving 10-50+ nonprofits per deal ($12K-$50K).

---

## 2. What We Found

- **50 foundations researched** via F990 database + web research on program details
- **100% have capacity-building programs** - every foundation offers some form of nonprofit support
- **35+ explicitly hire external vendors** for coaching, training, and technical assistance
- **7 Tier 1 opportunities identified** with direct contacts and upcoming deadlines (Baton Rouge, New Haven, Omaha, Atlanta, Marin, Ozarks, Monterey)
- **Existing vendor ecosystem confirmed** - Resilia, Catchafire, Nonprofit Leadership Center already operate in this space, validating the market

---

## 3. What Worked Well

- **Parallel research agents** (5 agents researching 10 foundations each) dramatically accelerated web research
- **Structured output format** made it easy to compile into CSV and prioritize prospects
- **Deadline tracking** surfaced actionable timing (Ozarks Jan 10, Orange County March, Atlanta April launch)
- **Contact info collection** yielded 8+ direct program officer names and emails for immediate outreach

---

## 4. What to Improve Next Time

- **Budget info was sparse** - few foundations publish what they pay vendors; would benefit from direct outreach to learn typical contract sizes
- **Application requirements unclear** - some foundations have RFP processes vs. informal vendor relationships; need to clarify before pitching
- **No warm introductions identified** - should cross-reference against Alec's network for any existing connections to these foundations

---

## 5. Key Insight

**This is a consultant-led market, not a SaaS-led market.** Foundations hire people who facilitate, teach, and guide their nonprofits. The product powers the value, but the relationship is what they buy. TheGrantScout should position as a capacity-building specialist, not just software.
