## RED FLAGS - AVOID IN PITCHES

- Starting with "I'm reaching out to..."
- Calling it a "solution" instead of describing what it does
- Mentioning the price before proving the value
- Generic "nonprofit leaders" instead of specific role (grant officer, executive director)
- Overusing AI/machine learning language without explanation
- Making claims you can't back up with data
- Treating all editors the same (customize every pitch)

---

## GREEN FLAGS - INCLUDE IN PITCHES

- Specific data from your research
- Clear hook: "Why this matters to your readers right now"
- Named editor (not "Dear Editor")
- Concise (under 200 words)
- Offer something specific (op-ed, interview, data, contributed piece)
- Verifiable claims only
- Respect for their publication's editorial focus
- Professional tone without jargon

---

## FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE

**Day 1**: Send initial pitch
**Day 6-7**: If no response, send brief follow-up (1 paragraph, LinkedIn message preferred)
**Day 14**: If still no response, try different angle or move to next contact
**Day 30+**: Wait 30 days, re-pitch with seasonal/news angle

**Never**: Send more than 2-3 follow-ups without engagement. Move on.

## WHAT TO DO IF YOU GET A "YES"

1. **Respond within 24 hours** - "Thank you for your interest!"
2. **Ask clarifying questions**:
   - What angle interests them most?
   - What's their timeline?
   - What do they want from you (interview, data, case study)?
   - Any specific founder quote/angle they want?
3. **Deliver what you promised** - on time, high quality
4. **Make it easy for them** - short quotes, clear data, compelling story
5. **Follow up after publication** - thank them, ask for tips for next story
6. **Reuse the story** - social amplification, case study, pitch to other publications

## MEASUREMENT & SUCCESS

**Track**:
- Pitches sent (goal: 15+ in first month)
- Response rate (target: 10-15%)
- Stories published (goal: 1-2 in 60 days)
- Leads generated per article (estimate: 500-2000)
- Relationships built (indicator: 3+ repeat stories from same publication)

**Celebrate**:
- Every response (even "no thanks")
- Every publication mention
- Every quote used
- Every inbound media inquiry

## REMEMBER

- **Reporters are human** - personalization matters
- **Data is currency** - bring insights they can't get elsewhere
- **Relationships > transactions** - think long-term, not one article
- **Respect their craft** - they're not just a distribution channel
- **Follow the angle they want** - not the angle you think is best
- **Be responsive** - they work on tight deadlines
- **Deliver value** - every interaction should improve their story, not just yours
