# TheGrantScout PR Outreach Messaging Guide
## Media Pitch Templates & Key Talking Points

---

## CORE POSITIONING

**TheGrantScout** is an AI-powered grant discovery platform ($99/month) that uses IRS 990-PF tax data to help nonprofits find and connect with foundations most likely to fund their work. We democratize foundation intelligence that was previously available only through expensive consulting or insider networks.

**Vision**: Every nonprofit should have access to the same data-driven funding intelligence that major foundations use to make decisions.

---

## THREE CORE NEWS ANGLES

### Angle 1: DEMOCRATIZATION / DATA TRANSPARENCY
**"Foundation Funding Data as a Public Good"**

- IRS 990-PF data is public, but 99% of nonprofits don't know how to use it
- TheGrantScout translates 8.3M historical grant records into actionable intelligence
- Makes foundation matching accessible to nonprofits without grant consultants
- News hook: Tech democratizing access to funding intelligence

**Best for publications**: Inside Philanthropy, Chronicle of Philanthropy, Nonprofit Quarterly, McKinsey

---

### Angle 2: TECHNOLOGY / AI INNOVATION
**"How AI is Solving the Grant Matching Problem"**

- 143,000+ foundations; how do nonprofits know which ones to target?
- Semantic embeddings match nonprofit missions to foundation giving patterns
- Machine learning identifies hidden funding opportunities
- 10-signal scoring algorithm considers: prior relationships, geographic match, grant size alignment, etc.
- News hook: AI/ML application in nonprofit sector

**Best for publications**: Nonprofit Tech for Good, TechSoup, Idealware, McKinsey, Stanford SSIR

---

### Angle 3: OPERATIONAL EFFICIENCY / TIME SAVINGS
**"Replacing Manual Grant Research with AI"**

- Average nonprofit grant officer spends 40-60 hours per year on foundation research
- TheGrantScout reduces this to 5-10 hours per year
- Eliminates guesswork; focuses effort on highest-probability prospects
- Cost comparison: $99/month vs. $5,000+/year for consultant
- News hook: Nonprofit operations/efficiency angle

**Best for publications**: NonProfit Times, Nonprofit Quarterly, Idealware, Nonprofit Tech for Good

## OBJECTION HANDLING

### "Why should I cover another SaaS product?"
- This isn't just a software product; it's about foundation funding transparency
- Touches on sector accountability, equity, and efficiency
- News angle: how AI is changing nonprofit-foundation relationships
- Data story: what 8.3M grants reveal about how funding really works

### "Aren't there already tools for grant research?"
- Paid databases charge $5,000+/year and have limited matching intelligence
- TheGrantScout brings AI + affordability
- Only tool using full IRS 990-PF semantic analysis
- Different positioning: AI-powered discovery vs. database subscription

### "This seems like a startup pitch disguised as news"
- Lead with the data/transparency/equity angle, not the product
- Pitch as sector story, not company story
- Offer data-driven insights reporters can use independently
- Frame as "what this reveals about how philanthropy works"

## FOLLOW-UP STRATEGY

### Tier 1 Publications (High Priority):
1. Initial pitch: Direct email to named editor
2. Wait 5-7 days
3. Follow-up: Phone call or LinkedIn message
4. If no response: Try alternate contact or refresh angle
5. Try again in 60 days with different news hook

### Tier 2 Publications (Medium Priority):
1. Initial pitch via contact form or general email
2. Wait 10 days
3. Follow-up: Phone or LinkedIn
4. If no response: Quarterly outreach with seasonal angles

### Tier 3 Publications (Lower Priority):
1. Quarterly newsletter/trends pitch
2. Guest blog/contributed content offers
3. Evergreen feature ideas
