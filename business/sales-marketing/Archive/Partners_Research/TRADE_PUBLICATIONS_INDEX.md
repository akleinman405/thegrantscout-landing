## TIPS FOR SUCCESS

1. **Customization is king** - Generic emails get deleted. Use editor's name, publication focus, recent articles.

2. **Lead with value** - Your first sentence should explain why THEY should care, not why you're cool.

3. **Data is your credibility** - Use specific numbers from your research. "143,000 foundations" is better than "lots of foundations."

4. **Short and scannable** - Editors are busy. Use bullet points, short paragraphs. Make it easy to say yes.

5. **Specific call to action** - "Interested in an interview?" not "Let me know if you want to work together."

6. **Respect their deadline** - When they say "can you send this by Friday?", send it Wednesday.

7. **One pitch, one ask** - Don't suggest multiple story angles at once. Pick the strongest one.

8. **Follow their style** - Chronicle writes differently than TechSoup. Read recent articles. Mirror their tone.

9. **Relationships > transactions** - You're building a source relationship, not closing a deal.

10. **Persistence without harassment** - 2-3 touches is good. 5+ is too much.
