## RESEARCH FINDINGS

### Strengths Found:
1. **Well-organized editorial teams** - Most publications have clear editorial hierarchies and accessible editor contact information
2. **Active coverage** of nonprofit technology and funding topics
3. **Multiple entry points** - Different editors cover different angles (technology, operations, equity, strategy)
4. **Professional communities** - Editors are active on LinkedIn and responsive to well-crafted pitches
5. **Strong industry influence** - These publications shape nonprofit sector strategy and technology adoption

### Challenges Identified:
1. **Limited personal email addresses publicly available** - Some editors behind institutional email filters
2. **No centralized media list** - Had to research individually rather than finding coordinated database
3. **Publication-specific story windows** - Different timing windows for quarterly vs. daily publications
4. **Technology beat coverage varies** - Some publications (Idealware, TechSoup) exclusively tech; others (Chronicle) broader
5. **Gatekeeper problem remains** - Getting past editorial gatekeepers requires strong story angle per publication

### Data Accessibility:
- **High**: Chronicle, Inside Philanthropy, Nonprofit Quarterly, PND, SSIR (direct contact info available)
- **Medium**: Council of Foundations, Idealware, NonProfit Times (some info, may need research)
- **Lower**: McKinsey.org, Candid, Giving USA (institutional contacts, less personal info)
