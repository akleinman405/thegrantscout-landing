# Mission Clarity Analysis: 17 Nonprofits

**Analysis Date:** 2025-12-11
**Organizations Analyzed:** 17
**Data Source:** Organization websites + 990 mission statements

---

## Executive Summary

This analysis examined 17 nonprofits across arts, education, science, and heritage sectors to assess mission clarity, structure, and organizational descriptiveness. Key findings:

- **77% have clear, one-liner missions** (13 of 17 score 1-2 on clarity scale)
- **94% follow "We help X by doing Y" structure** (16 of 17)
- **Mission statements average 15.6 words** when clearly stated
- **82% have active fundraising campaigns** visible on websites (14 of 17)
- **47% have descriptive organization names** that clearly indicate purpose (8 of 17)

## Key Insights & Patterns

### 1. Clarity Sweet Spot: 12-16 Word Missions
Organizations with missions between 12-16 words had the highest clarity scores and easiest website navigation to find them.

### 2. Museums & Broadcasters Run Broader Programs
- Average 6-8 programs vs. 2-4 for mission-focused orgs
- More complex missions require more explanation
- This correlates with community anchor institution status

### 3. Theater Orgs Have Weakest Name Clarity
- 4 of 5 theater organizations use abstract/metaphorical names
- Exception: Renaissance Performing Arts (most explicit name = easiest mission to find)

### 4. Fundraising Visibility Tracks with Organization Age
- Established orgs (30+ years): 90%+ have visible campaigns
- Newer/smaller orgs: 50% have visible campaigns

### 5. "By Doing" Method More Important Than "Helping"
Most organizations can clearly state their beneficiary (children, communities, artists) but struggle articulating their unique method/approach.

## Recommendations for Mission Clarity

### For Organizations Scoring "2" (Moderate):
1. **Add formal mission statement to homepage** (currently buried or absent)
2. **Reduce mission statement to under 16 words** (Weston Ranch at 31 is too long)
3. **Lead with beneficiary, not origin story** (Weston Ranch buries main point)
4. **Test mission clarity:** Can visitors state mission in under 10 seconds?

### For Organizations with Abstract Names:
1. **Add tagline below name** ("Kahuli Leo Lea: Hawaiian Music & Cultural Education")
2. **Create "Why We Exist" elevator pitch** on homepage
3. **Use navigation headers** to guide visitors (e.g., "Our Work" instead of vague tabs)

### For All Organizations:
1. **Benchmark against museums/symphonies** - these have clearest missions (1-3 clarity scores)
2. **Place mission statement in top third of homepage** - current practice varies widely
3. **Test with outsiders** - ask 3 people who don't know org: "What do they do?"
