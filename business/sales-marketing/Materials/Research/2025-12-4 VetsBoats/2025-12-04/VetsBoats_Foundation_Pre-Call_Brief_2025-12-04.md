### What They Claim to Measure

### How They Measure - UNCLEAR

- **No third-party evaluation** evident
- **No standardized instruments** mentioned (PHQ-9, PCL-5 for PTSD, etc.)
- Charity Navigator shows "Impact & Measurement: Not scored"

### External Research (Not VetsBoats-Specific)
VA studies on Mindfulness-Based Therapeutic Sailing show:
- Increased psychological flexibility (p=0.008)
- Increased positive affect (p=0.008)
- Higher SUD treatment completion rates
- **But:** PTSD-specific outcomes not measured in these studies

### Bottom Line
They count participants, not outcomes. This is common for small orgs but limits grant competitiveness for outcome-focused funders.

## Revised Talking Points for Call

1. **Acknowledge their success:** They've gotten $58K+ in grants - validate that. Don't position as if they're starting from zero.

2. **Probe the debt:** $300K liability is significant. Is grant funding for growth, or are they hoping grants can address cash flow issues? (Grants rarely solve cash flow problems)

3. **Budget reality check:** Have they ever paid for fundraising/grant help? What's their budget for this?

4. **VA Adaptive Sports Grant:** Real opportunity ($16M pot, sailing eligible) - July 2025 deadline. Could be a good test case.

5. **Outcome measurement:** If they want larger institutional grants, they'll need pre/post data. Are they open to implementing simple surveys?

6. **Capacity question:** With all-volunteer model, who actually writes and manages grants? Is Terry doing it himself?

7. **Be realistic about fit:** If they can't/won't pay for services, they may not be right for a paid subscription. Could be better as a referral or future prospect after stabilization.

---

## Red Flags to Watch For

- Expecting grants to solve debt problems
- No willingness to pay for professional services
- Terry is already stretched too thin
- Looking for someone to write grants for free
- Resistance to implementing outcome measurement
