# Client Acquisition Game Plan
**Date:** 2025-12-10  
**Goal:** 100 founding members  
**Timeline:** End of Q1 2026 (aggressive) / End of Q2 2026 (realistic)

---

## 1. Passive/Automated Campaigns (Set and Forget)

### 1.1 Email Campaign → Targeted Nonprofits
- **Info needed:**
  - Nonprofit name, EIN, contact email
  - Revenue ($500K-$5M)
  - NTEE code / sector
  - State
  - Foundation grant history (few/none = higher priority)
- **How to gather:**
  - F990 database query (C3, C4 queries from Research Team output)
  - Enrich with contact emails via email address finding script
- **Platform:** Email campaign script

### 1.2 Email Campaign → Grant Writing Firms
- **Info needed:**
  - Firm name, website, contact email
  - Geographic focus
  - Client types they serve
  - Size (solo vs. team)
- **How to gather:**
  - Web search: "grant writing consultant [state]"
  - LinkedIn search: "Grant Writer" + filter by location
  - GPA (Grant Professionals Association) directory
- **Platform:** Email campaign script

### 1.3 Email Campaign → Capacity-Building Funders
- **Info needed:**
  - Foundation name, EIN
  - Contact info (website, program officer if available)
  - Evidence they fund capacity building / nonprofit support
  - Typical grant size
- **How to gather:**
  - F990 query: grant purpose ILIKE '%capacity%' OR '%nonprofit support%' OR '%technical assistance%'
  - Cross-reference with foundation websites
- **Platform:** Email campaign script

### 1.4 Social Media / LinkedIn Posts
- **Info needed:**
  - None — just post
- **How to gather:**
  - N/A
- **Action:** Post on personal LinkedIn, Facebook announcing service

## 2. Active Network Mining (High-Value Research)

### 2.1 Beta Clients' Funders → Their Other Grantees
- **Info needed:**
  - List of foundations that funded each beta client
  - Other nonprofits those foundations have funded
  - Contact info for those nonprofits
- **How to gather:**
  - F990 query: Find foundation EINs from beta client grant history
  - F990 query: Find all other grantees of those foundations
  - Filter to similar size/sector
- **Outreach angle:** "We helped [beta client] — [Foundation X] also funds you, thought you might benefit"

### 2.2 LinkedIn Connections Audit
- **Info needed:**
  - Which of your connections work at nonprofits
  - Their role (ED, Development Director = decision maker)
  - Org size and sector
- **How to gather:**
  - LinkedIn: Export connections or manually review
  - Filter by title: "Executive Director," "Development," "Grants Manager"
- **Action:** Warm outreach to qualified connections

### 2.3 Friends & Family Nonprofit Connections
- **Info needed:**
  - Who in your network works at or knows someone at a nonprofit
  - Their boss / decision maker name
  - Org name and what they do
- **How to gather:**
  - Direct asks / texts
  - Already have some leads (mentioned bosses are interested)
- **Action:** Ask for warm intros

### 2.4 Beta Client Warm Intro Asks
- **Info needed:**
  - Who beta clients know (peer EDs, professional networks)
  - Which relationships are strong enough to ask
- **How to gather:**
  - Direct ask in email/call: "Who else do you know that struggles with grant discovery?"
  - Review their LinkedIn connections
- **Action:** Request 1-2 intros per beta client

## 3. High-Leverage Calls (Multiplier Targets)

### 3.1 Foundations That Fund Capacity Building
- **Info needed:**
  - Foundation name, EIN
  - Program officer / contact
  - Evidence of capacity building or "nonprofit support" grantmaking
  - Geographic focus
  - Typical grant size
- **How to gather:**
  - F990 query (same as 1.3)
  - Foundation websites for contact info
- **Pitch:** "Fund TheGrantScout subscriptions for your grantees — multiply your impact"

### 3.2 Grant Writing Firms (Partnership)
- **Info needed:**
  - Firm name, principal contact
  - Number of clients / client types
  - Current service offerings
  - Interest in referral or white-label arrangement
- **How to gather:**
  - Same as 1.2
  - Discovery call to qualify
- **Pitch:** "We handle discovery, you handle writing — referral fee or bundled service"

### 3.3 State Nonprofit Associations
- **Info needed:**
  - Association name, state
  - Membership size
  - Training/webinar program (do they host external speakers?)
  - Contact for partnerships or programming
- **How to gather:**
  - National Council of Nonprofits directory
  - Association websites → "Partner with us" or "Training" pages
- **Pitch:** "Free webinar for your members on finding foundation funding"

### 3.4 Fiscal Sponsors
- **Info needed:**
  - Organization name
  - Number of sponsored projects
  - Geographic/sector focus
  - Contact for partnerships
- **How to gather:**
  - NNFS (National Network of Fiscal Sponsors) directory
  - Web search: "fiscal sponsor [state]"
- **Pitch:** "Offer grant discovery to your sponsored projects — volume discount available"

## 4. Sponsor/Subsidy Angle (B2B Play)

### 4.1 Community Foundations
- **Info needed:**
  - Foundation name, assets, geographic focus
  - Whether they have "nonprofit support" or "capacity building" programs
  - Program officer contact
- **How to gather:**
  - F990 query: Large community foundations by assets
  - Foundation websites for program info
- **Pitch:** "Fund 10 subscriptions for local nonprofits — $12K/year, we handle everything"

### 4.2 Corporate CSR Programs
- **Info needed:**
  - Company name
  - CSR focus areas (nonprofit capacity, community development)
  - Contact / application process
- **How to gather:**
  - Web search: "[Company] nonprofit support program"
  - Target: Tech companies, banks, large employers in your area
- **Pitch:** "Sponsor grant discovery for nonprofits in your community"

### 4.3 Government / SBA Programs
- **Info needed:**
  - Program name
  - Eligibility requirements
  - Whether they fund tools/services for nonprofits
  - Application process
- **How to gather:**
  - Research Team deep-dive on SBDC, state nonprofit infrastructure programs
  - SAM.gov for relevant federal programs
- **Pitch:** "Technical assistance tool for nonprofit capacity building"
