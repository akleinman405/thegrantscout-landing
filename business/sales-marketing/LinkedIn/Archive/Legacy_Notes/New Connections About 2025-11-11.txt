Deb Jarrett is the Founder and CEO of Dharamsala Animal Rescue (DAR), a nonprofit working at the intersection of animal welfare, public health, and social change in northern India. After a 15-year career in technology and business development, Deb felt called to pursue something more meaningful. That search led her to Dharamsala, where a single moment—stopping to help an injured street dog that everyone else ignored—sparked a movement. Since 2009, she has led DAR in transforming the lives of more than 25,000 street dogs through rescue, sterilization, rabies vaccination, and community education. As Deb says, "We can rescue every dog we find, but until the community sees them as worthy of care and love, nothing truly changes." Deb is a TEDx and Machik Global Conference speaker, Consulting Producer of Tails of the Himalayas, and author of The Stray Life, a blog chronicling her years in India. Her work has been featured in CNN, Quartz, and The Better India, and she continues to advocate for both animal and human empowerment through grassroots action.
