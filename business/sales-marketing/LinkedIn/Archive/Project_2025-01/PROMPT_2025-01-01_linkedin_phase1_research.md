## Phase 1 Tasks (After Approval)

### Task 1: Competitor LinkedIn Analysis
Research these competitors from `/mnt/project/COMPETITOR_DATABASE.csv`:

**Priority Competitors (Category A - Automated Grant Matching):**
- Instrumentl (https://www.instrumentl.com)
- GrantWatch (https://www.grantwatch.com)
- OpenGrants (https://opengrants.io)
- Granter.ai (https://granter.ai)

**Secondary Competitors (Category B - Grant Database):**
- Candid/Foundation Directory (https://fconline.foundationcenter.org)
- GrantStation (https://grantstation.com)
- Grantmakers.io (https://www.grantmakers.io)

For each competitor, use web search to find their LinkedIn company page and document:
- **Posting frequency:** How often do they post? (daily, 3x/week, weekly, sporadic)
- **Content types:** What formats do they use? (text-only, carousels, images, videos, polls, articles)
- **Content themes:** What topics do they cover? (product updates, tips, data insights, customer stories, industry news, thought leadership)
- **Engagement patterns:** Which posts get most engagement? What drives comments?
- **Tone/voice:** Professional, casual, educational, promotional?
- **Follower count:** Approximate size of their audience
- **Notable tactics:** Anything unique or effective they're doing

### Task 2: LinkedIn B2B Best Practices
Research 3-5 authoritative sources on:
- Optimal posting frequency for B2B companies
- Best times to post
- Content types that drive engagement
- How to grow a LinkedIn audience from scratch
- B2B lead generation via LinkedIn organic content
- Hashtag strategy
- Algorithm factors (what gets reach)

Sources to consider: LinkedIn's own business blog, HubSpot, Hootsuite, Buffer, Sprout Social, relevant case studies.

### Task 3: Nonprofit/Foundation Sector Patterns
Search for:
- How nonprofit-serving companies use LinkedIn
- What resonates with nonprofit development/fundraising professionals
- Examples of grant-related content that performs well
