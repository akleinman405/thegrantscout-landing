This framework defines TheGrantScout's LinkedIn content strategy for both founder (Aleck) personal profile and company page. Built on Phase 1 competitor research findings, it prioritizes:

1. **Data-driven differentiation** - Leverage 143K foundations + 8.3M grants database

### Pillar 1: Foundation Intelligence (35%)

**Purpose:** Establish TheGrantScout as THE source for foundation giving insights. This is your competitive moat - no competitor regularly publishes database-driven analysis.

**Content Themes:**
- Foundation behavior patterns ("We analyzed X foundations...")
- Geographic giving insights (state-by-state, regional corridors)
- Sector funding breakdowns (healthcare, education, arts, etc.)
- Grant size distribution and trends
- Repeat funding patterns
- "Openness" analysis (which foundations accept applications)
- Surprising/counterintuitive findings

**Example Posts:**

> **Carousel: "5 States That Punch Above Their Weight in Foundation Giving"**
> Slide 1: Hook - "California has the most foundations. But these 5 states give MORE per capita..."
> Slides 2-6: Data for each state with context
> Slide 7: CTA - "Want to find foundations in your state? Link in bio"

> **Text Post: "The 90% Problem"**
> "90% of US foundations don't have websites.
> That means Google can't help you find them.
> But their 990-PF filings are public record.
> We analyzed 143,184 foundations.
> Here's what we learned about the ones nobody talks about..."

> **Poll: "What's the #1 thing you wish you knew about foundations?"**
> - Their actual giving priorities
> - Who makes funding decisions
> - How to get on their radar
> - Grant size ranges

**Formats:** Carousels (primary), Text posts, Polls, Infographics

**Database Queries to Power This Pillar:**
```sql
-- Top states by foundation count
SELECT state, COUNT(*) as foundations, SUM(total_assets_eoy_amt) as total_assets
FROM f990_2025.pf_returns
WHERE tax_year = 2023
GROUP BY state ORDER BY foundations DESC;

-- Foundations open to applications
SELECT COUNT(*) as open_foundations
FROM f990_2025.pf_returns
WHERE only_contri_to_preselected_ind = FALSE
  AND grants_to_organizations_ind = TRUE;

-- Average grant size by sector (via NTEE)
SELECT LEFT(recipient_ntee, 1) as sector,
       AVG(amount) as avg_grant,
       COUNT(*) as grant_count
FROM f990_2025.fact_grants fg
JOIN f990_2025.dim_recipients dr ON fg.recipient_ein = dr.ein
GROUP BY LEFT(recipient_ntee, 1)
ORDER BY avg_grant DESC;
```

## Brand Voice & Tone

### Core Voice Attributes

| Attribute | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Data-driven** | Lead with numbers and evidence | "We analyzed 8M grants. Here's what we found..." |
| **Direct** | Clear, no jargon, get to the point | "Most foundations don't have websites." |
| **Helpful** | Genuinely useful, not salesy | Focus on reader benefit first |
| **Confident** | Authoritative but not arrogant | "Here's what the data shows" vs "I think..." |
| **Human** | Conversational, authentic | Write like talking to a smart friend |

### Tone by Content Type

| Content Type | Tone | Example |
|--------------|------|---------|
| Data insights | Authoritative, curious | "This surprised us..." |
| Tips/how-tos | Helpful, practical | "Here's exactly how to..." |
| Industry commentary | Thoughtful, measured | "Here's what this means..." |
| Founder posts | Personal, vulnerable | "I learned this the hard way..." |
| Customer stories | Celebratory, genuine | "Really proud of [customer]..." |

### Writing Guidelines

**DO:**
- Start with the hook (most interesting finding first)
- Use numbers and specifics
- Write short sentences
- Use line breaks liberally
- End with a clear takeaway
- Ask questions to drive comments

**DON'T:**
- Use corporate jargon ("synergy," "leverage," "optimize")
- Start with "I'm excited to announce..."
- Use excessive exclamation points!!!
- Write walls of text
- Be overtly promotional
- Use engagement bait ("Comment YES if you agree!")

### Sentence Starters to Use

- "We analyzed..."
- "Here's what the data shows..."
- "Most people don't know..."
- "Unpopular opinion:"
- "I used to think... Now I know..."
- "The biggest mistake I see..."
- "After talking to 50+ grant professionals..."

### Sentence Starters to Avoid

- "I'm thrilled to share..."
- "We're excited to announce..."
- "Check out our..."
- "Don't miss..."
- "Tag someone who..."

### Coordination Rules

1. **Founder posts first** - Company page can reshare with additional context 4-24 hours later
2. **Don't duplicate** - If founder covers a topic, company page adds different angle
3. **Company amplifies** - Use company page to boost reach of best founder content
4. **Official announcements** - Product updates, partnerships go on company page first
5. **Personal stories** - Never on company page, always founder profile
