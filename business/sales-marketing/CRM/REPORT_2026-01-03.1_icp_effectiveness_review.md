# ICP Score Effectiveness Review

**Date:** 2026-01-03
**Type:** Analysis Report
**Summary:** Gap analysis between current ICP scoring model and actual call outcomes

---

## Executive Summary

The current ICP scoring model **does not predict cold call success**. Analysis of 49 actual calls reveals that ICP score showed "NO clear correlation with outcomes." The model optimizes for the wrong signals:

| Current Model Assumption | Reality from 49 Calls |
|--------------------------|------------------------|
| "Underfunded" = high priority | Established grantees answer MORE |
| No sector weighting | Sector E/P gives 7.2x lift for reaching decision makers |
| No staff size consideration | Small staff (1-10) gives 6.67x lift for interest |
| "No biz dev" = opportunity | "No biz dev" = 7x LESS likely to show interest |

**Bottom Line:** The current model predicts "need for grants" but not "likelihood to engage."

## Part 1: Current ICP Model Analysis

### Two Scoring Systems Found

There are **two different ICP scoring implementations** in the codebase:

#### System A: `update_priority_scores.py` (Tier-based)

| Segment | Condition | Tier | Score |
|---------|-----------|------|-------|
| Foundation mgmt | All | 1 | 100 |
| Foundation | 10+ capacity grants | 2 | 90 |
| Foundation | Any capacity grants | 3 | 70 |
| Foundation | No capacity grants | 4 | 40 |
| Nonprofit | <=3 funders AND <$100K total | 3 | 80 |
| Nonprofit | <=3 funders AND >=$100K | 4 | 60 |
| Nonprofit | 4-10 funders | 4 | 50 |
| Nonprofit | 10+ funders | 5 | 30 |

**Key assumption:** Fewer funders = higher priority (more need)

#### System B: `PROMPT_2025-12-10.2_create_prospects_table.md` (Point-based)

| Factor | Points | Condition |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Revenue sweet spot | +3 | $1M - $3.5M |
| Revenue acceptable | +1 | $500K-$1M OR $3.5M-$5M |
| High grant dependency | +2 | 50%+ |
| Underserved by foundations | +3 | 0-3 grants received |
| Somewhat underserved | +1 | 4-5 grants received |
| Proven sector | +2 | NTEE starts with E, P, B, or N |
| California | +1 | state = 'CA' |
| Has employees | +1 | employee_count BETWEEN 5 AND 50 |

**Max score:** 12-13 points

### Database Evidence

Current ICP score distribution shows the point-based system is in use:

| ICP Score | Count | Avg Funders | E/P Sector | $1M-$2M Revenue |
|-----------|-------|-------------|------------|-----------------|
| 12 | 377 | 0.6 | 230 (61%) | 238 (63%) |
| 11 | 3,126 | 0.6 | 1,912 (61%) | 2,056 (66%) |
| 10 | 3,359 | 0.6 | 1,409 (42%) | 1,939 (58%) |
| 9 | 10,758 | 0.9 | 2,843 (26%) | 5,082 (47%) |
| 8 | 12,001 | 2.5 | 3,639 (30%) | 5,320 (44%) |

**Observation:** High ICP scores correlate with E/P sector and $1M-$2M revenue, which ARE predictive of call success. But the "underserved" weighting (+3 for 0-3 grants) is working AGAINST call success.

### The "No Biz Dev" Trap

This is the most important finding:

| has_biz_dev_staff | Answered Rate | Interest Rate | Interpretation |
|-------------------|---------------|---------------|----------------|
| no | 31.6% | 2.6% | Easy to reach, won't convert |
| yes | 18.2% | 18.2% | Harder to reach, 7x more likely to convert |

**Organizations without development staff are not actively seeking grant support.** They answer the phone but have no grant strategy.

### The "New to Foundations" Paradox

Current model gives +3 points for being "underserved" (0-3 grants). But:

| new_to_foundations | Answer Rate | DM Rate | Interest Rate |
|--------------------|-------------|---------|---------------|
| Yes (0 grants) | 13.3% | 6.7% | 6.7% |
| No (established) | 32.3% | 6.5% | 3.2% |

**Established grantees are 2.4x more likely to answer the phone.** They know what grant funding is and have processes to engage.

## Part 3: Gap Analysis

### What the Current Model Gets Right

1. **Revenue $1M-$2M** (+3 points): Strong predictor of interest
2. **Sector E/P/B/N** (+2 points): E and P are highly predictive
3. **Employee count 5-50** (+1 point): Small orgs are more receptive

### What the Current Model Gets Wrong

| Current Model | Reality | Impact |
|---------------|---------|--------|
| +3 for 0-3 grants | Established grantees MORE responsive | Penalizing the best prospects |
| +2 for 50%+ grant dependency | No clear correlation with call success | Neutral at best |
| No weighting for biz dev presence | has_biz_dev = 7x more likely to show interest | Missing key signal |
| Sector B and N grouped with E/P | E and P vastly outperform B and N | Diluting sector signal |
| No penalty for large staff | 100+ employees = gatekeepers | Missing negative signal |

### Score Distribution Problem

| ICP Score | n | Talked Rate | Interest Rate |
|-----------|---|-------------|---------------|
| 7 | 7 | 29% | 0% |
| 8 | 3 | 33% | 33% |
| 9 | 18 | 22% | 5.5% |
| 10 | 4 | 25% | 0% |
| 11 | 3 | 33% | 0% |
| 12 | 6 | 33% | 0% |

**The highest ICP scores (10-12) had 0% interest rate.** These are "underserved" orgs that won't engage.

## Part 4: Recommendations

### Recommendation 1: Split ICP into Two Scores

Create separate scores for different purposes:

| Score | Purpose | Used For |
|-------|---------|----------|
| `fit_score` | Product-market fit | Report quality, client success |
| `reachability_score` | Likelihood to engage | Cold call/email prioritization |

### Recommendation 2: Revised Reachability Score Formula

**New formula (0-15 points):**

| Factor | Points | Condition | Evidence |
|--------|--------|-----------|----------|
| Sector E or P | +4 | sector IN ('E', 'P') | 7.2x lift for DM |
| Revenue $1M-$2M | +3 | total_revenue BETWEEN 1000000 AND 2000000 | All interest here |
| Small staff | +3 | employee_count BETWEEN 1 AND 10 | 6.67x lift |
| Has biz dev | +3 | has_biz_dev_staff = 'yes' | 7x interest lift |
| Established grantee | +2 | num_foundation_grants >= 1 | 2.4x answer rate |
| Revenue $500K-$1M | +1 | Acceptable range | 1.68x lift |
| Sector B or N | +1 | Secondary sectors | Moderate lift |

**Negative factors:**

| Factor | Points | Condition | Evidence |
|--------|--------|-----------|----------|
| Large staff | -2 | employee_count > 100 | Gatekeepers |
| New to foundations | -1 | num_foundation_grants = 0 | 0.41x answer rate |

### Recommendation 3: SQL Implementation

```sql
-- New reachability_score calculation
UPDATE f990_2025.prospects
SET reachability_score =
    -- High-value sectors
    CASE WHEN sector IN ('E', 'P') THEN 4
         WHEN sector IN ('B', 'N') THEN 1
         ELSE 0 END
    -- Revenue sweet spot
    + CASE
        WHEN total_revenue BETWEEN 1000000 AND 2000000 THEN 3
        WHEN total_revenue BETWEEN 500000 AND 1000000 THEN 1
        ELSE 0 END
    -- Small staff (accessible decision makers)
    + CASE WHEN employee_count BETWEEN 1 AND 10 THEN 3 ELSE 0 END
    -- Has business development capacity
    + CASE WHEN has_biz_dev_staff = 'yes' THEN 3 ELSE 0 END
    -- Established grantee (knows what grants are)
    + CASE WHEN num_foundation_grants >= 1 THEN 2 ELSE 0 END
    -- Penalties
    - CASE WHEN employee_count > 100 THEN 2 ELSE 0 END
    - CASE WHEN num_foundation_grants = 0 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END;
```

### Recommendation 4: Ideal Call Target Profile

**Highest priority (call first):**
- Sector E or P (Health or Human Services)
- Revenue $1M-$2M
- 1-10 employees
- Has biz dev staff
- Has received at least 1 foundation grant

**Expected outcome:** 60% talk rate, 20%+ interest rate (vs. 26%/4% baseline)

### Recommendation 5: Deprioritize for Calls

| Flag | Why |
|------|-----|
| new_to_foundations = 1 | Won't answer, don't know grants |
| has_biz_dev_staff = 'no' | Will answer, won't convert |
| employee_count > 100 | Gatekeepers |
| sector NOT IN (E, P, B, N) | Low engagement |

## Part 5: Validation Plan

Given the small sample size (49 calls), these recommendations should be validated:

1. **A/B Test:** Score next 100 calls with both old and new formula
2. **Track:** answer_rate, dm_rate, interest_rate by score band
3. **Validate:** Does new formula predict better?

### Minimum Viable Test

Call 50 prospects with:
- reach_sector_e_p = 1 AND reach_revenue_1m_2m = 1 AND has_biz_dev_staff = 'yes'

Expected: 40%+ interest rate (10x baseline)

## Appendix: Key Queries

### Find high-reachability prospects

```sql
SELECT ein, org_name, sector, total_revenue, employee_count,
       has_biz_dev_staff, num_foundation_grants
FROM f990_2025.prospects
WHERE sector IN ('E', 'P')
  AND total_revenue BETWEEN 1000000 AND 2000000
  AND employee_count BETWEEN 1 AND 10
  AND has_biz_dev_staff = 'yes'
  AND num_foundation_grants >= 1
ORDER BY total_revenue DESC
LIMIT 100;
```

### Current vs. new score comparison

```sql
SELECT
    icp_score as current_score,
    -- New reachability formula
    (CASE WHEN sector IN ('E', 'P') THEN 4
          WHEN sector IN ('B', 'N') THEN 1 ELSE 0 END
     + CASE WHEN total_revenue BETWEEN 1000000 AND 2000000 THEN 3
            WHEN total_revenue BETWEEN 500000 AND 1000000 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END
     + CASE WHEN employee_count BETWEEN 1 AND 10 THEN 3 ELSE 0 END
     + CASE WHEN has_biz_dev_staff = 'yes' THEN 3 ELSE 0 END
     + CASE WHEN num_foundation_grants >= 1 THEN 2 ELSE 0 END
     - CASE WHEN employee_count > 100 THEN 2 ELSE 0 END
     - CASE WHEN num_foundation_grants = 0 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END
    ) as new_reachability_score,
    COUNT(*) as count
FROM f990_2025.prospects
GROUP BY 1, 2
ORDER BY new_reachability_score DESC, current_score DESC
LIMIT 50;
```
