## If You Get Stuck

**Common questions:**

**Q: "Which company should I call first?"**
A: Foundation Group (Roschelle Ridenhour). Easiest decision-maker to reach, clearest pitch, most likely to be receptive.

**Q: "What if they say 'we're not interested in new vendors'?"**
A: Ask "Is there a specific reason, or just not the right time?" Listen for real objection vs. polite deflection. If real, ask for referral.

**Q: "How long should I wait between follow-ups?"**
A: 5 days after first call/email. If no response, second follow-up at 10 days. Then pause for 60 days before trying again.

**Q: "What if they want a price quote before meeting?"**
A: "Happy to discuss pricing after we validate this would be valuable for your segment. Can we schedule a 15-minute call first?"

**Q: "Who do I contact if I get transferred?"**
A: Take the transfer gracefully, but if not the right person, ask "Who should I really be talking to about [specific decision type]?" and get back to your target.

## Important Reminders

- **Personalize every call:** Use their company specifics, not generic pitch
- **Lead with outcome, not features:** "Save 10 hours per nonprofit" not "8.3M grants analyzed"
- **Get to decision-maker early:** Don't waste time with gatekeepers
- **Ask permission before pitching:** "Worth 15 minutes?" before diving in
- **Have a specific ask:** Not "let's chat" but "can we schedule a demo Thursday?"
- **Follow up systematically:** 5-day, 10-day, 60-day cadence
- **Track everything:** Use call tracker template to document learnings
