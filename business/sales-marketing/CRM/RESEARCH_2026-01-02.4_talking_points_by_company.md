## 1. FOUNDATION SOURCE
**foundationsource.com | 2,300 foundations, $40B AUM**

### Opening Frame
"You help foundations manage their operations and grantmaking. We help them make smarter grantmaking decisions by finding better nonprofits to fund. Thought this might be interesting given your client base."

### Research Statement
"We analyzed 8.3 million grants from IRS 990 data (2016-2024) using semantic matching to help foundations discover nonprofits they wouldn't find through traditional searches. Initial customers are getting 5-10x better nonprofit matches than random selection."

### Pain Point to Explore
**Opener:** "Your foundation clients probably get a lot of grant applications. Are they usually a good fit?"

**Listen for:**
- "We get a lot of poor matches"
- "It's hard for nonprofits to find us"
- "Application quality varies widely"
- "We wish we knew about more nonprofits in our sector"

### Your Value Prop
"Here's what we've found: Nonprofits do terrible foundation research. Most use Candid or Google. Your foundation clients could get better applicants if those nonprofits had better research. We can help on that side."

### The Offer
1. **For their foundation clients:** "Use TheGrantScout to find nonprofits worthy of funding in your sector"
   - Data: 8.3M grants, semantic matching finds non-obvious fits
   - ROI: Better applications, more strategic grantmaking

2. **For their nonprofit clients:** "Use TheGrantScout to find foundations likely to fund you"
   - Data: Historical grant patterns, foundation capacity, giving preferences
   - ROI: Higher success rate, less research time

3. **For Foundation Source:** Commission on referrals or bulk pricing for your clients

### Example Use Case
"We worked with [nonprofit doing education work]. They found 45 foundations they'd never heard of via TheGrantScout. They applied to 5, got funded by 2. Cost: $100/month. Traditional grant consultant: $3K-5K/month."

### If They Push Back
**"We already have Candid"** → "Candid is great for published data. We add semantic matching—finding foundations that fund similar work, even if not explicitly listed."

**"Our clients do their own research"** → "Some do. Many don't. And even sophisticated users like having a data layer to validate their instincts. It's like Bloomberg for grants."

**"How do we make money on this?"** → "Commission per referral, or we white-label it as 'Foundation Source Grant Research' for your clients. Your client gets value, you deepen the relationship, we grow."

## 2. ROCKEFELLER PHILANTHROPY ADVISORS (RPA)
**rockpa.org | $500M+ advisory AUM, $4B grantmaking facilitated**

### Opening Frame
"You help philanthropists design giving strategies. We help them execute those strategies by identifying vetted nonprofits in their chosen sectors. We think our data could be useful in your strategic engagements."

### Research Statement
"We've built a dataset of foundation giving patterns over 9 years (8.3M grants). This lets us show philanthropic clients: 'Here's the nonprofit ecosystem in [sector/geography]. Here's who's actually getting funded. Here's the white space.' It validates or challenges their strategy hypotheses."

### Pain Point to Explore
**Opener:** "When you're designing a philanthropic strategy with a client—say they want to fund education in Texas—how much research do you do on the nonprofit landscape?"

**Listen for:**
- "We do a lot of custom research"
- "It's one of the most time-consuming parts"
- "We wish we had better data on what's working"
- "Our team spends weeks on this"

### Your Value Prop
"Our data could save your team weeks on every engagement. Instead of custom research, you can show clients: 'Here are 200 education nonprofits in Texas getting foundation funding. Here's their funding trajectory. Here's who's funding them.' Then your team can do the qualitative work—vetting, relationship-building, impact analysis."

### The Offer
1. **Data partnership for engagements:** License our data for your client engagements
2. **White-label research:** Branded as "RPA Strategic Foundation Research"
3. **Per-engagement licensing:** Fee per strategy engagement

### Example Use Case
"Client comes to RPA wanting to fund healthcare innovation in the Southeast. You show them: 'We found 89 nonprofits in this space getting foundation funding. Here's the funding landscape, the gaps, the hot players.' That validates your strategy recommendation and saves you 2 weeks of research."

### If They Push Back
**"We have our own research"** → "Perfect. Our data could complement it. Some partners use our dataset to validate their recommendations or find emerging players they might have missed."

**"Our clients are sophisticated; they know their space"** → "Absolutely. And even sophisticated clients benefit from seeing the data. It's the difference between thinking you know your sector and seeing the actual funding flows."

**"This seems like tool licensing, not strategy"** → "It is. Think of it as strategy infrastructure. The data layer lets your strategists focus on judgment and relationships, not data gathering."

## Universal Phrases (All Companies)

### If they ask "Why us?":
"You already have the relationships with the foundations/donors. They trust you. You recommend tools. We just want to be on that list because we solve a real problem: nonprofit research and matching. It's a natural fit."

### If they ask "What's your business model?":
"We charge nonprofits $100/month for grant research. When you recommend us, they become customers. We can also do white-label partnerships or affiliate arrangements."

### If they ask "How mature is the product?":
"Early stage but proven. We've been testing with beta nonprofits and getting strong engagement. The technology is solid (semantic matching on 8.3M grants). We're at the stage where we need partner distribution."

### If they ask "Who's your competition?":
"Candid (Foundation Directory Online) is traditional database. GrantStation is nonprofit-focused. We're different because we use AI matching to find non-obvious fits. Think of us as 'Bloomberg for grants' vs. 'telephone directory.'"

### If they ask "Why should we care?":
"Because your clients have problems we solve. Foundations want better nonprofit applicants. Nonprofits want to find foundations faster. You want to deepen your relationships with both. We help on all three fronts."

## Red Flag Responses & How to Handle

| Response | What It Means | Your Reply |
|----------|---------------|-----------|
| "We'll look into it" (vague) | Low interest, polite brush-off | Push gently: "Who would be good to continue the conversation with?" Get specific next step. |
| "Send us a demo" (before real conversation) | Looking to evaluate but not committed | Better: "Before I do, can I ask—how would you actually use something like this with your clients?" Get clear use case first. |
| "We have in-house research" | Potentially competitive threat | Pivot: "What areas would you like to outsource to focus your team on strategy?" Find collaboration angle. |
| "Too early for us" | Might be real, might be brush-off | Follow up in 6 months. Ask: "What would need to be true for this to make sense?" Set trigger for next conversation. |
| "We're building our own" | Real competitive threat | Ask questions: Timeline? Why build vs. partner? See if you can influence toward partnership. |
