### Whittier Trust

- **Scale:** Smaller than Bessemer, more specialized
- **Approach:** Family office model with philanthropy specialization

## Sales Strategy by Segment

### Segment 1: Foundation Administration (Foundation Source)
**Opportunity:** Recommend to 2,300+ foundation clients + 1,800+ nonprofit clients
**Pain:** Foundations want better applicant quality; nonprofits want research
**Approach:** B2B2C referral model
- Foundation Source → "Use TheGrantScout to find the best nonprofits"
- Foundation Source → "Recommend to your nonprofit grantees for research"
**Likelihood:** High (clear pain point, easy fit)
**Deal Structure:** Affiliate, referral fees, or bulk discount for their clients

### Segment 2: Strategy Advisory (RPA, TPI, Bridgespan)
**Opportunity:** Embed research in consulting engagements
**Pain:** Strategy consulting requires sector/nonprofit research
**Approach:** Partnership + white-label
- "Here's TheGrantScout data validating your sector choice"
- "TheGrantScout shows which nonprofits are attracting funding in your focus area"
**Likelihood:** Medium-High (good fit but requires buy-in from partners)
**Deal Structure:** White-label, data licensing, or affiliate per engagement

### Segment 3: Wealth Managers (Bessemer, Whittier)
**Opportunity:** Add nonprofit research to philanthropic planning
**Pain:** Wealthy clients want research on nonprofits before major gifts
**Approach:** Client value-add + white-label
- "Bessemer Philanthropy Intelligence" powered by TheGrantScout
- Recommend to clients creating foundations or making major gifts
**Likelihood:** Medium (good fit but different business model than advisory)
**Deal Structure:** White-label, bundled offering, or affiliate per recommendation

## Competitive Threats

### Existing Grant Databases
- **Candid (Foundation Directory Online)** - Aggregates foundation/grant data, but lacks semantic matching
- **GrantStation** - Nonprofit-focused grant finding, but relies on published data
- **ProPublica Nonprofit Explorer** - IRS 990 data, but not optimized for matching

**Advantage:** TheGrantScout uses semantic matching (NLP) to find non-obvious matches. Competitors are keyword-based.

### Potential Internal Solutions
- **RPA/TPI/Bridgespan** could build internal research capability
- **Foundation Source** could add grant research module to their platform
- **Wealth managers** could hire researchers

**Mitigation:** Move fast to establish relationships before they build in-house.

### Emerging Competitors
- AI-powered grant matching (likely coming)
- Nonprofit platforms (e.g., Donorbox) adding foundation matching
- ChatGPT plugins for grant research

**Advantage:** Legitimate business relationship with decision-makers = sticky.

## Collaboration vs. Competition Risk

### Low Risk (Collaboration Likely)
- Offering is complementary (they advise, we research)
- No direct revenue overlap
- Solves their client's pain point
- Increases stickiness of their offering

### Medium Risk (Competition Possible)
- They could build in-house or acquire competitor
- They might see nonprofit research as core to their value prop
- Large consulting firms often prefer proprietary tools

### Mitigation
- **Move fast:** Establish relationships within 6 months
- **White-label:** Offer branded solution to reduce threat
- **Integration:** Embed in their existing platforms (hard to unwind)
- **Switching costs:** Make it easy to use but hard to leave

## Potential Partnership Models

### Model 1: Affiliate/Referral
- They recommend TheGrantScout to their clients
- We pay commission per new customer
- **Pros:** Low friction, no tech integration needed
- **Cons:** Low conversion if not embedded

### Model 2: White-Label
- We provide data/interface, they brand as their own
- **Pros:** High stickiness, they promote as their offering
- **Cons:** Requires tech integration, lower visibility for brand

### Model 3: Data Licensing
- They license our grant/nonprofit dataset
- Integrate into their own platform
- **Pros:** Scalable, high-value partnership
- **Cons:** Requires deep integration, might cannibalize direct sales

### Model 4: Consulting Partnership
- They recommend we do custom research for their clients
- Or they embed our data in their consulting work
- **Pros:** High-value bundles, strengthens their offering
- **Cons:** Custom work is time-intensive

### Model 5: Integration Partnership
- We integrate with their platforms/APIs
- Their clients use TheGrantScout within their workflow
- **Pros:** Highest stickiness, hard to unwind
- **Cons:** Most technically complex

By selling to them, we don't just reach their customers—we become a trusted part of their practice. This creates:

The goal isn't just to make these sales. It's to become *essential* to how they serve their clients.
