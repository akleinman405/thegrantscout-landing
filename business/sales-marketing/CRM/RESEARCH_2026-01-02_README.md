## How to Use This Package

### Before Week 1 Calls:
1. Read the main report (RESEARCH_2026-01-02.1) completely
2. Skim the competitive landscape (RESEARCH_2026-01-02.3)
3. Keep the quick reference card (RESEARCH_2026-01-02.2) on desk

### Before Each Individual Call:
1. Review quick reference card (2 minutes)
2. Read that company's section in talking points doc (5 minutes)
3. Review pain points and pitch angle from main report (3 minutes)
4. Look up recent news about the company (5 minutes)
5. Find 2nd-degree LinkedIn connections if possible (5 minutes)

### During Each Call:
1. Have quick reference card visible
2. Have talking points doc nearby
3. Use opening frame to start
4. Ask exploration questions
5. Listen for pain points, then pivot to value prop

### After Each Call:
1. Note what worked/didn't work
2. Update your mental model of each company
3. Track pain points mentioned (good for later meetings)
4. Send customized follow-up based on their reaction

## Key Insights from This Research

1. **Nobody is doing nonprofit research** - This is a massive gap. All 6 companies would benefit from this.

2. **Two different sales angles:**
   - **B2C through them:** Foundation Source, Whittier, Bessemer (recommend to clients)
   - **B2B:** RPA, TPI, Bridgespan (embed in their engagements)

3. **Three customer types they serve:**
   - **Foundations** (want to find good nonprofits)
   - **Nonprofits** (want to find good foundations)
   - **Wealthy families/individuals** (want to give strategically)

4. **Partnership models that work:**
   - Affiliate (they recommend, we pay commission)
   - White-label (we provide data/UI, they brand it)
   - Data licensing (they license our data)
   - Consulting partnership (we do custom research)

5. **Biggest opportunity:** Foundation Source + Whittier + Bessemer (referred distribution at scale)

## Things to Watch Out For

- **RPA is in leadership transition (March 2025)** - May be distracted, could be opportunity
- **Bridgespan advises both nonprofits AND foundations** - Unique opportunity but also unique complexity
- **Wealth managers (Bessemer/Whittier) think differently than advisors** - They care about client value-add, not strategy validation
- **Foundation Source has 15,000+ advisors in their network** - They might distribute wider than their own clients
- **Competitive threats exist** (Candid, GrantStation) but ours is differentiated through AI matching
