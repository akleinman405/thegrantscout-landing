## 1. SF Challenge / America One (943242538)

### Background & Mission

The **San Francisco Challenge (DBA AmericaOne)** was incorporated in 1996 as the entity behind the **AmericaOne America's Cup syndicate**. Paul Cayard's team reached the finals of the 2000 Louis Vuitton Cup, losing 5-4 to Prada. After the Cup campaign, Doug Smith negotiated the sale of physical assets to Larry Ellison's Oracle Racing. The proceeds funded the AmericaOne Foundation, which pivoted to philanthropy supporting Olympic sailing, adaptive/disabled sailing, and youth sailing access.

**Key transition (2014):** After COO Bob Billingham's death, Paul Cayard took over as Managing Director. The foundation launched **Project Pipeline** — a $5M, 10-year commitment to US Sailing Olympic development.

**Second transition (2023):** AmericaOne shifted from pure grantmaking back into a **racing team (A1R)**, directly developing Olympic athletes through coaching and sports science. Community grants (BAADS, TISC, Team Paradise) have been maintained as legacy commitments.

**Current address:** 127 University Ave, Berkeley, CA 94710

## 2. 1687 Foundation (263772474)

### What the 1687 Foundation Actually Is

This is a **private operating foundation** (per IRS classification) that primarily **conducts its own programs** rather than functioning as a traditional grantmaker. $162M in assets, $28M in revenue, $8.7M in charitable disbursements (2024).

**Founded after the 1995 Oklahoma City bombing** by Danny "Woody" Wood (now deceased) and Patti Jo Peck Wood. Based in McKinney/Plano, TX. Self-funding — does not accept outside donations. 0 employees listed.

### Three Core Programs (operated directly)

1. **Book Ministry** — 20M+ Christian books distributed free to nonprofits serving incarcerated, military, first responders, homeless, foster care. ~1M books/year from 16,000 sq ft warehouse in Sisters, OR.
2. **Retreat Program** — Free ranch accommodations near Abilene, TX for 501(c)(3)s serving veterans, first responders, foster/adoption families. **Booked solid — not currently accepting new applications.**
3. **Hunting & Fishing Ministry** — Outdoor healing events at the ranch for disabled children, veterans, terminally ill children.

- No "/apply-for-grant" or "/funding" page exists on the website

- Retreat Program is booked solid and "not reviewing applications"
- The Shareway lists "no active grants currently"

**Organizational grants ($100K–$580K) are board-directed and relationship-based:**
