Important: Private Foundation Status Advisory

VetsBoats is classified as a private foundation (IRS BMF foundation code 03, filing Form 990-PF). This is different from a public charity (501(c)(3) with public support), and it affects grant eligibility.

What this means for your applications:

  • Some foundations restrict grants to "public charities" or "501(c)(3) organizations that are not private foundations." Always check guidelines before applying.
  • All 5 foundations in this report have been verified as either having funded other private foundations before OR having no stated restrictions against it.
  • When applying, include your IRS determination letter and be transparent about your classification.

Recommended actions:

  1. Obtain a copy of your current IRS determination letter
  2. Consult with your tax advisor about the operating foundation checkbox on your 990-PF (marked FALSE since 2019, if this is a filing error, correcting it strengthens your position)
  3. If asked about your status, emphasize that VetsBoats operates direct programs (therapeutic sailing, boat restoration) rather than making grants to other organizations

Foundation Types Explained

Open Application

What it means: Foundation has a submission process (online portal, email, specific forms). You submit your request during their open window.

Your action: Complete and submit application this month.

LOI-Ready

What it means: Foundation accepts unsolicited Letters of Inquiry (2-3 page summary of your org and request). If they're interested, they'll invite you to submit a full proposal.

Your action: Write and submit LOI this month.

Cultivation

What it means: Foundation is invitation-only or rarely funds without existing relationship. Cold applications have very low success rates.

Your action: Research staff/board, identify connections, begin relationship-building. Application comes later (months to years).

Note: Cultivation foundations are only included in this report when there is:

  • Near-perfect alignment with your mission, geography, and funding needs, AND
  • A clear path to relationship (you have a connection to their board/staff, they host accessible events, or a shared grantee can introduce you)

Understanding the Timeline

Typical Grant Process (3-9 months)

  • Month 1: Research + Submit LOI/Application
  • Months 2-3: Wait for response (6-8 weeks typical)
  • Months 3-4: If invited, write full proposal (10-20 hours)
  • Months 4-6: Wait for review + board decision
  • Months 6-9: Decision received

This report covers Month 1. Expect decisions in 4-9 months for the application-ready foundations (#1-3, #5). Barry (#4) is a cultivation foundation operating on a 6-18 month timeline. Note that Barry and Van Beuren (#5) share the Sail to Prevail connection, building that relationship strengthens both paths.
