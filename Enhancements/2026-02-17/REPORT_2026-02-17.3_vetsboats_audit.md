## Executive Summary

Matt Goettelman reported that the Bill Simpson Foundation email (`hello@billsimpsonfoundation.org`) in his February 2026 report was bouncing. Investigation revealed **25 total data accuracy and contact information errors** across all 5 foundations in the report.

**Three-layer audit performed:**

- Van Beuren had the most errors (8 total), including a completely reversed funding trend ("declining" was actually growing).
- Kovler PF-to-PF grant count was inflated 7x (41 reported vs 6 actual).
- All corrections applied. Report, .docx, and .pdf regenerated.

## Audit Methodology

### Layer 1: Grant Data Cross-Check
- SQL queries against `f990_2025.fact_grants` for every specific number cited in the report
- PF-to-PF grant counts verified by joining `fact_grants` with `dim_foundations`/`pf_returns`
- Repeat funding rates computed from actual recipient frequency
- Geographic distributions computed from recipient state fields
- Funding trends computed from tax_year aggregation

### Layer 2: Contact Verification
- `dig MX` on all email domains (5 domains checked)
- HTTP status check on all URLs (6 URLs checked)
- Web research on foundation websites for current staff/officers
- LinkedIn and third-party verification of key contacts
- 3 parallel research agents for Bill Simpson + Herzstein, Kovler + Barry, Van Beuren

### Layer 3: Opportunity Verification
- Foundation websites checked for current application processes
- Deadlines verified against website and third-party sources (Instrumentl, GrantStation)
- Application portals tested for liveness
- First-time applicant requirements confirmed

## Notes

### What Went Right
- Three-layer audit caught errors that a single-layer review would miss
- Parallel research agents (3 simultaneous) made contact verification fast
- Bill Simpson Foundation is confirmed as a strong, active match despite the email issue

### What Went Wrong in the Original Report
- PF-to-PF grant counts were likely hallucinated during enrichment (not verified against DB)
- Repeat funding rates were estimated rather than computed
- Van Beuren "declining" trend was a misinterpretation
- Contact info was pulled from 990-PF filings but not cross-checked against current websites
- PSEC market size was taken from an outdated source

### Recommendations
1. **Build `09_audit_report.py`** to automate SQL verification of every cited number
2. **Add contact verification step** to `SOP_report_generation.md` (MX check + website staff check)
3. **Cross-reference 990-PF with foundation websites** when they conflict (website is more current)
4. **Never cite PF-to-PF counts without SQL verification** - this was the most consequential error class
