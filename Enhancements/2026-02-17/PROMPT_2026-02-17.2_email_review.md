# EMAIL QUALITY CONTROL REVIEW PROMPT

**Date:** 2026-02-17
**Version:** 2.0
**Source:** REPORT_2026-02-17.1_email_prompt_redesign.md (Section 9)
**Purpose:** Run after email generation, before sending. Catches problems that would destroy credibility, trigger spam filters, or make the sender look unprofessional.

---

## Instructions

You are reviewing a batch of cold emails before they are sent to real nonprofit
prospects. Your job is to catch problems that would destroy credibility, trigger
spam filters, or make the sender look unprofessional.

For each email, evaluate against this checklist:

## Hard Reject Criteria (any one = email cannot be sent, must be rewritten)

- [ ] Foundation cited has `assets > $5 billion`
- [ ] Foundation cited has `mega_penalty = 0.50` (on the exclusion list or $1B+ assets)
- [ ] Foundation's `geo_tier` is "incidental" for the prospect's state
- [ ] Foundation's `state_pct < 0.05` for the prospect's state
- [ ] Example grant recipient does recognizably different work than the prospect
- [ ] Example described as "similar work" but `example_match_tier` is 3 or 4
- [ ] Contact first name does not match the email address (check `email_quality_flags`)
- [ ] Subject line sector does not match body sector
- [ ] Dollar amount is fabricated or rounded beyond recognition
- [ ] Foundation is obviously invitation-only (Ford, Mellon, Gates, MacArthur, Hewlett, Packard)
- [ ] Email contains any phrase from the banned phrases list
- [ ] Email contains "for free," "free list," or "leads"
- [ ] CAN-SPAM elements missing (no signature with company name and opt-out)

## Soft Flags (accumulate: 3+ flags = email needs revision)

- [ ] Opening line uses generic crisis/uncertainty language without org specificity
- [ ] Same opening strategy as 3+ other emails in the batch
- [ ] Same foundation presentation format as 3+ other emails in the batch
- [ ] Same CTA as 4+ other emails in the batch
- [ ] Both foundations are in the top 3 by `foundation_rank` for that cohort
- [ ] Grant amounts cited would be >5x the prospect's total revenue
- [ ] Email is under 40 words or over 90 words
- [ ] "We" used instead of "I" in the body (body should use personal voice)
- [ ] Transition phrase ("worth a look," "caught my eye," etc.) used more than once in the batch
- [ ] `display_text` not used correctly (wrong foundation count cited)

## Template Detection Test

Read all emails in the batch back-to-back. Answer:
- Can you identify the template by email 3? If yes, the batch fails.
- If you covered the prospect-specific details, would any two emails be
  interchangeable? If yes for more than 2 pairs, the batch fails.

## Scoring (per email)

- **5/5:** Would send without changes. Specific, accurate, varied, credible.
- **4/5:** Minor wording tweaks needed. Foundations and tone are solid.
- **3/5:** Needs one rewrite (usually the opening or the foundation selection).
- **2/5:** Significant problems. Would not send without major revision.
- **1/5:** Hard reject. Foundation mismatch, factual error, or data quality issue.

**Quality floor:** No email below 4/5 gets sent. Emails scoring 3/5 get one revision
attempt. Emails scoring 1-2/5 get fully rewritten or removed from the batch.

## Output Per Email

For each email, produce:
- **Score** (1-5)
- **Hard reject flags** (if any, with specific rule violated)
- **Soft flags** (if any)
- **Specific revision suggestion** (if score < 4)

## Batch-Level Output

- Template detection test result (PASS/FAIL)
- Distribution of opening strategies used (Strategy 1-7 with counts)
- Distribution of foundation presentation formats used (A/B/C/D with counts)
- Distribution of CTAs used (with counts)
- Paragraph count distribution
- Any banned phrases detected (with email number)
- Emails recommended for manual human review (with reason)

## Batch-Level Distribution Checks

These are soft requirements. Flag but do not reject:
- Opening strategies: at least 4 different strategies used
- Foundation formats: at least 3 different formats used
- CTAs: at least 3 different CTAs used
- Paragraph counts: at least 2 different counts used
- No single strategy/format/CTA used more than 40% of the time

## Data Accuracy Spot-Checks

For 5 randomly selected emails in the batch:
1. Verify the foundation name matches a real foundation (check against `cohort_foundation_lists_v2`)
2. Verify the dollar amount cited matches `total_giving_5yr` or `median_grant` or `example_grant_amount`
3. Verify the example recipient name matches `example_recipient_name` in the data
4. Verify the state referenced matches the prospect's `state`
5. Verify the sector term used matches `sector_label`

Report any discrepancies as hard reject flags.
