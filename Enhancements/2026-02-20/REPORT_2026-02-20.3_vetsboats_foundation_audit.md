## Background

VetsBoats (EIN 464194065) is a **private foundation** that files 990-PF returns. This is the single most important eligibility fact for funder matching, because many institutional funders restrict grants to public charities classified under Section 509(a) of the Internal Revenue Code.

Two deliverables were produced for VetsBoats:

1. **Grant Report** (2026-02-06, updated 2026-02-18): 5 foundation recommendations with detailed profiles
2. **Outreach Contacts** (2026-02-18): 5 foundation contacts for cold outreach

Matt performed manual verification of all 10 foundations by visiting their websites and copying eligibility language into `5. Runs/VetsBoats/2026-02-18/manual verification.md`.

## Root Cause Analysis

### Why did the pipeline recommend these foundations?

The pipeline's LASSO V6.1 scoring algorithm matches foundations to clients based on **grant history patterns**: sector overlap, geographic overlap, grant size, foundation activity level. This data comes from IRS 990-PF filings and is accurate. These foundations genuinely DO fund veterans, sailing, and adaptive recreation programs.

### What the pipeline does NOT check

| Missing Check | Impact | Foundations Affected |
|---------------|--------|---------------------|
| Private foundation exclusion language on website | 4 foundations explicitly exclude PFs | Rees-Jones, Autzen, Heinz, Barker |
| Public charity requirement (509(a)) | Same 4 plus Bill Simpson | Bill Simpson, Rees-Jones, Autzen, Heinz, Barker |
| Geographic restriction specificity | "Pacific Northwest" vs "Oregon only" | Van Beuren, Rees-Jones, Autzen, Heinz, Barker |
| Email/website currency | Stale forms, dead mailboxes | Bill Simpson |

### The fundamental problem

The 990-PF data shows **who a foundation has funded**, not **who a foundation will accept applications from**. A foundation in Pittsburgh that funds veterans in southwestern PA will match VetsBoats on sector overlap, but the eligibility page says "southwestern PA only." The grant history is real. The eligibility restriction is also real. The pipeline only sees the first.

### For VetsBoats specifically

VetsBoats faces a unique challenge: as a **private foundation** (not a public charity), it is categorically excluded by any funder that requires Section 509(a) classification. This is a common requirement among larger institutional funders, and it hit 5 of our 10 recommendations.
