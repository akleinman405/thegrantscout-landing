## Executive Summary

On February 6, 2026, we built VetsBoats' first monthly grant opportunity report entirely by hand across 3 Claude Code CLI sessions, 6 review checkpoints, and roughly 10 hours of work. The automated pipeline (`process_client.py`) could not run because VetsBoats lacked all three prerequisites: embedding tables (archived), a `nonprofit_returns` record (VetsBoats files 990-PF only), and a completed client questionnaire.

Every pipeline phase was replicated manually via SQL queries, Python scripts, web research agents, and human review pauses. The final deliverable was a 33-page PDF profiling 5 foundations with a combined potential funding range of $25,000-$200,000, plus a 17-foundation prospect pipeline for future months.

The report went through two rounds of quality review (a 44-test scoring rubric), scoring 70.66/100 on the first pass (FAIL, with 1 critical error and 8 high-severity errors) and 93.57/100 on the second pass (PASS). A final formatting session fixed approximately 90 rendering issues before PDF delivery.

## Why the Report Was Built Manually

The standard pipeline (`4. Pipeline/process_client.py`) automates the full workflow from client profile to formatted report. It requires three things VetsBoats didn't have:

1. **Embedding tables (archived Jan 4, 2026).** The `emb_nonprofit_missions` and related embedding tables were dropped to free 52GB of disk space. Without embeddings, the pipeline can't compute semantic similarity between VetsBoats' mission and grant purposes.

2. **A `nonprofit_returns` record.** VetsBoats files Form 990-PF (private foundation return), not Form 990 or 990-EZ. The pipeline's client loading step expects to find the organization in `nonprofit_returns`, which only contains 990/990-EZ filers.

3. **A completed client questionnaire.** The standard workflow begins with a structured questionnaire that captures mission, programs, geographic scope, budget, and known funders. VetsBoats had not completed one. The client profile had to be reconstructed from a sales call transcript, a pre-call research brief, and online verification.

Additionally, a critical constraint discovered mid-process made automation even less viable: **VetsBoats is a private foundation**, not a public charity. The standard scoring model has no feature for "PF-to-PF eligibility." Most funders either explicitly exclude private foundations or have never funded one. This constraint required manual judgment at every enrichment and selection step.

**Goal:** Establish a verified, complete client record in the database.

**What happened:**

Three funders from the pre-call brief were wrong.

Found Matt Goettelman's correct title (Advisory Board Member, not Director of Development as stated in pre-call brief), confirmed current financials (FY2024 revenue: $136,943), and updated the expansion city list.

5. **Ran Q&A research** to answer 5 questions from Alec about keyword breadth, target grant purpose phrasing, and first-grant size statistics from the database.

**Output:** `CHECKPOINT_1_client_profile.md` - Full client profile with all 39 `dim_clients` fields populated from verified sources, including identity table, financial profile, mission and programs, key people, 5 known past funder EINs, 8 data quality flags, 10 proposed keywords with hit counts, 3 `target_grant_purpose` options, and 5 questions for review.

**Human review:** Alec approved with adjustments: 11 keywords (added "veteran fishing"), Max-of-3 semantic scoring approach, $20K-$750K budget range, exclude all 5 known funders.

**Database change:** VetsBoats inserted into `f990_2025.dim_clients` as ID 8.

### Checkpoint 2: Sibling Discovery and Grant Landscape

**Goal:** Map the funding landscape. Who funds orgs like VetsBoats?

**What happened:**

1. **Inserted 28 known siblings** from January 16 research into `calc_client_siblings` with tiered similarity scores (0.65-0.90) as a proxy for embedding-based similarity.

2. **Searched for additional siblings** by analyzing mission text in organizations appearing in grant purpose texts mentioning sailing + veteran/therapeutic language. Found 5 new siblings: Sail to Prevail (RI), Team Paradise (FL), Freedom Waters (FL), Sailing Angels (TX), Raider Sailing (WA). Alec overrode the budget filter to keep Freedom Waters (recent year above $750K but strong mission alignment). Total: 33 siblings.

3. **Populated `calc_client_sibling_grants`** with all grants from 33 siblings using a complex INSERT that included inline keyword matching for all 11 keywords. 347 grants from 144 unique foundations loaded.

4. **Ran Python semantic similarity scoring** using `sentence-transformers` (`all-MiniLM-L6-v2`) with the Max-of-3 approach: each grant purpose was scored against 3 `target_grant_purpose` variants, and the highest score was kept. 340 of 347 grants scored. Threshold of 0.55 cleanly separated relevant from irrelevant grants (100% of keyword matches also scored >= 0.55).

5. **Result:** 47 target grants from 19 foundations. 26 keyword matches, plus additional semantic-only matches.

- `LOG_2026-02-06_checkpoint2_siblings_grants.md` (detailed activity log)
- `DATA_2026-02-06_full_review.csv` (347 grants, 23 columns)
- `DATA_2026-02-06_full_review.xlsm` (Excel version)

## The Quality Review Process

The VetsBoats report was the first to go through the formal review SOP, which was created during this same session. The process warrants detailed documentation because it caught significant errors.

### Review Architecture

The SOP defines a 4-agent review team:

1. **Agent 1: Data Verification** - Cross-references every dollar amount, percentage, trend, and count in the report against the database
2. **Agent 2: Contact & Web Verification** - Checks emails, phone numbers, URLs, and foundation websites
3. **Agent 3: Internal Consistency** - Compares facts across sections (does the "At a Glance" match the detailed profile?)
4. **Agent 4: Client Fit** - Checks that all foundations match the client's geography, sector, and eligibility

### Scoring System

- 44 tests across 5 gates
- Each test weighted by severity (CRITICAL = instant fail, HIGH = large point deduction, MEDIUM/LOW = smaller)
- Pass threshold: 90/100
- Hard gates: zero tolerance for known funders in report, expired deadlines, invalid EINs, inactive foundations, dollar contradictions, grant misattributions

### Key Insight

The review's most impactful finding was systematic: **AI-generated statistics diverge from database values when the model writes from contextual inference rather than running verification queries.** All 5 giving trends were wrong. 4 of 5 repeat rates were inflated. Two grant amounts were fabricated. The fix was straightforward (query `calc_foundation_profiles` and replace), but without the review process these errors would have reached the client.

## Lessons Learned

### What Worked Well

1. **Parallel research agents.** Running 3 research streams simultaneously (SF Challenge, 1687 Foundation, connection paths) during Checkpoint 5 saved significant time. Each agent had a specific, bounded topic and returned source-cited results.

2. **DB-first enrichment before web research.** Pre-screening 8 of 17 foundations via database queries alone (geographic restrictions, PF exclusion policies from 990-PF fields) eliminated nearly half the candidates before spending any time on web research.

3. **Honest assessment of constraints.** Being upfront about VetsBoats' PF status and the narrow viable pool (rather than padding with weak matches) produced a more credible report. The "0 READY, 3 CONDITIONAL" finding drove the conversation about what a realistic report looks like for this client.

4. **The Sail to Prevail bridge.** Discovering that both Barry and Van Beuren fund Sail to Prevail created the strongest insight of the report: one relationship unlocks two cultivation targets. This kind of cross-foundation intelligence is what differentiates TheGrantScout.

5. **Session state files.** Saving detailed continuity files between sessions (`SESSION_STATE_*.md`) preserved context across context window compactions and session boundaries. Each new session could pick up exactly where the last left off.

6. **The quality review caught real errors.** First-pass score of 70.66 rose to 93.57 after corrections. Without the review, fabricated grant amounts and wrong trends would have reached the client.

### What Needs Improvement

1. **PF clients need a different scoring pipeline.** The standard model has no PF-to-PF eligibility feature. Future enhancement: add "PF grants count" to the scorer so PF clients get foundations with proven PF funding ranked higher.

2. **Foundation enrichment should be cached.** The 17 foundations researched during this run were not stored in a database table. Future reports for VetsBoats (or similar clients) would need to re-research them.

3. **AI-generated statistics must be verified against DB.** The draft contained fabricated numbers (Sail to Prevail $200K vs actual $50K). All statistics in reports should be pulled from database queries, not generated from model inference.

4. **Connection path analysis could be automated.** The Matt Goettelman to Ron Levin to Kovler connection was found manually. A future tool could cross-reference client advisory board members against the `officers` table to surface these automatically.

5. **Report assembly took too long.** Writing 5 foundation profiles from scratch required multiple sessions. A template with merge fields populated from structured data (like the pipeline's `07_report_data.json` to `08_report.md` path) would cut this to a single session.

6. **MCP postgres tool reliability.** The tool entered "aborted" transaction state after any SQL error, making all subsequent queries fail. All database work after Session 1 was done through direct `psql` via Bash. This workaround should be the default for multi-query research.
