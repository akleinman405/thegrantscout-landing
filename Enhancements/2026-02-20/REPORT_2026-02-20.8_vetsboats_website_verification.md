1. **Has actually funded other private foundations** (strongest predictor)

**Key lesson:** The "has funded other PFs" filter had a high false-positive rate. Many DB records showed PF recipients that were actually:
- Public charities with "Foundation" in their name (Dick's: Jeremy Lin Foundation, Da Bigger Picture Foundation)
- DAF pass-throughs or consulting entities (Philanthropy International)
- Closed-portfolio family foundations (Danford)

**Key discovery:** The deep crawl found explicit PF exclusions that the shallow scrape missed entirely:
- **Dr. Scholl Foundation** explicitly excludes "foundations that are themselves grantmaking bodies" (on Preferences page that failed to scrape in v1.0)

- **Union Pacific Foundation** states "applications will not be accepted from private foundations"
- **Rosendin Foundation** says "no other types of tax-exempt organizations will be considered"

**The single strongest actionable lead is Charles H. Stout Foundation** with an application window open NOW through June 15, 2026, and a near-identical programmatic match (funded Waves Project adaptive scuba for disabled veterans 4 times).

This is the strongest VetsBoats prospect since Charles H. Stout Foundation. VetsBoats combines two of the foundation's explicit priorities: veterans AND disability services, in the foundation's own geographic backyard.

Lesson: treat bare "501(c)(3)" requirements from large foundations as likely PF exclusion.
