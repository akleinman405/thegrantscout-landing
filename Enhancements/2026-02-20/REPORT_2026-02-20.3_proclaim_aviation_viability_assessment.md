## Executive Summary

Proclaim Aviation Ministries is a $3M religious nonprofit in rural SW Minnesota that trains missionary aviators. They have minimal foundation funding history ($37,200 from 5 small family foundations) despite being in the 71.5th percentile for funder count among their X-sector peers. The key question: can TGS surface enough new foundation prospects to deliver meaningful value?

**The answer is yes, conditionally.** The initial estimate of 15-25 high-confidence matches was too pessimistic. The sibling overlap analysis, which identified 11 missionary aviation and religious training organizations, reveals **118 production-ready foundations** that fund Proclaim's peer organizations but not Proclaim. Adding MN+X cohort foundations (153 in the cohort, 25 in the curated list) and co-funder network discoveries, the total addressable pool is approximately **150-175 unique production-ready foundations** after deduplication.

This is at the lower end of TGS's typical 50-500 finalist range but well above the viability floor. The critical uplift comes from the missionary aviation niche having several large, well-funded sibling organizations (MAF with 128 funders, Wycliffe with 246 funders) that serve as excellent proxy signals.

**Verdict: CONDITIONAL GO** with specific engagement conditions.
