LB Charitable Foundation

  • Why relevant: Veterans are explicitly named in their mission statement. They have funded 11 veteran organizations totaling $290,000. No restriction on private foundation applicants found. California-based with $50M in assets.
  • Caveat: Application pages returned 404 errors during research. Direct email outreach may be needed.
  • Next step: Email grants@lbcharitablefoundation.org to introduce VetsBoats and confirm the application process. Disclose private foundation status upfront.
  • Contact: grants@lbcharitablefoundation.org
  • Website: lbcharitablefoundation.org

Bob Woodruff Foundation

  • Why relevant: Core mission is veteran services. Funds adaptive sports including water-based programs through Move United ($2.5M+) and Disabled Sports USA. Average grant is $80,000 with a median of $30,000. Rolling applications year-round.
  • Caveat: Their application requires filing Form 990 or 990-EZ (not 990-PF). This may be a hard disqualifier for VetsBoats.
  • Next step: Email grants@bobwoodrufffoundation.org to confirm whether organizations filing 990-PF are eligible. If confirmed, this becomes a top-priority application.
  • Contact: grants@bobwoodrufffoundation.org
  • Website: bobwoodrufffoundation.org

Richard Reed Foundation

  • Why relevant: Funded Achieve Tahoe $55,000 for "adaptive sports and recreational activities for individuals of all ages and all disabilities." Also funded equine therapy for veterans ($40,000). Median grant is $10,000 with 57 grants across the portfolio. Best adaptive recreation giving track record outside our primary leads.
  • Caveat: Based in Arizona (filed from AZ) with no California sailing grants in our database. Small foundation driven by founder interests.
  • Next step: Email info@richardreedfoundation.org. Lead with the adaptive recreation angle and cite the Achieve Tahoe parallel. VetsBoats is doing similar work through sailing.
  • Contact: info@richardreedfoundation.org
  • Website: richardreedfoundation.org

Christopher & Dana Reeve Foundation

  • Why relevant: Awarded $25,000 to Achieve Tahoe for adaptive sports. Funds adaptive sports programs nationally, with Bay Area precedent (BORP received grants). Quality of Life grants up to $25,000. Veterans receive "special consideration." Median grant is $25,000 across 137 grants.
  • Caveat: Programs must serve people with paralysis specifically (spinal cord injury, stroke, MS). Private foundation eligibility is unconfirmed. One source lists PFs as ineligible.
  • Next step: Email QOL@Reeve.org to confirm whether private foundations can apply. If eligible, next deadline is March 12, 2026. Action needed immediately.
  • Contact: QOL@Reeve.org
  • Website: christopherreeve.org

Widgeon Point Charitable Foundation

  • Why relevant: Funds Sail to Prevail, an adaptive sailing program for people with disabilities in Rhode Island, the closest analog to VetsBoats in any foundation's portfolio. Also funds Sail Maine, NY Sailing Foundation, and Maine Adaptive Sports. Exceptional sailing + adaptive sports fit. $39M in assets with $3.9M in annual giving.
  • Caveat: Was invite-only in 2025 due to volume of AI-generated applications. Historically accepts LOIs from March 1 through September 15.
  • Next step: Monitor for 2026 application reopening. If they reopen, submit LOI immediately citing VetsBoats' parallel to Sail to Prevail.
  • Contact: Not publicly available. Monitor foundation website for updates.
