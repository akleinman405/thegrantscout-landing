## Executive Summary

Queried IRS 990 officers data across 11 organizations and found **9 multi-board connectors** linking Bay Area sailing organizations to target foundations. The Bay Area sailing leadership is a remarkably tight network centered on three organizations: St. Francis Sailing Foundation, Treasure Island Sailing Center, and St. Francis Yacht Club.

**Key metrics:**
- 11 organizations searched (2 PF targets, 1 PC target, 8 sailing network orgs)
- 201 officer records analyzed
- 9 people sit on 2+ relevant boards
- 3 "triple connectors" (Paul Heineken, Bill Kreysler, Rolf Kaiser) bridge SFSF + TISC + StFYC
- 9 contact cards produced for Alec review (all 9 approved)
- 1 formal grant program identified (SFSF, quarterly cycle, grants@stfsf.org)

**Biggest discovery:** St. Francis Sailing Foundation has a formal grants program that funds disabled sailors in the Bay Area. Typical awards $11K-$75K, with recent averages of $158K-$179K per award. VetsBoats should apply for the April 2026 cycle.

## Key Finding: SFSF Grants Program

St. Francis Sailing Foundation has a **formal grants program** that VetsBoats qualifies for:

- **Review cycle:** Quarterly (January, April, July, October)
- **Eligibility:** Disabled sailors, Bay Area organizations, underprivileged youth
- **Contact:** grants@stfsf.org
- **Website:** https://www.stfsf.org/grants/
- **Application deadline:** End of quarter before the event/program

**Grant sizing (sources noted):**
- Typical awards: $11,000 - $75,000 (source: Instrumentl)
- 2024 total giving: $357,580, 2 awards (source: Instrumentl)
- 2023 total giving: $473,639, 3 awards (source: IRS 990, DB-verified)
- Total assets: $8.1M in 2024 (Instrumentl); $7.7M in 2023 (DB-verified)
- Known recent grantees: TISC, Alameda Community Sailing Center, Call of the Sea

**Recommendation:** Submit for April 2026 cycle. Get warm intro to Rolf Kaiser or Maureen Roddy first. A $15K-$25K first ask would be reasonable.
