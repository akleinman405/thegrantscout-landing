## Executive Summary

Schedule I data is **already fully imported** and represents a major untapped asset. The `schedule_i_grants` table contains **996,940 grants from 48,069 public charity grantmakers** totaling $174 billion (2019-2023). This is entirely separate from the 990-PF private foundation grant data (1.76M grants from 92K foundations) that currently powers all matching.

**Critical VetsBoats discovery:** Schedule I reveals **$155,948 in grants VetsBoats already received from public charities** that were invisible in the 990-PF data:

| Grantmaker | Amount | Year | Purpose |
|-----------|--------|------|---------|
| American Online Giving Foundation | $125,948 | 2022 | General Support |
| Silicon Valley Community Foundation | $30,000 | 2023 | Workforce Development |

These two grants alone represent 56% of VetsBoats' 2023 revenue ($279K). Combined with the $43.5K from 3 known PF funders, VetsBoats' total documented grant funding is **$199,448**, not the $43.5K we thought from 990-PF data alone.

The audit also found rich public charity funding data for VetsBoats' peer orgs (Achieve Tahoe, TISC, Judd Goldman, Sail to Prevail, BAADS), enabling a "public charity peer funder discovery" matching strategy that doesn't exist today.

Of the 9 IRS 990 schedules NOT imported, only **Schedule L (Related Party Transactions)** has high relevance for governance risk scoring. The rest are low-priority.

## Missing Schedules Assessment

Of the 16 IRS 990 schedules (A through R), 7 are imported. Here's the assessment of the 9 that aren't:

### High Relevance

**Schedule L: Transactions with Interested Persons**
- Contains: Board/officer related-party transactions, excess benefit disclosures, conflict of interest data
- Relevance to matching: **HIGH.** Governance quality is a factor in funder decisions. Schedule L red flags could explain why foundations decline applicants.
- Relevance to VetsBoats: **HIGH.** Could reveal any governance concerns that might affect funder confidence.
- Estimated rows: ~100K
- Import effort: 3-4 hours
- **Recommendation: IMPORT**

### Medium Relevance

**Schedule C: Political Campaign and Lobbying**
- Contains: Lobbying expenditures, political activity disclosures
- Relevance to matching: **MEDIUM.** Some foundations exclude advocacy-heavy orgs.
- Relevance to VetsBoats: **LOW.** VetsBoats is direct services, not advocacy.
- Estimated rows: ~300K
- **Recommendation: DEFER** (nice-to-have, not urgent)

**Schedule D: Supplemental Financial (Parts II-IV, not yet imported)**
- Contains: Securities, real estate, other investments held
- Currently imported: Part I (DAF summary) and Part V (endowments) only
- Relevance to matching: **MEDIUM.** Could enrich foundation financial profiles.
- **Recommendation: DEFER**

## Impact on Report Generation and Matching

### What Schedule I Enables (That We're Not Currently Doing)

**1. Public Charity Grantmaker Matching**

The current LASSO V6.1 scoring model uses only 990-PF data (fact_grants from 92K private foundations). Schedule I adds 48,060 public charity grantmakers. For a PF client like VetsBoats (where 83% of private foundations are ineligible), public charity grantmakers are potentially MORE relevant than private foundations.

Integration path:
- Add Schedule I grantmakers to the scoring pipeline
- Create `calc_public_charity_grantmaker_features` (parallel to `calc_foundation_features`)
- Score public charity grantmakers on: geographic match, sector match, grant size match, peer org overlap

**2. Peer Funder Discovery (Public Charity Track)**

The "47 foundations fund your peers but not you" email angle currently uses only 990-PF data. Schedule I enables a parallel version: "12 community foundations and veteran orgs fund your peer adaptive sailing programs but not you yet."

For VetsBoats specifically, Schedule I shows:
- Christopher Reeve Foundation funds Achieve Tahoe ($25K) but not VetsBoats
- St. Francis Sailing Foundation funds TISC ($75K) but not VetsBoats
- Multiple DAF sponsors fund peer orgs but not VetsBoats

**3. Recipient-Level Grant History**

Because Schedule I has 93.9% recipient EIN coverage (vs ~0% in pf_grants), we can build complete grant histories for any nonprofit. For VetsBoats, we now know about the $156K in public charity grants. For any prospective client, we can show them their full incoming grant picture before they even sign up.

**4. Funder Intelligence for Reports**

Current reports include "Recent Similar Grants" using 990-PF data only. Adding Schedule I would show clients which community foundations and public charities fund similar orgs, a much more actionable signal for small nonprofits.

## Notes

1. **The $125,948 AOGF grant is likely aggregated individual donations**, not a single grant decision. American Online Giving Foundation operates as a giving platform (like Network for Good). Still, it shows VetsBoats has significant individual donor support routed through DAF-like platforms.

2. **2021 and earlier Schedule I data has limited coverage** (907 grantmakers in 2021 vs 30K+ in 2023). This is an IRS data release timing issue, not an import gap. As more TEOS ZIPs are released, earlier years will fill in.

3. **Schedule I has 93.9% recipient EIN coverage** vs ~0% in pf_grants. This is because IRS 990 Schedule I requires the filer to report recipient EINs, while 990-PF Part XV does not. This makes Schedule I far superior for recipient matching and network analysis.

4. **The 48,060 unique public charity grantmakers** are almost entirely distinct from the 92,039 private foundation grantmakers. Only 9 organizations appear in both datasets. This means Schedule I represents a genuinely new funder universe, not a redundant copy of what we already have.

5. **St. Francis Sailing Foundation (EIN 942956977)** gave $75K to TISC and $65K to St. Francis YC Junior Sailing in 2023. This is a high-priority relationship target for VetsBoats through the Bay Area sailing community.
