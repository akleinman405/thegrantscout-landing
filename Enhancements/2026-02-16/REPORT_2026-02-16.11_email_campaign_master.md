- **Nonprofit service:** Help clients reach foundations that match their mission

### Performance Benchmarks (2025-2026)

| Metric | Average | Good | Elite (Top 10%) |
|--------|---------|------|-----------------|
| Open rate | 27.7% | 45%+ | 50%+ |
| Reply rate | 3.4-5.1% | 5.5-10% | 10.7%+ |
| Positive reply % | 48-53% | 61-65% | 65%+ |
| Meeting/booking rate | 0.69% | 1.25-1.86% | 2.34% |

**TGS target:** 6.5% positive reply rate (timeline hooks + L3 personalization + nonprofit sector baseline).

### Hook Strategy

| Hook Type | Reply Rate | Meeting Rate |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Timeline ("Since you recently...") | 10.01% | 2.34% |
| Numbers ("X% of orgs like yours...") | 8.57% | 1.86% |
| Social proof ("Org X achieved...") | 6.53% | 1.25% |
| Problem ("Struggling with...") | 4.39% | 0.69% |

**TGS advantage:** Our 990 data enables timeline and numbers hooks at scale. Reference specific foundation names, grant amounts, and NTEE sectors in every email.

### Email Structure

1. **Personalized hook** (1 sentence, 10-15 words): References a specific grant, org milestone, or sector trend
2. **Value bridge** (1-2 sentences, 20-30 words): Connects their situation to what we offer
3. **Soft CTA** (1 sentence): Low-commitment question ("Worth a quick look?")
4. **Minimal signature**: Name, title, TheGrantScout (no HTML, no images)

### "Give First" Strategy

Research confirms that leading with free, verifiable value dramatically outperforms sales-first approaches. Personalized cold emails with specific insights achieve 10-25% reply rates vs 1-5% for generic pitches.

**TGS implementation:**
- Put 2-3 real foundation matches (names, amounts, purposes) in the email body
- Every claim must be verifiable within 30 seconds (real EINs, real grants)
- Follow-ups bring new value each time (new match, new data point), never "just checking in"
- Offer to help even if they cannot pay (builds reputation, converts later)

### Crisis Ethics Framework (7 Rules)

The federal funding crisis makes our service more relevant but creates ethical risk. Seven rules govern all outreach:

1. **Lead with the solution, not the wound.** Open with what we found, not what they lost.
2. **Never reference specific cuts** unless the recipient mentioned them first.
3. **Make value unconditional.** The insight is free whether or not they subscribe.
4. **Price the same as pre-crisis.** No "emergency packages."
5. **Acknowledge context with restraint.** "Current funding environment" not "devastating crisis."
6. **Pass the "worst day" test.** If they read this after laying off staff, does it feel like solidarity?
7. **Offer to help even if they cannot pay.**

### Key Content Rules

| Rule | Evidence |
|------|----------|
| Plain text only | HTML bounces 652% more, rich signatures reduce replies 23% |
| No tracking pixels | 10-15% lower reply rates, flags AI spam filters |
| 0-1 links maximum | Multiple links trigger phishing detection |
| No images | Images are a spam signal in cold email |
| No spam trigger words | "Free," "guarantee," "exclusive," "act now" = 67% more spam |
| Subject lines 4-7 words | Under 40 chars = 37% higher open rates |
| Include recipient name in subject | 43% reply rate when name included |

### Two Buyer Segments

**Nonprofits ($99/month):**
- Hook: Specific foundation matches from our database
- CTA: "I can send your top 5 matches, would that be useful?"
- Personalization: State + NTEE sector + budget band + recent 990 data

**Foundations ($50/month per grantee):**

### Hybrid Architecture

PostgreSQL (source of truth) manages prospect data, scoring, analytics, and content generation, then pushes prospects to Smartlead via API. Smartlead (sending infrastructure) handles warm-up, SMTP, mailbox rotation, follow-up scheduling, OOO handling, and sends webhook events back to PostgreSQL.

### Infrastructure Findings

| Topic | Key Finding | Priority |
|-------|------------|----------|
| **Warm-up** | 3-4 weeks minimum. Stagger 20 subdomains in batches of 5. Smartlead handles automatically. | Tier 1 |

## Key Decisions Made

These conclusions emerged from the research and should be treated as settled:

1. **Serper.dev over Google Custom Search, SerpAPI, or Bing** for search-based URL discovery. 5-35x cheaper for equivalent results.

2. **Trestle over ZoomInfo, Apollo, Clearbit** for contact enrichment. Works from phone numbers (which we have) not domains (which we don't for 66% of foundations).

3. **MillionVerifier over NeverBounce, ZeroBounce, Reacher** for email verification. Cheapest at $129/100K and doesn't charge for catch-all results.

4. **Pattern inference over commercial APIs** as the primary email discovery method for foundations with URLs. 50-65% yield at $0.03-0.07/email vs $0.40-1.00 for commercial APIs.

5. **DAFs are excluded.** Not in 990-PF data, no application path, donor-driven only.

6. **Bank-managed foundations are highly reachable.** 7 major banks with philanthropy desks, 4 with online grant portals. This is standard industry practice, not a novel discovery.

7. **Physical mail is viable for the long tail.** 3-5% response rate cold, 8-15% with phone follow-up. Not obsolete for foundation outreach.

8. **State charity registries are not useful.** No email fields, no bulk downloads (except CA), no website fields.

9. **Smartlead Pro over DIY, Instantly, Woodpecker, Lemlist** for sending infrastructure. At $78.30/month (annual), it costs less than standalone warm-up alone ($180-400/month) and includes warm-up, sender rotation, follow-up automation, bounce handling, and API integration.

10. **3 touches, not 4.** Initial (Day 0), follow-up (Day 3), break-up (Day 10). Touches 1-2 capture 86% of replies. The break-up email adds 7-14%. A 4th touch has negligible lift and increases spam risk.

11. **No open/click tracking.** Tracking pixels make emails 15% more likely to land in spam and correlate with 2x lower reply rates. Monitor engagement through actual replies only.

12. **Reoon + BounceBan for pre-send verification** over MillionVerifier (previous recommendation). Reoon ($12/10K) for primary pass, BounceBan (~$10/batch) for catch-all domain secondary pass. Re-verify quarterly.

13. **Timer-based follow-ups over behavioral triggers.** At our volume (150-300/day), the complexity of open/click-based triggers is not justified. Simple day-count scheduling (Day 0/3/10) with reply-detection pause is sufficient.

14. **Timeline hooks over problem hooks.** Timeline hooks ("Since you recently...") achieve 10% reply rate and 3.4x more meetings than problem hooks ("Struggling with..."). Our 990 data enables timeline hooks at scale.

15. **L3 personalization (situation-specific), not L1 (merge tags).** Cohorts of 50 or fewer with state + NTEE + budget segmentation achieve 2.76x higher reply rates than 1,000+ blasts with name/company merge tags.

16. **"Give first" with real data in the email body.** Put 2-3 verifiable foundation matches (names, amounts) directly in the first email. Do not gate value behind a demo or reply. Each follow-up brings new value, never "just checking in."

17. **7-rule crisis ethics framework for all outreach.** Lead with solution not wound, never reference specific cuts, make value unconditional, pass the "worst day" test. See Phase 4 section for full framework.

18. **Peer Funder Discovery is the lead angle, not Government Cuts.** 20 angles evaluated across 4 dimensions. Peer Funder Discovery scored 14/15 (highest). Government Cuts scored 7/15 (lowest). The crisis angle should be context in follow-ups, not the first touch.

19. **ALTER TABLE fp2/np2, not new tables, for prospect profiles.** Extends existing master tables with campaign-support columns rather than creating new prospect profile tables. Avoids schema proliferation.

20. **CREATE VIEW for AI email context.** Two flat views (vw_np_email_context, vw_fdn_email_context) provide denormalized prospect data for prompt consumption. Views stay current without refresh schedules.
