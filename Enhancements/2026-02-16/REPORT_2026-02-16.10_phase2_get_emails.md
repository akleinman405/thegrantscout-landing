## Phase Summary

**Goal:** Get email addresses (and identify the right contact person) for as many foundations as possible, using both web scraping and non-web methods.

**The key insight:** There are two completely different populations requiring different strategies:

- **Population A (has website):** ~104,000-134,000 foundations after Phase 1 (starting from 49,265 IRS-filed URLs). Strategy: scrape websites + pattern inference from officer names.
- **Population B (no website):** ~9,000-39,000 foundations with no discoverable web presence after Phase 1. Strategy: mine IRS data, reverse phone lookup, bank desk mapping, physical mail.

The combined pipeline can reach 70-90% of all viable foundations with at least one contact method (email, phone, or bank desk contact).

## Verification and Quality

### MillionVerifier ($129/100K emails)

**The clear winner** for bulk verification. Doesn't charge for catch-all domain results. Use for all bulk verification.

### Catch-All Domain Handling

15-38% of domains are catch-all (accept all addresses). These bounce at 27x the rate of verified addresses. Strategy:
1. Flag catch-all domains during verification
2. Separate into "risky" tier with lower confidence
3. Send to catch-all addresses in small batches with bounce monitoring
4. Prefer generic role addresses (info@, contact@) on catch-all domains

### Freshness Management

| List | Re-verification Frequency | Annual Cost |
|------|--------------------------|------:|
| 12K foundations | Every 6 months | ~$74 |
| 240K nonprofits | Quarterly | ~$516-800 |
| **Total** | | **~$600-900/yr** |

### Bounce Thresholds

- Hard bounce rate: keep under 2%
- Gmail spam complaint: keep under 0.3%
- Immediately suppress hard bounces
- Suppress soft bounces after 3 consecutive failures
