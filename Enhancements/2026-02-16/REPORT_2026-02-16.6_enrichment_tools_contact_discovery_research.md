## Executive Summary

We researched 7 categories of tools and approaches for finding contact information (especially email addresses) for ~95,000 private foundations that lack websites. Our foundations have phone numbers (85,600), mailing addresses (95,494), officer names from 990-PF filings, and EINs.

**Key finding:** No single tool solves this problem. The B2B enrichment tools (Hunter.io, Apollo, ZoomInfo) are designed for corporate contacts and will have very low hit rates (estimated 10-25%) for small family foundations. The most promising approaches for our specific use case are:

1. **Trestle Reverse Phone API** -- Best ROI for our data. At $0.07/query, we can enrich 85,600 phone numbers and get owner names, email addresses, and current addresses. Estimated 40-60% match rate for email.
2. **Smarty/SmartyStreets Address Classification** -- Cheap way to segment our 95K addresses into residential vs. commercial, enabling targeted outreach strategies. RDI included in standard pricing.
3. **Physical Mail** -- Surprisingly effective for small family foundations at $0.75-$1.50/piece. 3-5% response rate expected, which at scale yields thousands of contacts.
4. **Candid Search** -- Newly merged GuideStar + Foundation Directory at $100/month (down from $299). Contains foundation contact data for 304K+ grantmakers.

The corporate B2B tools (ZoomInfo at $15K+/year, Clearbit/Breeze now requiring HubSpot enterprise) are poor fits for this use case.

## 2. USPS Address Intelligence

### What's Available

The USPS provides address metadata through its Address Information System (AIS), accessible via third-party APIs. The key data point for our use case is the **Residential Delivery Indicator (RDI)**, which classifies every deliverable US address as either residential or commercial.

### Why This Matters for Foundation Segmentation

| Address Type | What It Likely Means | Outreach Strategy |
|--------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Residential | Family foundation run from home | Personal letter; phone call |
| Commercial (non-professional) | Foundation has an office | More formal approach; may have staff |
| PO Box | Foundation uses mail drop | Mail only; phone follow-up |
| Bank/Trust Company address | Managed by financial institution | Contact bank trust department |
| Law Firm address | Attorney manages foundation | Contact law firm grants coordinator |

By classifying our 95,494 addresses, we can tailor outreach by segment. A personal letter to a residential address will feel very different from a formal inquiry to a bank trust department.

### Smarty (formerly SmartyStreets)

**Capabilities:** US address validation with 55+ metadata points including RDI, ZIP+4 geocoding, vacancy status, county FIPS, carrier route, and congressional district.

**Pricing:**
- Free: 250 lookups/month
- Free trial: 1,000 lookups over 42 days
- Paid plans start at ~$54/month for 25,000 lookups
- RDI is included in standard address validation (no extra cost)
- No setup fees

**For our use case:** At 95,494 addresses, we need approximately 100K lookups. Estimated cost: $100-$300 one-time for a bulk run.

**API:** Yes, RESTful API. Supports batch processing.

### Lob

**Capabilities:** Address verification + direct mail automation. Free for US address verification.

**Pricing:**
- US address verification: Free
- International: $0.15/call
- Direct mail integration included (printing + mailing service)

**For our use case:** Lob is particularly interesting because it combines address verification with direct mail sending. We could verify addresses AND send mail through the same platform.

| Criterion | Rating (1-5) | Notes |
|-----------|:---:|-------|
| Coverage of small family foundations | 5 | All USPS addresses covered |
| Cost per lookup | 5 | Free (Lob) to ~$0.003 (Smarty at volume) |
| Data quality | 5 | USPS-sourced; definitive |
| Legal safety | 5 | Public postal data |
| Implementation effort | 4 | Simple API; batch processing supported |

**Verdict:** HIGHLY RECOMMENDED. Cheap, universal coverage, zero legal risk. Classify all 95K addresses for under $300. Use the segmentation to drive outreach strategy.

## 3. LinkedIn as an Officer Lookup Tool

### The Opportunity

We have officer names from 990-PF filings. Many foundation officers (trustees, directors, presidents) have LinkedIn profiles under their personal or professional identities. LinkedIn could help us:
- Confirm officer identity and current role
- Find their professional email or reach them via InMail
- Identify mutual connections for warm introductions
- Discover their professional background (useful for outreach personalization)

### LinkedIn Sales Navigator

**Pricing:**
- Core: $99.99/month ($79.99 with annual billing)
- Advanced: $149.99/month ($139.99 annual)
- Advanced Plus: Custom pricing (enterprise)
- 30-day free trial available

**Capabilities:** Advanced search filters (name, company, title, location, industry), lead recommendations, InMail messaging, CRM integration.

**Limitations for foundations:** LinkedIn's company database may not include small private foundations. Searching by person name + "foundation" keyword would work, but matching accuracy depends on whether officers list their foundation role on LinkedIn. Many family foundation trustees do NOT list their foundation role, as it is a volunteer/board position rather than their primary job.

**Estimated match rate:** 30-50% of foundation officers likely have LinkedIn profiles, but only 15-25% would be identifiable as being associated with a specific foundation.

### PhantomBuster (LinkedIn Automation)

**Pricing:**
- Starter: $69/month (5 automations)
- Pro: $159/month (15 automations)
- Team: $439/month (unlimited)
- 14-day free trial

**Capabilities:** LinkedIn profile scraping, Sales Navigator export, automated connection requests, message sequences, email enrichment.

**Important:** PhantomBuster does not have its own email database. It relies on external enrichment providers (Apollo, Hunter, etc.) for email addresses. It is primarily a LinkedIn data extraction and automation tool.

### Legal Considerations

**hiQ v. LinkedIn (Ninth Circuit, 2022):** The court ruled that scraping publicly available LinkedIn profiles does not violate the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA), establishing important precedent. However:

- LinkedIn's Terms of Service still prohibit scraping. Violation is a contract issue (not criminal), but LinkedIn can ban accounts.
- Creating fake accounts to scrape logged-in data IS illegal (established in the final settlement).
- GDPR applies in Europe if scraped data identifies individuals.
- The hiQ case ended in a settlement with a permanent injunction against hiQ specifically, though the legal precedent for public data scraping remains.

**Practical risk:** LOW for small-scale manual searches. MODERATE for automated scraping at scale. LinkedIn actively detects and blocks automation tools. Account restrictions or bans are common.

| Criterion | Rating (1-5) | Notes |
|-----------|:---:|-------|
| Coverage of small family foundations | 2 | Officers may not list foundation roles |
| Cost per lookup | 2 | $100-160/month for tools + manual effort |
| Data quality | 4 | High when a match is found |
| Legal safety | 3 | Public profiles OK; automation risky |
| Implementation effort | 2 | Semi-manual process; automation risks account ban |

**Verdict:** Useful as a supplementary research tool for high-value prospects, not for bulk enrichment. Best used selectively for top-scoring foundations where we need to build a relationship.

### Why Mail Works for Small Family Foundations

1. **Personal touch:** 70% of recipients find direct mail more personal than digital outreach
2. **Trust:** 56% of people consider mail the most trustworthy marketing channel
3. **No spam filter:** 100% delivery rate to valid addresses
4. **Permanence:** Letters sit on desks; emails disappear in inboxes
5. **Foundation culture:** Many small foundation trustees are older individuals who prefer traditional communication

### Mail + Phone Combo

Industry research (and grant writing professionals) strongly recommend calling foundations before or after mailing. Program officers prefer to talk with potential grantees. A combined approach:
1. Send personalized letter introducing our service/client
2. Follow up with phone call 5-7 days later referencing the letter
3. Track responses and build contact database

This approach can deliver 8-15% response rates.

### USPS Every Door Direct Mail (EDDM)

EDDM is designed for geographic saturation (every address in a ZIP code). NOT useful for our targeted foundation outreach. We already have specific addresses. Standard or First-Class mail is the right approach.

### Lob (Automated Mailing Platform)

Lob combines address verification with automated letter/postcard sending. We could:
1. Verify all 95K addresses through Lob (free)
2. Send personalized letters through Lob's API
3. Track deliverability and returns

**Pricing:** Address verification is free. Letter printing + mailing through Lob is approximately $1.00-$2.00 per piece (higher than self-managed but fully automated).

## 7. Reverse Phone Lookup

### The Opportunity

We have 85,600 foundation phone numbers from 990-PF filings. These are the phone numbers foundations report to the IRS. For family foundations, this is often a personal phone number. Reverse phone lookup APIs can return the owner's name, email addresses, current address, and demographics.

### Trestle (Recommended)

**How it works:** Reverse Phone API validates phone numbers and returns owner identity, demographics, current addresses, alternate phone numbers, and email addresses.

**Pricing:**
- Reverse Phone: $0.07/query
- Email Deliverability Check add-on: $0.005/query
- Reverse Address: pricing similar
- Enterprise volume discounts available for 10,000+ queries/month
- Pay-as-you-go; no minimum commitment
- Only billed for queries, not matches

**Data returned per query:**
- Phone validity, line type (mobile/landline/VoIP), carrier
- Owner name(s) associated with the phone
- Owner demographics (age, gender)
- Current addresses
- Alternate phone numbers
- **Email addresses** (this is our key target)

**For our use case:** At $0.07/query, enriching all 85,600 phone numbers would cost approximately $5,992. With email deliverability checks ($0.005 each), total cost: ~$6,420.

**Estimated match rates:**
- Phone validity: 70-80% (some 990-PF phone numbers may be outdated)
- Owner name match: 60-70%
- Email address found: 40-60% of valid phones
- **Net email yield: ~25,000-35,000 email addresses** from 85,600 phone numbers

| Criterion | Rating (1-5) | Notes |
|-----------|:---:|-------|
| Coverage of small family foundations | 4 | Works well for personal/residential phone numbers |
| Cost per lookup | 5 | $0.07/query; ~$6,000 for all 85K numbers |

| Legal safety | 4 | Public records aggregation; standard for identity verification |

**Verdict:** HIGHLY RECOMMENDED. Best ROI tool for our specific data. Run all 85,600 phone numbers through Trestle for approximately $6,000 and expect 25,000-35,000 email addresses.
