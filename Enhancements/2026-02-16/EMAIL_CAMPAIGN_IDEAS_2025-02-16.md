# Email Campaign Ideas & Angles

## NONPROFIT ANGLES (B2C - $99/mo)

### 1. Federal Funding Cuts (NEW - Top Priority)
- Reference the specific government program they were getting funded from that got cut/frozen
- Don't sell — just acknowledge the disruption and ask if they want a free list of private foundations from their state that fund similar work
- Personalize using F990/SAM.gov data to identify which nonprofits lost federal funding
- Sectors to target: health/human services, education, housing, arts, community development

### 2. "Found X Foundations Funding Your Sector in Your State"
- Cold email with a specific number of matched foundations
- "Most nonprofits I talk to know maybe 20% of the foundations that could fund them"
- Offer to walk through the list in 15 minutes

### 3. "How to Get This Paid For"
- Show nonprofits that capacity building grants can cover the subscription
- List 5-10 common grant types that cover tools like this
- Removes the price objection entirely

### 4. Warm Referral from Shared Funder
- "X Foundation funds both [Beta Client] and your organization. We helped [Beta Client] with grant discovery — thought you might benefit too."
- Use F990 data to find grantees of beta clients' funders

### 5. CNE / Capacity Builder Referral
- Partner with orgs like CNE who do nonprofit assessments
- They recommend TGS when nonprofits say "I need to find new funders"
- PATH-style TA grants cover the cost

## FOUNDATION ANGLES (B2B - $50/mo per grantee, 10 min)

### 1. Curated Qualifying Nonprofit List (NEW - Top Priority)
- Offer a free list of nonprofits who qualify to be funded by them based on their giving history
- Helps with their review/discovery process
- Build the relationship, then pitch TGS as a capacity building grant to their grantees

### 2. Capacity Building for Grantees (Core B2B Pitch)
- "Help your grantees find additional funding from other foundations"
- Target foundations with history of intermediary/consultant/capacity funding
- $50/mo per grantee, annual subscription, 10-grantee minimum

### 3. Erb Foundation Model (Pilot Offer)
- Send curated list of their grantees who'd benefit most
- Propose a pilot group of 10-20 nonprofits
- Include ROI framing: "If half get one new grant this year..."

### 4. Foundation Management Companies (High Leverage)
- One relationship = access to dozens of foundations' grantees
- Whittier Trust (42+ foundations), Foundation Source (2,288), etc.
- 40+ target companies identified across seven tiers
- Pitch to Director of Philanthropic Services

### 5. Community Foundation Cohort Sales
- "Fund grant discovery for your grantees — $50/mo × 10 orgs = $6K/year, we handle everything"
- Position as a program offering they can provide

### 6. Invitation-Only Foundations
- Their grantees especially need help finding OTHER funders
- "Your grantees can't easily apply elsewhere — we help them discover who else funds their work"
