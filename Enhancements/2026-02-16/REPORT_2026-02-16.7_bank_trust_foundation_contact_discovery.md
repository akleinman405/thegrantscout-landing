## Executive Summary

Bank-managed foundations represent a significant and systematically contactable segment of the foundation universe. Our database contains 12,825 foundations with a major bank listed as an officer; 9,007 of those have no website. The good news: the major banks have built **public-facing grant portals** that serve as centralized access points, and the 990-PF application contact fields already contain bank-specific phone numbers and addresses for ~1,500 of these foundations.

**Key findings:**

- **Three banks have excellent public portals** with searchable directories: Bank of America (~160 foundations), JPMorgan Chase (~115 foundations), and Wells Fargo (unknown count, but searchable by state/program area). PNC and Truist also have structured programs.
- **fsrequests.com is Foundation Source's grant application portal.** It redirects to online.foundationsource.com. Foundation Source manages 2,000+ private foundations, and some of them accept applications through fsrequests.com/{foundation-slug}.
- **DAFs are NOT in the 990-PF universe.** DAF sponsoring organizations file Form 990 (not 990-PF), and they do not accept unsolicited grant requests. DAFs are a dead end for our pipeline.
- **The "philanthropy desk" approach is the most scalable path.** Each bank has a philanthropy/charitable trust department with a general phone number. For foundations not listed on public portals, calling the bank's philanthropy desk and asking about a specific trust by name is the standard grant-writer approach.
- **Community foundations can host private foundations,** but these are generally classified as component funds (filing under the community foundation's 990), not as standalone 990-PF filers in our database.

## 1. How Grantseekers Currently Contact Bank-Managed Foundations {#topic-1}

### The Standard Workflow

The standard approach experienced grant writers use for bank-trusteed foundations follows a predictable pattern:

1. **Identify the trustee bank** via 990-PF officer listings or Candid/Foundation Directory Online
2. **Check the bank's public portal** (if one exists) to see if the foundation is listed with application guidelines
3. **If not listed on the portal**, call the bank's philanthropic services or charitable trust department and ask about the specific foundation by name
4. **Submit an LOI or formal application** per the bank's instructions for that specific trust

### What Grant Databases Say

- **Instrumentl** includes bank-trusteed foundations in its database, showing past grantees, average grant amounts, giving frequency, location preferences, and board members. When a foundation is bank-managed, Instrumentl typically notes the trustee bank.
- **GrantStation** includes Truist Trusteed Foundations as a profiled grantmaker and lists the contact (Sofia Bork Aun at sofia.b.aun@truist.com for Truist).
- **Candid/Foundation Directory Online** provides the most comprehensive profiles, including trustee information and any available application procedures.

### Key Insight for TheGrantScout

The grant-seeking world already treats bank-trusteed foundations as contactable entities. The bank's philanthropy desk IS the front door. The 990-PF data we have (officer names, application contacts, phone numbers) maps directly to how professionals approach these foundations. The missing piece is systematically connecting our 9,007 bank-managed foundations to the correct bank portal or desk.

**Scalability:** HIGH. This is standard practice. The bank is the known intermediary.

**Cost:** LOW. Phone calls and portal lookups.

**Decision-maker access:** MEDIUM. The bank philanthropy officer screens applications and either makes decisions (if the bank has discretion) or forwards to the foundation's advisory committee.

## 4. Donor-Advised Fund (DAF) Sponsors {#topic-4}

### Are DAFs in the 990-PF Universe?

**No.** DAFs are NOT standalone 990-PF filers. Here is how they work:

- A DAF is a **fund within a sponsoring organization** (a 501(c)(3) public charity)
- The **sponsoring organization** files Form 990 (not 990-PF)
- DAF activity is reported on **Schedule D of Form 990**
- The sponsoring organization has **legal control** over the funds; donors only have **advisory privileges**
- There are 3.3 million+ DAF accounts across the major sponsors

### Can Nonprofits Submit Unsolicited Grant Requests to DAF Sponsors?

**No.** This is a dead end for grant seekers:

- **Fidelity Charitable:** "We do not accept applications for funding." Nonprofits should work directly with their donors who use DAFs.
- **DAFgiving360 (Schwab):** Grants are recommended by individual donors, not the sponsor. No application process for nonprofits.
- **Vanguard Charitable:** Same model. Donor-driven, no unsolicited applications.

### How DAFs Differ from Bank-Trusteed Foundations

| Feature | Bank-Trusteed Foundation | DAF Account |
|---------|------------------------|-------------|
| Tax filing | 990-PF (own EIN) | Part of sponsor's 990 |
| In our database? | YES | NO |
| Application process | Often yes (through bank) | No (donor-driven only) |
| Decision maker | Bank trust officer or advisory committee | Individual donor |
| Contactable by nonprofits? | YES | Only through the donor |

**Recommendation for TGS:** DAFs are irrelevant to our pipeline. They are not in our 990-PF data, and there is no way to submit grant requests through the sponsor. Remove any DAF-related entities from consideration.

## 5. Attorney/CPA as Contact Path {#topic-5}

### How Common Are Attorney/CPA Officers for No-Website Foundations?

From our database (officer title analysis of 95,823 no-website foundations):

| Officer Type | Foundations |
|-------------|------------|
| Bank/trust company | 14,459 |
| Attorney (title contains "attorney", "counsel", "law", "esq") | 110 |
| CPA (title contains "CPA", "account") | 41 |
| Other (family members, individuals) | 81,183 |

**Note:** These counts are based on title field matching and undercount significantly. Many attorneys and CPAs serving as foundation officers do not include their professional title. The actual number is much higher, but we cannot systematically identify them from 990-PF data alone.

### Standard Practice for Contacting Through Professional Advisors

Grant writers do use attorneys and CPAs as a contact path, but with important caveats:

1. **Look up the officer on the 990-PF.** If the only contact is a law firm or CPA firm, send a brief Letter of Inquiry addressed to the foundation (not the professional personally).

2. **Address the LOI to the foundation's president, chairperson, or trustee.** Use "c/o [Law Firm Name]" for the mailing address.

3. **Keep it brief and professional.** An informal inquiry email gauging interest is appropriate before investing time in a full proposal.

4. **Understand the role.** The attorney or CPA may be:
   - The actual decision-maker (common for small family foundations)
   - A pass-through who forwards requests to the family
   - A gatekeeper who screens out non-qualifying requests

### Legal/Ethical Considerations

- There is **no legal prohibition** on contacting a foundation through its listed officers, regardless of their profession
- The 990-PF is a **public document**, and the listed contact information is intended for this purpose
- Professional ethics rules do not prohibit an attorney or CPA from receiving grant inquiries on behalf of a foundation client
- The concern is more **practical**: the professional may simply discard inquiries if the foundation does not accept unsolicited applications

### Scalability Assessment

**Scalability:** LOW. Attorney/CPA officer foundations are individually addressable but not systematically contactable at scale. Each requires a custom LOI.

**Cost:** MEDIUM. Requires research time per foundation to draft a relevant inquiry.

**Decision-maker access:** VARIABLE. Sometimes the professional IS the decision-maker; sometimes they are a barrier.

**Standard practice?** YES, this is a well-known approach among experienced grant writers. The standard advice is: "If no other contact information exists, send an LOI to the foundation's registered agent or principal officer listed on the 990-PF."

## 6. Community Foundation Affiliations {#topic-6}

### Do Community Foundations Manage Private Foundations?

Yes, but with important distinctions:

- **Component funds** are held within a community foundation, share its tax-exempt status, and file under the community foundation's 990. **These are NOT in our 990-PF database** because they do not file separately.

- **Supporting organizations** are separate corporations managed by a community foundation. They have their own boards and may have their own EINs. Some may file 990-PF, but most file as public charities.

- **Noncomponent funds** may be held at a community foundation but do not share its tax-exempt status. These could potentially be 990-PF filers.

- **Geographic affiliates** are component funds serving defined regions under an advisory board.

### Can We Identify Community Foundation Affiliations?

**Not systematically from 990-PF data.** Community foundation component funds do not appear in our database because they file under the umbrella organization's 990. If a private foundation is affiliated with a community foundation, the only signal would be if the community foundation is listed as an officer or in the application contact fields.

### Are Community Foundation Staff a Viable Contact Path?

For foundations that ARE in our database but happen to have community foundation staff involvement: **potentially yes.** Community foundations:

- Can be located via the [Council on Foundations Community Foundation Locator](https://cof.org/page/community-foundation-locator)
- Staff may have knowledge of local private foundations (whether affiliated or not)
- Often serve as informal networking hubs for local philanthropy

**Scalability:** LOW. Community foundations are geographically specific and do not provide directories of affiliated private foundations (because those are internal component funds).

**Recommendation:** Not a primary contact discovery strategy, but community foundation staff can be a secondary resource when researching foundations in a specific geography.

- The 9,007 no-website bank-managed figure and 12,825 total bank-managed figure are based on officer name matching in the TGS database. Some foundations may be double-counted if they have multiple bank officers listed, though the queries use DISTINCT on EIN.
- The officer title-based analysis (attorney/CPA) significantly undercounts because many professionals do not include their title in the 990-PF officer field.
- Bank portal foundation counts (115 for JPMorgan, 160 for BofA, etc.) represent only the foundations where the bank has DISCRETIONARY grantmaking authority. Many more foundations use the bank as a passive trustee (managing assets only) with no formal application process.
- The `app_form_requirements` field in our 990-PF data is a JSONB field that sometimes contains URLs (like jpmorgan.com/onlinegrants) in its text content, confirming that foundations actively direct applicants to bank portals.
