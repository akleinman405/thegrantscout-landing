## Context

TheGrantScout is building a cold email pipeline to acquire customers in two segments: nonprofits ($99/month subscription) and foundations ($50/month per grantee, 10-grantee minimum). We have government funding cuts data covering 30+ events, 10 sectors, and 26,257 highly vulnerable nonprofits.

## Tasks

### Topic 1: "Give First" Strategy in Cold Email (Section 1A)

Research whether the "lead with free value first, sell later" approach (popularized by Hormozi, Brooke Castillo, etc. in content marketing / social media) actually works in cold email. Key questions:

1. Does "lead with free value" translate from social/content marketing to cold email? Key difference is consent.
2. Find case studies of cold email campaigns that led with free content and converted well.
3. Psychology of receiving free value from an unknown sender -- trust vs suspicion.
4. What makes a cold email freebie feel generous vs feel like a lead magnet trap?
5. Is there research on nonprofit/foundation recipients specifically?
6. Difference between "I found something useful for you" in inbox vs on social media.

### Topic 2: Ethical Line Deliberation (Section 1D)

Research and deliberate on crisis-based outreach ethics:

1. When does urgency become exploitation in B2B sales?
2. When does referencing lost funding feel manipulative vs helpful?
3. "We can help you through this" vs "we noticed you're struggling" -- meaningful difference?
4. How would a nonprofit ED feel receiving a cold email about their funding crisis on their worst day?
5. Nonprofit vendor reputation dynamics -- one tone-deaf campaign creates lasting damage.
6. Crisis-based marketing ethics -- when is it appropriate?

## Expected Output

Combined 6-8 page brief with clear headers, evidence citations, and actionable conclusions. Web search for real data and case studies, not generic advice.
