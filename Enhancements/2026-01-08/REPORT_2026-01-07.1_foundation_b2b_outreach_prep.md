## Key Metrics Defined

### Grant Dependency Percentage
**Definition:** Of ALL foundation grants this nonprofit has received (from any foundation in our database), what percentage came from this specific foundation?

**Why use this:**
- Larger numbers (more compelling)
- Defensible claim
- Foundation can verify by looking at their own grants
- Indicates lack of diversified foundation funders

**Example:** If Nonprofit X received $100K from all foundations combined, and $85K came from Meyer Memorial Trust, their grant dependency on MMT = 85%.

### Revenue Dependency Percentage
**Definition:** What percentage of the nonprofit's TOTAL annual revenue comes from grants from this foundation?

**Why NOT use this (for initial email):**
- Much smaller numbers
- Data quality issues (some revenue data clearly wrong)
- Foundation can't easily verify nonprofit revenue
- Risk of overstatement → loss of credibility

**Use case:** Save this for follow-up conversations when discussing specific at-risk grantees.

## What I Learned

### 1. Grant dependency is more useful than revenue dependency for B2B outreach
- Revenue data has quality issues (some clearly wrong values)
- Grant dependency numbers are larger and more compelling
- Foundations can verify grant dependency from their own records

### 2. "Institution" filtering is important
- Universities, hospitals, government entities, etc. don't need The Grant Scout
- These inflate grantee counts but aren't real prospects
- Simple regex filter removes ~5-15% of grantees

### 3. Meyer Memorial Trust is the standout prospect
- 18% of their community grantees are ≥80% dependent
- Many are smaller Oregon community organizations
- Strong fit for our value proposition

### 4. Some foundations aren't good fits
- Lisa & Douglas Goldman Fund: Only 6 orgs at ≥80% dependency - they spread grants thinly
- Joseph Drown Foundation: Only 4 orgs at ≥80% - same pattern
- These foundations' grantees are already diversified

### 5. Newer organizations funded recently are the best examples
- First funded 2021+ AND ≥80% dependency = strongest talking point
- These orgs haven't had time to diversify
- Meyer had 44 such orgs, Weingart had 32

## What I Wish I Had Known

### 1. The "revenue as % of..." calculation needs careful handling
- Some nonprofits have revenue in thousands vs actual dollars
- Creates wild outliers (11,000% dependency)
- Should have filtered for realistic ranges (0-200%) earlier

### 2. Complex multi-foundation joins are slow
- Trying to analyze 13 foundations in one query timed out
- Processing one foundation at a time is much faster
- Should have started with the tier-based approach

### 3. The best email stat isn't always the most impressive number
- 188 dependent orgs sounds good, but "188 out of 1,484" (13%) is different than "149 out of 836" (18%)
- Meyer's 18% is actually more compelling than Hilton's 13%
- Percentage vs absolute number matters for the pitch

### 4. Contact verification takes time
- Many emails are inferred from patterns, not confirmed
- Some contacts are program officers, not decision makers
- May need to find the right person once in conversation

## How To Approach This In The Future

### Step 1: Start with a clear metric definition
- Decide upfront: grant dependency vs revenue dependency
- Define the threshold (80% seems good)
- Filter for community orgs only from the start

### Step 2: Tier the foundations first
- Query basic stats (total grantees, dependency counts) quickly
- Sort by strength of data
- Don't waste time on foundations with weak data

### Step 3: Process foundations individually
- One foundation per query is faster than multi-join
- Can checkpoint progress
- Easier to debug

### Step 4: Keep emails short
- Don't list specific org names (privacy, risk of error)
- One compelling stat per email
- Save details for the follow-up call

### Step 5: Prepare for the follow-up
- Have specific examples ready but don't lead with them
- Know the "newer + dependent" orgs as strongest examples
- Have revenue dependency as a backup metric if asked
