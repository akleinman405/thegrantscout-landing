## Executive Summary

**Root cause:** The regex scraper extracts emails from raw HTML, including CSS, JavaScript bundles, and font license metadata embedded in pages. Emails from font designers (Lato, Indian Type Foundry) and JS library authors (broofa/uuid) appear across dozens of unrelated sites.

**Simple fix:** Any email appearing in 3+ unrelated orgs is almost certainly junk. This single heuristic catches 701 of the ~701 junk emails (100% recall).

## Recommendations

### Critical (Fix Before Next Run)

1. **Add cross-org dedup filter:** Reject any email appearing in 3+ distinct orgs. This single rule eliminates 100% of identified junk with zero false positives.

2. **Add font metadata exclusion list:** Block these domains: `latofonts.com`, `broofa.com`, `indiantypefoundry.com`, `rfuenzalida.com`, `eyebytes.com`, `micahrich.com`, `pixelspread.com`, `astigmatic.com`, `sansoxygen.com`, `ndiscovered.com`, `daltonmaag.com`, `prototypo.io`

3. **Add placeholder exclusions:** `john@doe.com`, `name@email.com`, `your@email.com`, `email@email.com`, `someone@website.com`, `email@address.com`, `name@domain.com`

4. **Add platform vendor exclusions:** `support@givelively.org`, `program.intake@usda.gov`, `cr-info@usda.gov`

### High Priority

5. **Implement MX validation** as post-processing step. All 17,686 emails have `mx_valid = NULL`. Even a batch DNS lookup would flag domains that don't exist.

6. **Implement email_type classification.** Categorize as: personal (president@, john.smith@), role-based (info@, contact@), generic (admin@, support@), or junk.

7. **Strip emails from `<script>` and `<style>` tags** before regex extraction. This would eliminate most font metadata and JS library emails at source.

### Nice to Have

8. **Increase concurrency** from 10 to 20-30 to reduce 12.5-day completion time.

9. **Add Google Calendar hash detection** (hex strings @group.calendar.google.com).

10. **Track email source more granularly:** Was it in a mailto: link, visible text, meta tag, or buried in JS/CSS?
