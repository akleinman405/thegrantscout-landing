## Root Cause

The name and email come from **two completely independent data sources**:

| Field | Source | How Populated |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| `contact_first_name` | IRS 990 officer data (`all_officers` JSONB) | Extracted first name of ED/CEO/Executive Director from most recent filing |
| `contact_email` | IRS 990 return-level data + web scraping | Organization-level email from filing or website |

**The fundamental problem:** The IRS 990 lists the ED/CEO as the responsible officer, but the organization's public email address (info@, admin@, or even a staff member's personal email) belongs to whoever manages the inbox, not the ED/CEO.

Specific failure modes:

1. **Generic inbox + ED name (68.4%):** "Hi Kathleen" arrives at info@org.com. The office manager or intern reading it has no idea who Kathleen is.

2. **Wrong person's email + ED name (26.1%):** "Hi Kathleen" arrives at lqualls@vineyardtheatre.org. L. Qualls reads it and knows immediately this is a spray-and-pray cold email with bad data.

3. **Stale officer data (e.g., Gage Academy):** Kathleen Allen was the ED from Sep-Jun of a prior year. Mary Lloyd took over as interim. The 990 captures a snapshot in time that may be 1-2 years old.

## Recommendations

### Immediate (Before Next Send)

1. **Stop using `contact_first_name` for email greetings.** The data is not matched to the email recipient. Use "Hi there" for all generic emails, and only use a name when it can be verified against the email address.

2. **For personal emails, extract the greeting name from the email prefix** instead of the 990 officer data:
   - `jsmith@org.com` -> try to resolve "J. Smith" or just use "Hi there"
   - `jennifer@org.com` -> "Hi Jennifer" (safe, email prefix IS the first name)
   - `info@org.com` -> "Hi there" (generic, no name)

3. **Add a name-email match flag** to the pull query. Only use a personalized name when:
   - Email prefix starts with the full first name (e.g., `kathleen@` + "Kathleen"), OR
   - Email prefix is `{first initial}{last name}` pattern and matches an officer

### Medium-Term

4. **Enrich with actual contact data:** Use tools like Hunter.io or Apollo to find the real name behind each email address, rather than guessing from 990 officer data.

5. **Split the greeting logic by email type:**
   - Generic emails: "Hi there" or "Hello" (no name)
   - Verified personal: "Hi {verified_name}"
   - Unverified personal: "Hi there"

### Data Fix for Existing Records

6. **Update `send_campaign.py`** to implement smart greeting selection before the next batch.

## Notes

- The 88% first-name coverage rate from the 2026-02-16 session was misleading. 88% of prospects have a name extracted from 990 data, but that name has almost nothing to do with who reads the email.
- The "who is Kathleen?" response is a best-case scenario. Worst case, recipients silently mark us as spam because the bad personalization signals low-quality mass email.
- The IRS 990 officer data is valuable for other purposes (knowing who runs the org), but should NOT be used as the email greeting name unless the email address can be traced to that specific person.
