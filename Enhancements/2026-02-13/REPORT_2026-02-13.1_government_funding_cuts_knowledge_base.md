# Part 1: What Happened

## 1. Executive Summary

The period from January 2025 through February 2026 represents the most disruptive period for nonprofit federal funding in modern history. Through a combination of executive orders, agency-level grant terminations, DOGE-led contract cancellations, the "One Big Beautiful Bill" reconciliation law, and a four-month government shutdown, the federal government upended funding relationships that nonprofits had depended on for decades.

**Scale of the crisis:**

- 1 in 3 nonprofits lost government funding in the first half of 2025
- 22,757+ nonprofit jobs confirmed lost (Jan-Jun 2025); actual total estimated at 40,000+
- DOGE terminated 10,000+ contracts worth approximately $71 billion
- USAID closed permanently on July 1, 2025, with $9 billion in aid rescinded
- EPA rescinded $20+ billion in climate and environmental justice grants
- The "One Big Beautiful Bill" cut $863 billion from Medicaid and $295 billion from SNAP over 10 years
- A government shutdown from October 2025 through February 2026 compounded all of the above

**Critical distinction:** Congress ultimately rejected the most extreme proposed cuts. The FY2026 Consolidated Appropriations Act (signed February 3, 2026) maintained or increased most domestic programs. However, executive actions, agency restructuring, and the reconciliation law caused lasting damage independent of the appropriations process.

**What this means for TheGrantScout:** Tens of thousands of nonprofits that previously relied on government funding now face an urgent need for private foundation support. Our database contains the data to identify which organizations are most vulnerable and match them with foundations active in their sectors. This document establishes the methodology for that Phase 2 analysis.

**What our database shows:**

- 57,448 nonprofits reported $97.9 billion in government grants (tax year 2023)
- 35,083 organizations derive 50%+ of their revenue from government sources
- 26,257 of those have little or no foundation grant pipeline (fewer than 3 foundation grants since 2022), representing $124.7 billion in at-risk funding
- Private foundations gave $108.9 billion across all sectors in our database (2022-2024)

# Part 2: Database Methodology

## 5. Available Data Fields for Phase 2

### 5.1 Government Grants Field (`government_grants_amt`)

The `government_grants_amt` column in `f990_2025.nonprofit_returns` captures the total government grants reported by each nonprofit on their Form 990/990-EZ return. This is a single aggregate number; it does not break down by federal program or agency.

**Coverage (tax year 2023, our most complete year):**

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Orgs reporting government grants > $0 | 57,448 |
| Total government grants reported | $97.9 billion |
| Average government grant per org | $1.7 million |
| Median government grant per org | $165,000 |

**Year-over-year data:**

| Tax Year | Orgs | Total Gov Grants | Avg Per Org | Median |
|----------|------|-----------------|-------------|--------|
| 2023 | 57,448 | $97.9B | $1.7M | $165K |
| 2022 | 43,197 | $223.5B | $5.2M | $466K |
| 2021 | 3,008 | $5.9B | $2.0M | $169K |

Note: 2022 totals are inflated by COVID-era relief programs (PPP, EIDL, etc.). 2023 represents a more normal baseline. 2021 and earlier have low coverage because our data import focused on recent years.

### 5.2 Government Dependency Ratio

Calculated as `government_grants_amt / total_revenue`. This is the single most important indicator for vulnerability analysis.

**Distribution across all government-funded orgs (tax year 2022-2023):**

| Dependency Level | Org Count | Total Gov Grants | % of Orgs |
|-----------------|-----------|-----------------|-----------|
| 75-100% | 20,455 | $171.2B | 20% |
| 50-74% | 14,628 | $46.8B | 15% |
| 25-49% | 16,814 | $39.0B | 17% |
| 1-24% | 48,669 | $64.4B | 48% |

**Key insight:** 35,083 organizations (35% of government-funded nonprofits) derive 50%+ of their revenue from government sources. These organizations are structurally dependent and most vulnerable to cuts.

### 5.3 NTEE Code Mapping

NTEE codes allow us to map organizations to impacted sectors. Coverage is partial:

| NTEE Status | Org Count | Total Gov Grants | % Coverage |
|-------------|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| Has NTEE code (via dim_recipients) | 45,342 | $175.7B | 47% of orgs, 55% of $ |
| Missing NTEE | 51,149 | $145.7B | 53% of orgs, 45% of $ |

**Strategy for missing NTEE:** Use mission text keyword matching (see Section 6) to assign sector categories to orgs without NTEE codes.

## 7. Master SQL Query Templates

### Template A: Find Government-Dependent Nonprofits in a Given Sector

Returns nonprofits in a specified sector with high government dependency.

```sql
SELECT
    nr.ein,
    nr.organization_name,
    nr.state,
    nr.government_grants_amt,
    nr.total_revenue,
    ROUND(nr.government_grants_amt::numeric / NULLIF(nr.total_revenue, 0) * 100, 1) as gov_dependency_pct,
    nr.mission_description,
    dr.ntee_code
FROM f990_2025.nonprofit_returns nr
LEFT JOIN f990_2025.dim_recipients dr ON nr.ein = dr.ein
WHERE nr.government_grants_amt > 0
  AND nr.total_revenue > 0
  AND nr.tax_year >= 2022
  -- Sector filter (adjust NTEE codes per sector):
  AND (
    dr.ntee_code LIKE 'E%'  -- Health
    OR dr.ntee_code LIKE 'F%'  -- Mental Health
    OR dr.ntee_code LIKE 'G%'  -- Disease/Medical
    OR dr.ntee_code LIKE 'H%'  -- Medical Research
    -- OR: use mission keyword fallback for missing NTEE:
    OR (dr.ntee_code IS NULL AND nr.mission_description ILIKE '%health%')
    OR (dr.ntee_code IS NULL AND nr.mission_description ILIKE '%medical%')
    OR (dr.ntee_code IS NULL AND nr.mission_description ILIKE '%clinic%')
  )
  -- Minimum dependency threshold:
  AND nr.government_grants_amt::float / nr.total_revenue >= 0.25
ORDER BY nr.government_grants_amt DESC;
```

**Tested result (Healthcare sector, 25%+ dependency):** Returns thousands of rows. Top orgs include major health systems with hundreds of millions in government grants.

### Template B: Calculate Vulnerability Score

Combines government dependency with foundation support level to produce a vulnerability ranking.

```sql
SELECT
    nr.ein,
    nr.organization_name,
    nr.state,
    nr.government_grants_amt,
    nr.total_revenue,
    ROUND(nr.government_grants_amt::numeric / NULLIF(nr.total_revenue, 0) * 100, 1) as gov_dependency_pct,
    COALESCE(fg.foundation_grant_count, 0) as foundation_grants,
    COALESCE(fg.foundation_total, 0) as foundation_total_amt,
    -- Vulnerability score: higher = more vulnerable
    ROUND(
      (nr.government_grants_amt::float / NULLIF(nr.total_revenue, 0)) * 100
      - LEAST(COALESCE(fg.foundation_grant_count, 0) * 5, 50)
    , 1) as vulnerability_score
FROM f990_2025.nonprofit_returns nr
LEFT JOIN (
    SELECT recipient_ein,
           COUNT(*) as foundation_grant_count,
           SUM(amount) as foundation_total
    FROM f990_2025.fact_grants
    WHERE tax_year >= 2022
    GROUP BY recipient_ein
) fg ON nr.ein = fg.recipient_ein
WHERE nr.government_grants_amt > 0
  AND nr.total_revenue > 0
  AND nr.tax_year >= 2022
  AND nr.government_grants_amt::float / nr.total_revenue >= 0.50
ORDER BY vulnerability_score DESC
LIMIT 100;
```

**Scoring logic:**
- Start with government dependency percentage (0-100)
- Subtract 5 points for each foundation grant (capped at 50 points)
- Higher score = more vulnerable (high gov dependency, low foundation support)

**Tested result:** 26,257 orgs with 50%+ dependency and fewer than 3 foundation grants. Average vulnerability score is very high for orgs with zero foundation grants.

### Template C: Find Foundations Active in a Given Sector

Identifies foundations that already fund a given sector, for matching with displaced government-dependent orgs.

```sql
SELECT
    df.ein as foundation_ein,
    df.name as foundation_name,
    df.state as foundation_state,
    COUNT(DISTINCT fg.recipient_ein) as unique_recipients,
    COUNT(*) as total_grants,
    SUM(fg.amount) as total_giving,
    AVG(fg.amount) as avg_grant,
    PERCENTILE_CONT(0.5) WITHIN GROUP (ORDER BY fg.amount) as median_grant
FROM f990_2025.fact_grants fg
JOIN f990_2025.dim_recipients dr ON fg.recipient_ein = dr.ein
JOIN f990_2025.dim_foundations df ON fg.foundation_ein = df.ein
WHERE fg.tax_year >= 2022
  AND fg.amount > 0
  -- Sector filter (adjust per sector):
  AND (dr.ntee_code LIKE 'E%' OR dr.ntee_code LIKE 'F%'
       OR dr.ntee_code LIKE 'G%' OR dr.ntee_code LIKE 'H%')
GROUP BY df.ein, df.name, df.state
HAVING COUNT(*) >= 3
ORDER BY total_giving DESC
LIMIT 50;
```

**Tested result (Healthcare sector):** 35,492 unique foundations made grants in this sector since 2022, totaling $9.1 billion.

## 8. Data Gaps & Enrichment Needs for Phase 2

### 8.1 Cannot Link to Specific Federal Programs

**Gap:** The `government_grants_amt` field is a single total. We cannot determine which specific federal programs (NIH, USAID, FEMA, etc.) an organization receives funding from.

**Workarounds:**
- Use NTEE codes + mission text to infer likely federal programs
- Cross-reference with USAspending.gov data (external source)
- Use Grants.gov awards data to match specific organizations to specific programs

### 8.2 NTEE Code Coverage

**Gap:** 53% of government-funded orgs (51,149 organizations holding $145.7B) have no NTEE code in our database.

**Workarounds:**
- Join with `dim_recipients` to get NTEE codes (covers 47% of orgs / 55% of dollar value)
- For remaining gaps, use mission text keyword classification
- Consider enriching from IRS BMF (Business Master File) data, which has NTEE codes for most EINs

### 8.3 Limited Multi-Year Trend Data

**Gap:** Our `nonprofit_returns` data is concentrated in tax years 2022-2023 (100K+ orgs). Earlier years have sparse coverage (3,000 orgs in 2021, 379 in 2020).

**Impact:** Cannot reliably calculate multi-year government funding trends at the org level.

**Workaround:** Use the 2022-2023 data as a snapshot baseline. For trend analysis, focus on sector-level trends from external sources.

### 8.4 No Grant-Level Federal Funding Data

**Gap:** We have detailed grant-level data for foundation giving (fact_grants: 8.3M records) but not for government funding.

**Potential External Sources:**
- **USAspending.gov:** Federal grant awards by agency, program, and recipient (downloadable)
- **Grants.gov:** Active and closed federal grant opportunities
- **SAM.gov:** Federal awards data
- **Federal Audit Clearinghouse:** Single audits for orgs spending $750K+ in federal funds

### 8.5 Enrichment Steps for Phase 2

1. **NTEE enrichment:** Download IRS BMF data to fill NTEE code gaps
2. **USAspending.gov integration:** Match org EINs to specific federal awards
3. **Mission text classification:** NLP/keyword model to assign sector categories
