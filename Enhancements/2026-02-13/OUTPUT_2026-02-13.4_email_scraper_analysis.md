# Email Scraper: Analysis & Improvement Plan (Revised)

## Context

The email scraping pipeline (scripts 04-07) has been built and smoke-tested on 10 orgs but **the database tables don't exist yet** (web_emails, web_staff, web_best_email). The Nov 2025 campaign (old scraper) had a 6.1% bounce rate. This plan answers 7 questions and proposes improvements, reviewed from business value, technical risk, and data quality perspectives.

**Key insight from reviews:** This plan serves TWO separate business problems that should be prioritized differently:
1. **Product** -- Foundation contact data for client reports (directly improves what clients pay for)
2. **Sales** -- Nonprofit emails for cold outreach campaigns (grows the sales pipeline)

---

## Your 7 Questions, Answered

### Q1: How accurate is our email scraper?

**Estimated 85-90% deliverable.** The Nov campaign delivered 93.9% (6.1% bounced).

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Nov 2025 delivery rate | 93.9% (113 bounced of 1,844) |
| Domain-match rate (spot check) | 87% (13/15) |
| Smoke test: syntax+MX valid | 8/8 (100%) |
| Website email yield | 15-30% of sites have extractable emails |

The scraper logic itself is solid. The gap is validation (catching stale addresses where someone left the org). But the bigger gap is **coverage** -- only 19% of foundation prospects have any email at all, and only 13.4% of foundations even have a discoverable website.

### Q2: Better ways to predict email validity?

**Yes. Two missing layers: SMTP verification and catch-all detection.**

| Layer | What It Checks | Have It? | Impact |
|-------|---------------|----------|--------|
| Syntax check | Format valid | YES | Baseline |
| MX record | Domain accepts mail | YES | Catches dead domains |
| **Catch-all detection** | Domain accepts ALL addresses | **NO** | ~30-40% of nonprofit domains are catch-all |
| **SMTP RCPT TO** | Specific mailbox exists | **NO** | Would catch stale addresses |

**Commercial API vs self-hosted Reacher:** For our scale (~12K foundation emails, ~74K nonprofit emails), a commercial service like ZeroBounce ($0.008-0.01/email, ~$100-750 one-time) is more cost-effective than self-hosting Reacher. No infrastructure to maintain, no IP reputation risk, higher accuracy. Self-hosted SMTP probing from our residential IP risks getting blacklisted.

### Q3: Does MX validation change the confidence score?

**No.** `06_validate_emails.py` only sets `mx_valid` and `syntax_valid` booleans. The `confidence` column is never updated after extraction.

**Fix:** Don't mix extraction confidence with validation results (they measure different things). Add a separate `validation_status` column (pending/mx_valid/mx_invalid/smtp_verified/smtp_rejected/catch_all) and let the materialized view compose a final ranking from both signals.

### Q4: Should we keep ALL emails?

**Yes, and the new pipeline already does.** `web_emails` stores every email; `web_best_email` picks one winner. Benefits of keeping all:
- **Bounce fallback** -- try grants@org.org if info@org.org bounces
- **Role-based targeting** -- foundations need grants@, campaigns need person emails
- **Multi-touch** -- initial to info@, follow-up to a specific person
- **Decay management** -- alternates when re-validating every 90 days

### Q5: Additional tools we can use?

| Tool | What It Does | Recommendation |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **Commercial email verification API** (ZeroBounce/NeverBounce) | SMTP + catch-all + disposable detection | Use instead of self-hosted Reacher. ~$100-750 for full corpus |
| **Playwright** (Python) | Headless browser for JS-rendered sites | Use for Tier 1-2 foundations only (15-20% of sites need it). Skip nonprofits |
| **disposable-email-domains** (GitHub) | 2K+ throwaway domain blocklist | Easy win, 30 min |
| **Hunter.io API** | Email pattern inference from name+domain | Pay-per-use, fills gaps where we have officer names but no scraped email |

### Q6: Are we saving contact people and job titles?

**Yes**, the new pipeline saves to `web_staff`: name, title, email, phone, staff_type, confidence.

**Untapped resource:** The `f990_2025.officers` table has IRS 990-reported officer names for every org (26.3M records, 140K+ unique foundations). But the data has quality issues:
- 77% of names are ALL CAPS
- Titles truncated at 12 characters (15.5% of records): "EXECUTIVE DI" instead of "Executive Director"
- 87K+ records have institutional names ("BANK OF AMERICA") in the person field
- Some records have name/title swapped

Cross-referencing is valuable but harder than estimated. Expect 40-55% true positive match rate, 5-10% false positives. Budget 12-16 hours, not the originally estimated 6-8.

### Q7: Different approaches for foundations vs nonprofits?

**Yes.** The ranking should differ:

| Aspect | Foundation | Nonprofit |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Best email | Role (grants@, programs@) | Person (firstname@) or general |
| Key pages | /grants, /apply, /funding | /contact, /donate |
| Officer cross-ref | Board, trustees (990-PF) | ED, staff (990/990-EZ) |
| Extra extraction | Grant deadlines, LOI process | N/A |

**Fix:** Separate materialized views with different email type rankings.

## Critical Risks Discovered During Review

### CRITICAL: Only 13.4% of foundations have websites

| Org Type | Total | Has Clean URL | Has Live URL | Coverage |
|----------|-------|--------------|-------------|----------|
| Foundations | 143,184 | 19,119 (13.4%) | 14,107 (9.9%) | Very low |
| Nonprofits | 670,514 | 358,082 (53.4%) | 293,778 (43.8%) | Moderate |

Of the 12,653 Tier 1 foundations, only ~6,816 (54%) have live websites. At 15-30% email yield, web scraping alone will produce emails for **1,000-2,000 Tier 1 foundations** (8-16%). Web scraping cannot reach the majority of foundations.

### CRITICAL: 16GB free disk space, 14GB cache needed

The HTML cache for 307K orgs is estimated at 14GB. This will nearly fill the disk. Must process in waves and delete cache between tiers.

### HIGH: Database tables don't exist yet

`web_emails`, `web_staff`, `web_org_metadata`, `web_pages`, and `web_best_email` have not been created. The pipeline has only been smoke-tested. The launchd agent is not loaded.

### HIGH: Residential IP blacklisting from SMTP verification

Self-hosted SMTP probing from a residential IP looks like spam reconnaissance. Risk of landing on DNS blacklists (Spamhaus, SORBS) affecting all email from the network. Use a commercial API or a disposable VPS instead.

### MEDIUM: web_staff INSERT has no ON CONFLICT clause

If the pipeline crashes and restarts, staff records will be duplicated. Need to add conflict handling or a DELETE-before-INSERT pattern.

### MEDIUM: Aggregator URLs survived the cleaning pipeline

URLs from cybo.com, rocketreach.co, buzzfile.com, eintaxid.com, ltddir.com made it through the 24-domain blocklist. Scraping these yields wrong emails.

### MEDIUM: caffeinate -i doesn't prevent lid-close sleep

Need `caffeinate -s` (system sleep prevention) and a PID lock file to prevent concurrent launchd runs.

### MEDIUM: Confidence score poorly calibrated

Two very different quality emails can both score 0.65 (e.g., grants@foundation.org via mailto vs info@foundation.org via regex). The 0.00-1.00 scale collapses into a narrow band around 0.55-0.65.

## What This Plan Does NOT Solve

1. **86% of foundations have no website.** Web scraping alone cannot provide contacts for the majority of foundations. Alternative channels (Candid API, LinkedIn, phone lookup, manual research) are needed for completeness.
2. **Officer data is 2-3 years old.** Even after cross-referencing, ~30-40% of officers may have changed roles. Re-verification helps but can't fully compensate.
3. **Catch-all domains (~30-40% of nonprofits)** make individual email verification impossible. These emails will deliver but may not reach the intended person.
