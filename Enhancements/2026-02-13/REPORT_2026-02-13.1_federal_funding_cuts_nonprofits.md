## Executive Summary

The period from January 2025 through February 2026 has been the most disruptive period for nonprofit federal funding in modern history. The Trump administration pursued a multi-pronged strategy: executive orders targeting DEI programs, the OMB spending freeze, DOGE-led grant terminations, agency-level funding freezes, the "One Big Beautiful Bill" reconciliation law, and a proposed FY2026 budget with $163 billion in domestic spending cuts.

**The critical distinction:** The administration's initial proposals were far more severe than what Congress ultimately enacted. While the White House proposed cutting domestic discretionary spending by 23%, Congress passed the FY2026 Consolidated Appropriations Act on February 3, 2026, with total discretionary spending of $1.653 trillion -- $10 billion ABOVE FY2025 levels. Many of the most extreme proposed cuts were rejected on a bipartisan basis. However, executive actions (grant terminations, agency restructuring, DOGE cancellations) caused significant damage independent of the appropriations process.

**Key numbers:**
- 1 in 3 nonprofits lost government funding in the first half of 2025
- At least 22,757 nonprofit jobs lost (Jan-Jun 2025); actual total estimated at 40,000+
- DOGE terminated ~10,000+ contracts worth ~$71 billion
- USAID closed July 1, 2025; ~$9 billion in foreign aid rescinded
- EPA rescinded $20+ billion in climate/environmental justice grants
- "One Big Beautiful Bill" cut $863B from Medicaid and $295B from SNAP over 10 years
- October 2025 government shutdown compounded all of the above

## 2. Executive Orders and Policy Documents

### EO 14151: "Ending Radical and Wasteful Government DEI Programs and Preferencing"
- **Date signed:** January 20, 2025
- **What it does:** Terminates all federal government DEIA activities, positions, and programs. Directs OMB to terminate all DEI-related mandates, policies, programs, and preferences. Removes DEI-related content from federal websites and museums.
- **Legal status:** **IN EFFECT**, though some provisions have been **partially enjoined** by courts. A federal court refused to lift a preliminary injunction blocking certain enforcement provisions.
- **Sectors affected:** All nonprofits with federal grants, especially those providing DEI training, civil rights organizations, LGBTQ+ organizations, arts/culture organizations, higher education institutions.
- **Source:** [Federal Register](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/01/29/2025-01953/ending-radical-and-wasteful-government-dei-programs-and-preferencing)

### EO 14173: "Ending Illegal Discrimination and Restoring Merit-Based Opportunity"
- **Date signed:** January 21, 2025
- **What it does:** Revokes Executive Order 11246 (affirmative action requirements for federal contractors). Directs DOJ to identify up to 9 compliance investigations targeting large nonprofits, foundations ($500M+ assets), corporations, and universities ($1B+ endowments). Requires contractors to certify they do not use DEI principles. Directs agencies to "combat illegal private-sector DEI."
- **Legal status:** **IN EFFECT**, partially enjoined. AG Pam Bondi directed DOJ to "investigate, eliminate, and penalize" organizations with "illegal" DEI programs.
- **Sectors affected:** Federal contractors (many nonprofits), large foundations, universities, professional associations, civil rights organizations.
- **Source:** [Federal Register](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/01/31/2025-02097/ending-illegal-discrimination-and-restoring-merit-based-opportunity)

### OMB Memo M-25-13: Federal Spending Freeze
- **Date issued:** January 27, 2025
- **What it does:** Directed all federal agencies to pause all grants, loans, and financial assistance programs pending review for alignment with presidential priorities.
- **Legal status:** **RESCINDED** January 29, 2025 (after court intervention), but the underlying executive orders and agency-specific freezes continued independently. National Council of Nonprofits, APHA, Main Street Alliance, and SAGE sued. DC federal judge issued administrative stay Jan 28. Second judge (23 state AGs suit) issued preliminary injunction Mar 6.
- **Sectors affected:** All nonprofits receiving any federal funding.
- **Source:** [Mayer Brown Analysis](https://www.mayerbrown.com/en/insights/publications/2025/02/updates-and-summary-of-the-evolving-executive-federal-funding-freeze)

### EO 14180: FEMA Review Council
- **Date signed:** January 24, 2025
- **What it does:** Established a task force to evaluate FEMA's structure, potentially recommending elimination of the agency or cutting its workforce in half. Led to cancellation of BRIC and Flood Mitigation Assistance programs.
- **Legal status:** **IN EFFECT**
- **Sectors affected:** Disaster relief nonprofits, emergency services, community resilience organizations.

### EO "Improving Oversight of Federal Grantmaking"
- **Date signed:** August 7, 2025
- **What it does:** Requires a senior political appointee to review ALL grant applications and awards for consistency with "agency priorities" and "national interest." Allows "immediate termination for convenience." Prohibits grants that fund racial preferences, challenge sex binary, support illegal immigration, or "promote anti-American values."
- **Legal status:** **IN EFFECT**
- **Sectors affected:** All nonprofits receiving federal grants. Creates particular risk for organizations working in immigration, LGBTQ+ rights, environmental justice, civil rights.
- **Source:** [National Council of Nonprofits](https://www.councilofnonprofits.org/impacts-recent-executive-orders-nonprofits)

### "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" (Budget Reconciliation)
- **Date signed:** July 4, 2025
- **What it does:** $863B in Medicaid cuts and $295B in SNAP cuts over 10 years. Imposes Medicaid work requirements (80 hrs/month). Withholds Medicaid funding from reproductive health nonprofits (Jul 2025-Jul 2026). Rescinds IRA appropriations for DOE clean energy programs. Increases foundation excise tax (graduated rate up to 10% for foundations with $5B+ assets). Rescinded ~$9B in foreign aid funding.
- **Legal status:** **SIGNED INTO LAW**
- **Sectors affected:** Healthcare nonprofits, community health centers, food banks, reproductive health organizations, clean energy nonprofits, private foundations (via excise tax increase).
- **Source:** [Bipartisan Policy Center](https://bipartisanpolicy.org/explainer/whats-in-the-2025-house-republican-tax-bill/)

## 3. Sector-by-Sector Impact

### 1. Healthcare & Public Health

**Programs cut/frozen/at risk:**
- **HHS overall:** Proposed 26.2% reduction (from ~$127B to $93.8B); Congress largely rejected this, providing $116.8B (+$210M over FY2025)
- **NIH indirect cost rate cap (15%):** Proposed Feb 7, 2025. Would have replaced institution-specific negotiated rates (often 50%+). **STATUS: PERMANENTLY BLOCKED** by federal judge who ruled it was "arbitrary and capricious" and violated federal statute.
- **CDC:** Proposed $3.58B reduction; consolidation of infectious disease, opioid, STI, and TB programs into single $300M grant. **STATUS: PROPOSED** (Congress rejected most cuts)
- **HRSA:** Proposed $1.73B reduction targeting Healthy Start, Ryan White, rural hospital programs, family planning, 15 workforce programs. **STATUS: PROPOSED** (Congress rejected most cuts)
- **Community Health Center Fund:** Congress actually INCREASED to $4.6B for FY2026 (largest increase in a decade)
- **Medicaid work requirements:** 80 hrs/month required by Dec 31, 2026. **STATUS: IN EFFECT** (signed into law Jul 4, 2025)
- **HHS DEI grant terminations:** $800M+ in LGBTQI+ research grants terminated. **STATUS: IN EFFECT**
- **Reproductive health:** One year of Medicaid funding withheld from family planning nonprofits (Jul 2025-Jul 2026). **STATUS: IN EFFECT**

**Dollar amounts:** Medicaid cuts of $863B over 10 years; up to 10 million people estimated to lose coverage; community health centers could lose up to $32B in revenue; 5.6 million CHC patients could lose Medicaid coverage.

**Number of organizations affected:** 1,400+ community health centers nationwide; thousands of research institutions; healthcare nonprofits receiving federal grants.

**Notable examples:** Bay Area health organizations targeted for disproportionate cuts in blue states.

### 2. Education & Research

**Programs cut/frozen/at risk:**
- **Title I:** Proposed 26% cut by House Appropriations Committee. **STATUS: REJECTED** -- Congress maintained level funding.
- **IDEA (special education):** Proposed cuts. **STATUS: REJECTED** -- level funding maintained.
- **FSEOG (supplemental education grants):** Proposed complete elimination ($910M). **STATUS: REJECTED** by Congress.
- **Institute of Education Sciences:** Proposed 67% cut (to $261.3M). **STATUS: PROPOSED** (partially rejected)
- **Education Dept contract cancellations:** $336M in Regional Educational Lab contracts + $33M in Equity Assistance Center grants terminated Feb 2025. **STATUS: IN EFFECT**
- **NSF:** Proposed 57% cut (from $8.8B to $3.9B). **STATUS: REJECTED** -- Congress approved ~$8.8B (slight decrease from $9B).
- **NSF indirect cost rate cap (15%):** **STATUS: LEGAL CHALLENGES** ongoing.
- **NIH research funding:** Study sections paused; advisory councils did not convene; delays in grant review. **STATUS: ONGOING DISRUPTION**

**Dollar amounts:** $350M+ in education grants already terminated; NSF proposal would have cut $5.1B; Congress saved most research funding.

**Notable examples:** NSF canceled 701 grants labeled as "DEI" ($203M); university research disrupted across the country.

### 3. Housing & Homelessness

**Programs cut/frozen/at risk:**
- **Section 8 / Housing Choice Vouchers:** Proposed 40% cut to rental aid; block-granting to states. **STATUS: REJECTED** by Congress (maintained at FY24/FY25 levels).
- **HUD overall:** Proposed $33B cut (43.6% reduction). **STATUS: REJECTED** (Congress maintained most programs).
- **Continuum of Care (CoC):** Some communities saw 70% cuts in permanent supportive housing funding. **STATUS: MIXED** (some cuts in effect, overall program maintained).
- **Fair Housing Initiatives Program:** Proposed elimination. **STATUS: UNCERTAIN** (not funded in some versions).
- **Section 202 (elderly housing), Section 811 (disability), HOPWA:** **STATUS: FUNDED** (received increases in final appropriations).
- **HOME Investment Partnerships:** Proposed cuts. **STATUS: MAINTAINED** at near-FY25 levels.
- **CDFI Fund:** Proposed elimination. **STATUS: PARTIALLY FUNDED** by Congress.

**Dollar amounts:** $33B proposed cut to HUD; specific communities already saw millions in CoC cuts.

**Number of organizations affected:** 170,000+ people who experienced homelessness rely on vouchers; thousands of housing nonprofits nationwide.

**Notable examples:** Atlanta nonprofits lost almost $10M when HUD slashed 70% of CoC permanent supportive housing funding.

### 4. Environmental & Conservation

**Programs cut/frozen/at risk:**
- **EPA overall:** Proposed 55% cut ($4.97B reduction, from $9.14B to $4.16B). **STATUS: LARGELY REJECTED** by Congress, but EPA FY2026 budget is at an all-time low.
- **Environmental justice grants:** 781 grants targeted for termination. **STATUS: IN EFFECT** (litigation ongoing). EPA canceled 400+ grants in 4th round of DOGE cuts ($1.7B).
- **Greenhouse Gas Reduction Fund (IRA programs):** $20B in climate grants scrapped (CCIA and NCIF). **STATUS: IN EFFECT** (lawsuit filed by nonprofits, tribes, and local governments).
- **EPA science budget:** Proposed 52% cut, eliminating 1,000+ science positions. **STATUS: PARTIALLY REJECTED** by Congress.
- **DOE clean energy grants:** $7.56B terminated Oct 2, 2025. **STATUS: IN EFFECT**
- **IRA energy appropriations:** Rescinded via "One Big Beautiful Bill" for energy loans, efficiency grants, transmission planning. **STATUS: IN EFFECT**
- **NOAA research and grants:** $1.3B in programs proposed for termination. **STATUS: PARTIALLY REJECTED** by Congress.

**Dollar amounts:** $20B+ in EPA climate grants terminated; $7.56B in DOE grants terminated; $3B in environmental justice grants rescinded overall.

**Notable examples:** Casa Familiar (San Diego) expected $12.7M for zero-emission transportation in Latino community -- funds frozen then terminated. Coalition including Earthjustice sued on behalf of 350+ grant recipients.

### 5. International Development

**Programs cut/frozen/at risk:**
- **USAID:** Agency officially closed July 1, 2025. Remaining programs merged into State Dept. **STATUS: IN EFFECT**
- **Foreign aid freeze:** 83% of USAID programs cut (~5,200 contracts). **STATUS: IN EFFECT**
- **Rescissions Act:** Clawed back ~$9B allocated to humanitarian organizations. **STATUS: IN EFFECT**
- **Annual foreign aid:** Dropped to $13B post-USAID closure. **STATUS: IN EFFECT** (Congress passed $50B aid bill Feb 3, 2026)

**Dollar amounts:** Annual foreign aid dropped to $13B; ~$9B clawed back; Congress later approved $50B.

**Number of organizations affected:** Thousands of international development NGOs; 23 million children estimated to lose education access; 95 million people could lose healthcare access.

**Notable examples:** Skoll Foundation launched $25M emergency fund specifically for grantees impacted by USAID cuts. GiveWell published analysis of funding gaps.

### 6. Arts, Culture & Public Media

**Programs cut/frozen/at risk:**
- **NEA:** Proposed elimination. 50%+ of open awards terminated (May 2025). **STATUS: FUNDING RESTORED** -- Congress approved $207M for FY2026.
- **NEH:** Proposed elimination. $175M in undisbursed grants clawed back by DOGE. **STATUS: FUNDING RESTORED** -- Congress approved $207M for FY2026.
- **Corporation for Public Broadcasting (CPB):** Completely defunded (effective Oct 1, 2025). **STATUS: IN EFFECT** -- $1.1B rescinded.
- **IMLS (Institute of Museum and Library Services):** Proposed elimination. **STATUS: UNCERTAIN** (partially funded by Congress).

**Dollar amounts:** $1.1B CPB funding rescinded; NEA/NEH each restored at $207M; $175M in NEH grants clawed back.

**Number of organizations affected:** 1,500+ public media stations (115 losing 30%+ of budgets, serving 43 million people); thousands of arts organizations; state humanities councils in all 50 states.

**Notable examples:** Six foundations committed $36.5M emergency fund for public media stations. Mellon Foundation provided $15M emergency to state humanities councils.

### 7. Workforce & Employment

**Programs cut/frozen/at risk:**
- **Job Corps:** Attempted closure of 99 contract-operated centers (May-Jun 2025). **STATUS: BLOCKED** by federal court; ~$1.8B funded in FY2026.
- **DOL overall:** Proposed $4.6B cut (35% reduction) to workforce development. **STATUS: LARGELY REJECTED** by Congress.
- **WIOA programs:** "Make America Skilled Again" (MASA) proposal to consolidate workforce programs into block grant. **STATUS: REJECTED** by Congress.
- **Apprenticeship programs:** Various proposed cuts. **STATUS: MIXED**
- **DOJ-funded workforce grants:** 373 grants terminated by DOGE ($820M). **STATUS: IN EFFECT**

**Dollar amounts:** $4.6B proposed cut; $820M in DOJ grants terminated; Job Corps saved at ~$1.8B.

**Notable examples:** Thousands of Job Corps students left in limbo during attempted shutdown; federal judge blocked closure.

### 8. Emergency Services & Disaster Relief

**Programs cut/frozen/at risk:**
- **FEMA workforce:** DHS drafted plans for dramatic cuts; CORE employees (40% of FEMA workforce, 8,000+ people) targeted. **STATUS: IN EFFECT** (cuts began Jan 1, 2026).
- **FEMA Review Council:** Recommendations expected to include cutting workforce in half. **STATUS: IN PROGRESS**
- **BRIC (Building Resilient Infrastructure and Communities):** Cancelled. **STATUS: IN EFFECT**
- **Flood Mitigation Assistance:** 2025 applications not accepted. **STATUS: IN EFFECT** ($600M in planned funding lost).
- **Legal Services Corporation disaster assistance:** Excluded from emergency supplemental ($103M cut). **STATUS: IN EFFECT**
- **FEMA regional offices:** Slashed by DOGE. **STATUS: IN EFFECT**

**Dollar amounts:** $600M in flood mitigation lost; $103M in legal disaster assistance cut; billions in potential reduced disaster response capacity.

**Notable examples:** FEMA employees received termination emails on New Year's Eve 2025; cuts came as climate-related disasters intensified.

### 9. Food & Nutrition

**Programs cut/frozen/at risk:**
- **SNAP:** $295B in cuts over 10 years via "One Big Beautiful Bill." New work requirements; exclusion of many non-citizen groups (refugees, asylees, trafficking survivors). **STATUS: IN EFFECT** (signed into law).
- **WIC:** Proposed $300M cut (from $7.597B to $7.306B). Fruit and vegetable benefits sharply reduced ($1.3B taken from 5.2 million participants). Senate approved $8.2B full funding, but final outcome mixed. **STATUS: MIXED** (some cuts in effect, some rejected).
- **School lunch programs:** Various proposed cuts. **STATUS: MIXED**
- **USDA food security reports:** Ended. **STATUS: IN EFFECT**

**Dollar amounts:** $295B in SNAP cuts over decade; 2.4 million people expected pushed out of SNAP; $1.3B in WIC fruit/vegetable benefits cut.

**Number of organizations affected:** Food banks nationwide report inability to meet increased demand; 42 million people on SNAP at risk during government shutdown.

**Notable examples:** For every meal a food bank provides, SNAP provides nine -- cuts cascade disproportionately to community organizations.

### 10. Civil Rights & Social Justice

**Programs cut/frozen/at risk:**
- **Legal Services Corporation:** Proposed elimination (from $560M to $21M for close-out costs). **STATUS: PROPOSED** (opposition from 160 law firms, 40 bipartisan state AGs, 37 bipartisan chief justices).
- **DEI programs government-wide:** All federal DEIA positions terminated; grants reviewed and many cancelled. **STATUS: IN EFFECT**
- **HHS DEI grants:** $800M+ in LGBTQI+ research grants terminated. **STATUS: IN EFFECT**
- **NSF "DEI" grants:** 701 grants cancelled ($203M). **STATUS: IN EFFECT**
- **Fair Housing enforcement:** Programs defunded or reduced. **STATUS: MIXED**
- **DOJ Civil Rights Division:** Redirected from disparate impact cases to "illegal DEI" investigations. **STATUS: IN EFFECT**
- **Equity Assistance Centers:** $33M in grants terminated. **STATUS: IN EFFECT**
- **Open Society Foundations investigation:** DOJ considering terrorism and racketeering charges. **STATUS: IN PROGRESS**

**Dollar amounts:** $560M LSC at risk; $800M+ in health DEI grants terminated; $203M in NSF DEI grants cancelled; billions in civil rights infrastructure affected.

**Notable examples:** National Urban League, National Fair Housing Alliance, and AIDS Foundation of Chicago sued over DEI EOs. EEOC redirected to investigate organizations for "illegal DEI." Nearly 200 major funders pushed back on OSF investigation.

- **Participants:** Ballmer Group, Gates Foundation, Stand Together, Valhalla Foundation, John Overdeck

### Important Caveat: Philanthropy Cannot Replace Federal Funding

Multiple sources emphasize that philanthropic response, while significant, cannot come close to replacing federal funding:
- Total US charitable giving in 2024 was $592.5B (Giving USA)
- Federal grants to nonprofits: ~$303B annually
- Foundation giving is a fraction of total charitable giving
- The Chronicle of Philanthropy noted that foundation response "fails to match Covid response" in speed and scale
- Many large foundations remained "largely mute" in the early months

### New Threat: Foundation Excise Tax Increase

The "One Big Beautiful Bill" creates a graduated excise tax on private foundation net investment income:
- Current rate: 1.39%
- New rates: Up to **10% for foundations with $5B+ assets**
- Estimated impact: $15.9B decrease in foundation distributions to nonprofits over 10 years
- Long-term: Foundation distributions to nonprofits could decrease 3-15%
- **Source:** [Johnson Center for Philanthropy](https://johnsoncenter.org/blog/how-proposed-private-foundation-tax-increases-could-reshape-grants-to-nonprofits/)

## Notes

### Accuracy Caveats
1. Dollar figures for DOGE terminations are disputed. DOGE's own website claims higher savings than independent analyses confirm.
2. The "One Big Beautiful Bill" 10-year cost estimates vary by source (CBO vs. White House vs. independent analyses).
3. Many executive actions are subject to ongoing litigation; legal status can change rapidly.
4. The distinction between "proposed" and "in effect" is critical -- many of the most dramatic proposed cuts were rejected by Congress.
5. Some agency-level actions (grant terminations, staff reductions) occurred independent of the appropriations process and remain in effect even though Congress funded the programs.

### Key Takeaway for TheGrantScout Clients
While Congress rejected most of the administration's proposed discretionary spending cuts, the damage to the nonprofit sector has been significant through:
- **Executive action:** Grant terminations, DEI restrictions, the grantmaking oversight EO
- **Reconciliation law:** Medicaid and SNAP cuts are law and will impact health and nutrition nonprofits for years
- **Agency disruption:** USAID closure, FEMA cuts, EPA grant rescissions, DOGE terminations
- **Chilling effects:** Many nonprofits self-censored DEI language and programs; hiring slowed dramatically
- **Government shutdown:** Compounded existing stress in Oct 2025-Feb 2026

Private foundations are stepping up but cannot replace federal funding at scale. The new foundation excise tax may further reduce philanthropic capacity over time.
