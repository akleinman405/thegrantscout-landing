## 2. Three Customer Types Discovered

### Type 1: Calendar Managers

**Characteristics:**
- Experienced grant writers who already know most opportunities
- Well-organized but stretched thin
- Have existing relationships and pipelines
- Primary pain point is keeping track of deadlines and requirements

**What They Want:**
- Organization of information in one place
- Reminders of upcoming deadlines
- Nothing overlooked - comprehensive coverage
- Annual calendar with selective alerts (not weekly bombardment)
- Could potentially value a "grant writing concierge" tier

**Example Organizations:**
- **Andy (RHF):** "Info organized in one place was helpful even though he already knew the opportunities"
- **Mariam (SNS):** Suggested "annual calendar with selective alerts" (3 alerts/year vs. weekly)

**Product Implications:**
- Value proposition is time savings and organization, NOT discovery
- May prefer less frequent, more targeted reports
- Dashboard/calendar features could be high value

---

### Type 2: Niche Seekers

**Characteristics:**
- Operate in specialized geographies or sectors
- Harder to find relevant opportunities through typical channels
- May have unique eligibility requirements
- Need discovery of funders they wouldn't naturally find

**What They Want:**
- Discovery of NEW funding sources (not ones they already know)
- Funders who align with their specific niche
- Local/regional funders that national databases miss
- Understanding of geographic giving patterns

**Example Organizations:**
- **Ka Ulukoa:** Hawaii-specific youth athletics/mental health - limited local foundation landscape
- **Horizons National:** Hyperlocal funding in 50+ specific communities where program sites are located

**Product Implications:**
- Geographic matching and regional funders are critical
- Federal/state opportunities less valuable than local foundations
- Need deep data on giving patterns by geography

---

### Type 3: Relationship Builders

**Characteristics:**
- Prefer prospecting and relationship-building over formal applications
- Complex/multi-faceted missions that don't fit standard grant categories
- Historical success through network-based funding
- Now seeking to diversify beyond personal relationships

**What They Want:**
- Foundation profiles for LOI/inquiry approach
- ~5 foundations to build relationships with (not 50)
- Contact information, events, connection points
- Board member overlap and network mapping
- Understanding of foundation giving style and preferences

**Example Organizations:**
- **Consuelo (PSMF):** "Quick intro proposal strategy to multiple foundations, see who responds"

**Product Implications:**
- Foundation-focused reports > Opportunity-focused reports for this segment
- LOI/inquiry format opportunities more valuable than formal applications
- Contact and networking information is essential
- Less focus on specific deadlines, more on foundation fit

## 5. Product Implications

### What to Change Based on Feedback

**Report Format Adjustments:**

1. **Remove "About [Org]" section** - Clients know their own organization
2. **Add complete Funder Snapshots** with 8 data-backed metrics:
   - Annual Giving
   - Typical Grant (median + range)
   - Geographic Focus
   - Repeat Funding Rate
   - Giving Style (general vs. program-specific)
   - Recipient Profile
   - Funding Trend
   - Comparable Grant
3. **Convert timeline to week-by-week** (8 weeks, not 60 days)
4. **Offer two report types:**
   - Opportunity-focused (for Calendar Managers, Niche Seekers)
   - Foundation-focused (for Relationship Builders)

**Feature Priorities:**

| Priority | Feature | Customer Type Served |
|----------|---------|---------------------|
| High | Foundation-focused report option | Relationship Builders |
| High | Funder Snapshot with database metrics | All |
| High | QA checklist before delivery | All |
| Medium | Annual calendar view | Calendar Managers |
| Medium | Grant writing assistance tier | Calendar Managers |
| Medium | Contact/networking information | Relationship Builders |
| Low | More frequent reports | None requested |

**QA Process Improvements:**

Before any report is delivered:
- [ ] All URLs manually tested
- [ ] All deadlines verified as current
- [ ] Grant program names verified against official sources
- [ ] Funder Snapshots populated with database queries
- [ ] Positioning strategies reference specific data points
- [ ] No "About [Org]" section included
- [ ] Timeline uses week-by-week format

## 6. Unanswered Questions

### What We Still Need to Learn

| Question | Current Signal | How to Resolve |
|----------|----------------|----------------|
| Opportunity-focused vs. foundation-focused preference? | Different customers want different things | BG1 Report 2 A/B test |
| Optimal report frequency? | Monthly seems right; some want annual calendar | Test different cadences |
| Grant writing tier demand? | Mariam interested | Survey more beta testers |
| How to handle multi-mission orgs? | Foundation-first approach (per PSMF) | Test with more complex orgs |
| What's the discovery vs. organization value split? | Calendar Managers value org; Niche Seekers need discovery | Segment analysis |
| Vertical vs. horizontal product? | Start horizontal | See which verticals engage most |

### Questions to Ask Future Testers

1. How did you find grants before this? How much time did that take?
2. Of the opportunities in this report, how many were completely new to you?
3. Would you prefer fewer, highly-curated matches or more comprehensive coverage?
4. What single piece of information would make you most likely to apply?
5. If you could only choose one: perfect match recommendations OR grant writing assistance?
6. What's your biggest frustration with foundation prospecting?
7. How do you currently track deadlines and requirements?

### A/B Tests to Run

| Test | Hypothesis | Measure |
|------|------------|---------|
| 5 opportunities vs. 10 opportunities | Fewer = higher quality perception | "How actionable was this report?" rating |
| Opportunity-first vs. Foundation-first format | Different customer types prefer different formats | Engagement and conversion by segment |
| Weekly vs. Monthly delivery | Monthly preferred | Open rates, feedback quality |
| With Funder Snapshot vs. Without | Snapshot increases perceived data backing | Trust/confidence rating |
