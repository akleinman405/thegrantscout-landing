- **Tier A (Open):** Full processing, primary report section
- **Tier B (Relationship-Required):** Lightweight processing, secondary report section with relationship paths
- **Tier C (Dormant):** Minimal processing, monitor only

## Agent Proposals Summary

### Points of Agreement (All Three Agents)

1. Accessibility must be assessed earlier in the pipeline
2. Better classification taxonomy needed (not just accessible/not)
3. Community foundations require separate handling
4. Mission fit alone is insufficient - need viability scoring
5. IRS indicator is necessary but not sufficient - website verification required
6. Geographic eligibility tracking needed (`funded_states`)
7. Human override capability required

### Resolved Disagreement

**Issue:** Should invite-only foundations be eliminated early?

**Data Engineer/PM:** Yes, for efficiency
**Nonprofit Expert:** No, they're valuable for relationship-building

**Resolution:** Classify for processing efficiency, but never eliminate based on IRS indicator alone. High-fit Tier B foundations included in report with relationship guidance.

## Edge Cases Documented

The new guide includes handling for:

1. **Local/Community Nonprofits** - Lower thresholds, auto-include community foundation
2. **National Nonprofits** - Higher thresholds, exclude hyper-local funders
3. **Identity-Based Missions** - 0.75/0.25 fit/viability weights, accept smaller pools
4. **Niche Sectors** - Include sector-specific databases
5. **Startups** - De-prioritize track-record-heavy funders, emphasize relationships
6. **Large Established** - Exclude small grants, include corporate foundations
