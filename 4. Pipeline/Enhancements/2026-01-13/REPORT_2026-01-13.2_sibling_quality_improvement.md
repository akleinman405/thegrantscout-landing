## Improved Sibling Finding Process

### Step 1: Define True Sibling Criteria
For PSMF, true siblings must have:
1. Patient safety OR healthcare quality mission
2. Multi-program structure (not single-issue)
3. Education/training component
4. Evidence-based approach
5. Hospital/health system engagement
6. Budget $200K-$3M

### Step 2: Database Search with Keywords
```sql
SELECT ein, organization_name, total_revenue, mission_description
FROM f990_2025.nonprofit_returns
WHERE total_revenue BETWEEN 200000 AND 3000000
AND (
    LOWER(mission_description) LIKE '%patient safety%'
    OR LOWER(mission_description) LIKE '%preventable harm%'
    OR LOWER(mission_description) LIKE '%healthcare quality%'
    OR LOWER(mission_description) LIKE '%quality improvement%'
)
AND LEFT(ntee_code, 1) IN ('E', 'F', 'G', 'H', 'B')
```

### Step 3: Manual Verification (Per Candidate)
Have agent review each candidate for:
- [ ] Mission explicitly mentions patient safety/quality
- [ ] Multiple programs listed in 990 Part III
- [ ] Education/training mentioned
- [ ] Works with hospitals/health systems
- [ ] Active (filed 990 in last 2 years)

### Step 4: Additional Sources to Search
1. **AHRQ PSO Registry** - ~90 Patient Safety Organizations
2. **State patient safety centers** - Search "[State] Patient Safety Center"
3. **Conference speaker lists** - IHI Forum, PSMF Summit attendees
4. **Grant databases** - Who do Commonwealth Fund, RWJF fund for patient safety?

### Step 5: Validate Against Known Organizations
Cross-reference with known patient safety orgs:
- Institute for Healthcare Improvement (too large but good reference)
- Leapfrog Group
- State PSOs (Maryland, Missouri, etc.)
- CAPS, Josie King Foundation
