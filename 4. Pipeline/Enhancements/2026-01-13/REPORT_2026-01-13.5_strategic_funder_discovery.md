## Why Sibling Analysis Failed

### The Category Error

We searched for "patient safety nonprofits" and found **operational organizations**:
- State Patient Safety Organizations (federally mandated)
- Hospital quality consultancies
- Single-issue advocacy groups

These exist to **deliver compliance services** and are funded by:
- State health departments
- Hospital association dues
- Federal quality improvement contracts

**PSMF doesn't compete in this funding pool at all.**

### The Three Structural Mismatches

| Dimension | PSMF | Typical "Sibling" |
|-----------|------|-------------------|
| Geographic scope | Global (48+ countries) | State/regional |
| Theory of change | Movement building, education | Direct service delivery |
| Revenue model | Foundation grants, corporate sponsors | Government contracts, hospital fees |

### Why Only 12 of 40 Siblings Had Foundation Grants

This is the most revealing data point. The 28 siblings with NO foundation grants operate on:
- Government contracts (not in foundation databases)
- Hospital system partnerships (B2B, not philanthropic)
- Membership dues

**Insight:** Operational patient safety organizations don't rely on foundation philanthropy. PSMF's foundation-dependent model makes it structurally different.

### Is PSMF "Sibling-less"?

**Yes, in the patient safety category.** PSMF is the only global, multi-program, movement-building organization focused specifically on patient safety.

**But PSMF has "cousins"** - organizations that share its OPERATING MODEL:
- Institute for Healthcare Improvement (IHI)
- Jhpiego (global health training)
- Seed Global Health (fellowship model)
- Global Health Council (convening/coalition)

**These organizations' funders are PSMF's true targets.**

## Notes

### Why This Analysis Matters

The sibling-based approach is a good DEFAULT methodology for most clients. But for organizations like PSMF that operate at the intersection of multiple categories (global + education + advocacy + patient safety), we need **model-based matching** rather than **category-based matching**.

### Generalized Lesson

When sibling analysis underperforms, ask:
1. Is the client "structurally different" from apparent siblings?
2. What is the client's OPERATING MODEL, not just their CAUSE?
3. Who funds organizations with similar models in DIFFERENT cause areas?

### For PSMF Specifically

Stop thinking: "Who funds patient safety organizations?"
Start thinking: "Who funds global health fellowship programs?"

That reframe unlocks an entirely different (and better) set of foundation prospects.
