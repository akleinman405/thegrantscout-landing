# Patient Safety Movement Foundation (PSMF) - Organization Profile

**Date:** 2026-01-13
**EIN:** 46-2730379
**Location:** Irvine, California
**Founded:** 2012 by Joe Kiani

---

## Mission & Vision

**Vision:** A safer healthcare system with ZERO preventable patient harm.

**Mission:** To disseminate Actionable Evidence-Based Practices, advance their integration by healthcare organizations, and cultivate data transparency by 2030.

**Core Belief:** One patient death is one too many.

---

## The Problem They Address

- 200,000-400,000 patients die from preventable incidents in American hospitals annually
- Preventable medical errors are the 3rd leading cause of death (after heart disease and cancer)
- This is a global problem affecting all healthcare systems

---

## Programs & Initiatives

PSMF runs **multiple programs simultaneously** - they are NOT a single-program organization:

### 1. Global Interprofessional Patient Safety Fellowship (Priority Program)
**The "Kiani Fellowship"**
- **Duration:** 12 months (January-December)
- **Format:** Monthly live virtual classroom sessions
- **Curriculum:** Year-long program developed by patient safety experts
- **Target:** Healthcare professionals from all WHO regions, any healthcare profession
- **Focus:** Theory and practice of patient safety, aligned with WHO Global Patient Safety Action Plan
- **Special emphasis:** Developing leaders from lower-middle and middle-income countries
- **Fellows include:** Intensivists, pediatric specialists, WHO technical officers, QI coaches, anesthesiologists, patient safety directors, accreditation surveyors from Argentina, Sudan, Lebanon, Uganda, Pakistan, Indonesia, Kenya, Malaysia, Australia
- **Learning model:** Monthly readings, active participation in discussions, application to practice

### 2. Actionable Patient Safety Solutions (APSS)
- **What:** Evidence-based clinical best practice documents (50+ topics)
- **Cost:** FREE to all hospitals
- **Development:** Workgroups of patient safety experts, healthcare tech professionals, hospital leaders, patient advocates
- **Organization:** Grouped head-to-toe (as experienced in patient care)
- **Components:**
  - APSS Blueprints (evidence-based guides)
  - APSS Education (webinars, videos, blogs, journal articles)
  - APSS Coaching
- **Mobile:** APSS App for smartphone access

### 3. Hospital Commitment Program
- **Scale:** 4,793+ hospitals across 48 countries committed
- **Impact:** 273,077+ lives saved since inception; 90,000+ in 2018 alone
- **Commitment:** Formal pledge to ZERO preventable deaths
- **Community:** Solidarity network of hospitals working toward common goal

### 4. Open Data Pledge
- Healthcare technology companies pledge to share data
- Enables development of predictive algorithms to identify errors before they become fatal
- Critical for data transparency goal

### 5. Patient Safety Leadership Association
- Forum for Chief Quality and Patient Safety Officers
- Global executive network
- Champions integration of evidence-based solutions and new technologies

### 6. Annual World Patient Safety, Science & Technology Summit
- Brings together patients, providers, medical tech companies, government, employers, payers
- Presents actionable solutions
- Highlights hospital commitments
- 11+ years running

### 7. Patient Safety Curriculum & Education
- Investment in future healthcare professionals
- For medical students, nursing students, pharmacists, behavioral therapists
- Curriculum development for healthcare professional education
- One of 17 APSS topics
- **Example:** $100,000 Patient Safety Curriculum Award (with Anesthesia Patient Safety Foundation)

### 8. Policy Advocacy
- Annual trips to Capitol Hill
- Advocate for payer policy changes
- Incentivize evidence-based solutions
- Promote public reporting and quality over quantity
