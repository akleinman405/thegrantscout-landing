## Problem Statement (Draft)

Healthcare professionals face ethical dilemmas daily when patient safety conflicts with institutional pressures, resource constraints, or systemic barriers. Despite patient safety being a fundamental ethical obligation, few health professional education programs explicitly prepare learners to navigate these tensions.

The consequences are significant:
- 200,000+ preventable patient deaths occur annually in US hospitals
- Healthcare workers experience moral distress when unable to advocate for safety
- New practitioners lack frameworks for addressing safety concerns within complex systems

PSMF's Kiani Fellowship has developed evidence-based curriculum on patient safety leadership, currently delivered to healthcare professionals globally. **This project will adapt these materials into standalone ethics training modules specifically designed for US health professions education.**

---

## Project Goals

1. **Develop** 3-5 patient safety ethics case modules suitable for integration into US medical, nursing, and allied health curricula
2. **Pilot** these modules with US health professions learners (medical students, nursing students, residents)
3. **Evaluate** learner outcomes using validated assessment tools
4. **Disseminate** findings and make modules freely available to health professions educators

---

## Project Design

### Phase 1: Curriculum Adaptation (Months 1-4)
- Review existing Kiani Fellowship curriculum for ethics-relevant content
- Identify 3-5 patient safety scenarios with strong ethical dimensions (e.g., speaking up about errors, resource allocation, disclosure obligations)
- Develop case-based learning modules with facilitator guides
- Convene advisory group of medical educators for review

### Phase 2: Pilot Testing (Months 5-9)
- Partner with 2-3 US health professions schools to pilot modules
- Target 100+ learners across medicine, nursing, pharmacy
- Collect pre/post assessments on ethical reasoning, safety attitudes, and self-efficacy
- Gather facilitator feedback on implementation

### Phase 3: Refinement & Dissemination (Months 10-12)
- Revise modules based on pilot feedback
- Publish findings in health professions education journal
- Make modules freely available on PSMF website and via MedEdPORTAL
- Present at national medical education conference (e.g., AAMC)
