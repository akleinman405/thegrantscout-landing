## What Makes a TRUE PSMF Sibling

Based on PSMF's profile, true siblings should have:
1. **Patient safety / healthcare quality focus** - core mission alignment
2. **Multi-program structure** - NOT single-issue organizations
3. **Education/training component** - fellowships, curricula, professional development
4. **Evidence-based approach** - toolkits, best practices, data-driven
5. **Hospital/health system engagement** - works with providers
6. **Similar budget** - $200K-$3M (comparable operational scale)

## How to Find More (Scaling to 100)

To expand from 20 to 100 siblings:

1. **State-level search**: Each state has patient safety centers (like Maryland, Missouri). Search for "[State] Patient Safety" in 990 database.

2. **PSO registry**: AHRQ maintains list of ~90 Patient Safety Organizations - pull their 990s.

3. **Medical specialty patient safety orgs**: Each specialty (cardiology, oncology, etc.) may have safety-focused organizations.

4. **Regional healthcare coalitions**: Many regions have QI collaboratives.

5. **International**: Canadian Patient Safety Institute, UK equivalents (if US-based operations).
