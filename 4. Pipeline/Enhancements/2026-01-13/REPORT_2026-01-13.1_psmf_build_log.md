## The Matching Goal

**Find foundations who gave TARGET GRANTS (matching purpose) to SIMILAR NONPROFITS (siblings).**

This is the intersection of two things:
1. **Similar nonprofits (siblings)** - organizations with missions like PSMF
2. **Target grants** - grants for the KIND of work PSMF does (fellowship programs, clinical education, patient safety)

**The key metric:** How many times did a foundation give a target grant to a sibling nonprofit?

### Phase 2c: Sibling Identification - COMPLETE

**How Siblings Were Identified (EXACTLY):**

```sql
-- Step 1: Generate client embedding (Python)
from sentence_transformers import SentenceTransformer
model = SentenceTransformer('all-MiniLM-L6-v2')
client_embedding = model.encode(client_mission_text)

-- Step 2: Find similar nonprofits via pgvector
SELECT
    enm.ein,
    ROUND((1.0 - (enm.mission_embedding_v <=> '[embedding]'::vector(384)))::NUMERIC, 4) as similarity
FROM f990_2025.emb_nonprofit_missions enm
JOIN f990_2025.nonprofit_returns nr ON enm.ein = nr.ein
WHERE nr.total_revenue BETWEEN 73035 AND 1825885  -- 0.2x to 5.0x budget
  AND enm.ein != '462730379'
  AND (enm.mission_embedding_v <=> '[embedding]'::vector(384)) <= 0.50
ORDER BY similarity DESC;
```

**Technical Details:**
- Embedding model: `all-MiniLM-L6-v2` (384 dimensions)
- Embedding column: `emb_nonprofit_missions.mission_embedding_v` (vector type)
- Similarity operator: `<=>` (pgvector cosine distance)
- Threshold: similarity >= 0.50
- Budget filter: 0.2x to 5.0x client budget

**What Was NOT Used:**
- Foundation average embeddings (`calc_foundation_avg_embedding`) - NOT used
- NTEE codes - NOT used (embeddings capture sector)
- Geography - NOT used for siblings (handled at foundation level)

**Results:**
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total siblings | 91 |
| Similarity range | 0.50 - 0.61 |
| Quality | ~82% relevant |

**Table Created:** `f990_2025.calc_client_siblings`

### Phase 2d: Foundations That Funded Siblings - COMPLETE

**Query Used:**
```sql
SELECT
    fg.foundation_ein,
    COUNT(DISTINCT fg.recipient_ein) as siblings_funded,
    COUNT(*) as total_grants,
    SUM(fg.amount) as total_amount
FROM f990_2025.fact_grants fg
WHERE fg.recipient_ein IN (
    SELECT sibling_ein FROM f990_2025.calc_client_siblings
    WHERE client_ein = '462730379'
)
GROUP BY fg.foundation_ein;
```

**Results:**
- 168 foundations have funded at least 1 PSMF sibling
- Only 1 foundation funded 2+ siblings (weak individual signal)
- Top funder by amount: Dell Foundation ($950K to Central Texas Allied Health Institute)
