## Methodology Lessons Learned

### Why Sibling Analysis Failed

**The Core Problem:** We categorized PSMF as a "patient safety nonprofit" and found similar organizations. But PSMF operates completely differently than state patient safety centers.

| Dimension | PSMF | State Patient Safety Centers |
|-----------|------|------------------------------|
| Geographic scope | Global (48+ countries) | Single state |
| Revenue model | Foundation grants, corporate sponsors | Government contracts, hospital fees |
| Theory of change | Movement building, education | Direct compliance services |
| Foundation grant reliance | High | Low (28 of 40 had ZERO foundation grants) |

**The Generalizable Lesson:** Match on PROGRAM TYPE, not CAUSE AREA.

- Wrong question: "Who funds patient safety organizations?"
- Right question: "Who funds global health fellowship programs?"

### Red Flags for Structural Uniqueness

When 2+ of these are present, STOP sibling analysis and switch to program-type matching:

| Red Flag | How to Detect | PSMF Example |
|----------|---------------|--------------|
| Low sibling grant rate | <50% of top 20 cause-area peers have foundation grants | 12 of 40 siblings had grants |
| Geographic outlier | Client is global; peers are domestic | PSMF global; peers state-level |
| Revenue model outlier | Client's primary revenue differs from 70%+ of peers | PSMF foundation-funded; peers government-funded |
| "Movement" or "platform" language | Client describes self as movement, network, or platform | PSMF is "movement building" |
| Fellowship/training as core program | Client's flagship is education/training program | Kiani Fellowship is priority |

### Decision Tree for Future Clients

```
STEP 1: Revenue Model Check
├── Foundation grants <30%? → Flag for program-type matching
└── Foundation grants >30%? → Continue

STEP 2: Geographic Scope
├── Client scope ≠ peer scope? → Flag for program-type matching
└── Scopes match? → Continue

STEP 3: Pilot Sibling Search (20 orgs)
├── <50% have foundation grants? → STOP, use program-type matching
└── >50% have grants? → Continue with sibling analysis

STEP 4: Quality Check (score top 10 sibling grants)
├── Average score ≤7? → Supplement with program-type matches
└── Average score >7? → Complete sibling analysis
```

### New Phase 2C: Structural Uniqueness Assessment

Score 0-12 based on:
- Hybrid operating model (+2)
- Fewer than 3 true peer organizations (+3)
- Peers don't share foundation funders (+2)
- Operates as funder/regranter (+3)
- Program type doesn't match peers (+2)

**Score 5+ = STRUCTURALLY UNIQUE** → Use program-type matching instead of sibling analysis

### Strategy Selection

| Client Situation | Strategy |

| Standard client + Relationship Builder | D: Hybrid → Deep profiles |

## Notes

### Key Strategic Insight

The PSMF case proves that **"what you care about" and "how you operate" are orthogonal dimensions**. Sibling matching works on the first; funder matching works on the second. When a client is an outlier on the operating dimension, sibling matching will fail regardless of how precisely we identify cause-area peers.

### For PSMF Specifically

- **Josiah Macy Jr. Foundation** is the top priority - open applications, perfect mission fit
- **Doctors Company Foundation** is excellent but paused - set reminder for mid-2026
- **Joe Kiani's network** is PSMF's unique asset - Medtronic requires warm introduction
- **Duke Endowment** requires geographic framing (NC/SC pilot) but has explicit Quality & Safety priority

### Quality of Original Prospect List

- 5 of 10 foundations were poor fits (50% false positive rate)
- Common error: Assuming foundations that run their own programs also fund external programs
- Common error: Not verifying open vs. invitation-only grantmaking
