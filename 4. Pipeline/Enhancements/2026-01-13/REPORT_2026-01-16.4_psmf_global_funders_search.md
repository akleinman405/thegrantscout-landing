## The "Invite-Only" Reality

### Key Finding

**The foundations most likely to fund PSMF's global fellowship (Skoll, Rockefeller, Ford, Schooner) are all invite-only.**

This is consistent across major philanthropies:
- Gates Foundation: Proactive grantmaking
- Ford Foundation: <1% unsolicited success rate
- Rockefeller Foundation: No unsolicited proposals
- Skoll Foundation: Referral-based only
- MacArthur Foundation: Generally doesn't fund unsolicited

### Why This Matters

PSMF cannot simply "apply" to the foundations with the best mission alignment. Instead, PSMF needs to:

1. **Build relationships** with program officers
2. **Get referred** by existing grantees or partners
3. **Attend convenings** where these funders are present (Skoll World Forum, etc.)
4. **Apply to open programs** (Bellagio residency, etc.) to build visibility

### Potential Introduction Paths

| Rockefeller | WHO partnerships, global health policy networks |

## Notes

### Key Insight

The foundation landscape for global health education is bifurcated:

1. **Major foundations** (Gates, Ford, Rockefeller, Skoll) have the resources and mission alignment but are **invite-only**
2. **Accessible foundations** (Macy, Brinson, Doctors Company) are open to applications but have **US-only restrictions**

This creates a strategic choice for PSMF:
- **Path A:** Apply to US-focused foundations with US-framed projects (smaller grants, faster)
- **Path B:** Build relationships with global foundations for larger, longer-term support (more effort, higher ceiling)

### Recommendation

Pursue **both paths simultaneously**:
- Apply to Macy/Brinson/Pfizer now for near-term wins
- Begin relationship-building with Skoll/Schooner/Rockefeller for future
- Wait for Doctors Company to reopen (best fit + accessible)
