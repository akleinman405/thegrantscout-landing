## Risk Analysis: Exclusion Lists

### Proposed Change
Ask clients: "Which foundations have you applied to, received grants from, or are currently tracking?"

### Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Description |
|------|-------------|--------|-------------|
| **Client memory errors** | HIGH | MEDIUM | Clients forget funders, especially smaller grants from 2+ years ago. Our database has their grant history - why rely on memory? |
| **Over-exclusion** | MEDIUM | HIGH | Enthusiastic clients might list every foundation they've ever heard of, excluding good opportunities |
| **Format inconsistency** | HIGH | LOW | Clients will give names, we need EINs. "Weinberg Foundation" could be Harry & Jeanette Weinberg, Bernard Weinberg, etc. |
| **Incomplete coverage** | HIGH | MEDIUM | Only captures known funders, not foundations the client has been rejected by (which might be worth re-trying) |

### Mitigation Recommendations

1. **Auto-pull grant history from database** - We have 8.3M grants. If PSMF received a grant from Weinberg in 2022, we should find it via `fact_grants.recipient_name` fuzzy matching, not ask them.

2. **Combine client input with database lookup** - Client list catches pending applications and informal relationships; database catches actual grant history.

3. **EIN validation** - When client gives a name, resolve it to EIN immediately and confirm. "Did you mean Harry & Jeanette Weinberg Foundation (EIN 52-6037034)?"

4. **Differentiate exclusion types**:
   - "Currently funded by" (exclude entirely)
   - "Recently rejected by" (flag for review, might retry)
   - "Planning to apply" (exclude to avoid duplication)
   - "Have relationship with" (might still include for different programs)

## Risk Analysis: Expanding to 500 Foundations

### Proposed Change
Expand scored output from top 100 to top 500 foundations.

### Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Description |
|------|-------------|--------|-------------|
| **Geographic mismatch amplified** | HIGH | CRITICAL | More foundations = more mismatches. FCSD got Aptos foundations (400 miles from San Diego). 500 foundations won't fix state-level matching. |
| **Selection paradox** | HIGH | MEDIUM | How do you pick 5 from 500? Manual review becomes infeasible. Need filtering logic that doesn't exist. |
| **Processing time increase** | MEDIUM | LOW | 5x more foundations = 5x more snapshot queries, ~25 minutes instead of ~5 minutes |
| **Quality dilution** | MEDIUM | MEDIUM | Top 500 includes lower-quality matches. Risk of surfacing mediocre foundations that crowd out manually curated better ones. |
| **Cognitive overload** | HIGH | HIGH | Without better filtering tools, operator must manually review 500 foundations per client. At 1 minute each = 8+ hours. |

### The Fundamental Problem

The scoring model uses `match_state_pct` as a top feature (+1.69 coefficient). This means:
- California foundations rank high for California clients
- But California has 58 counties and 480+ cities
- San Diego client gets Aptos foundation (400 miles away)
- Both are "California" but completely different geographies

**Expanding to 500 foundations doesn't fix this.** It just gives you 400 more wrong-state-match foundations.

### Mitigation Recommendations

1. **Add city/county to scoring model** - Before expanding pool, fix the geographic matching:
   ```
   match_city_pct = foundation's % of grants in client's city
   match_county_pct = foundation's % of grants in client's county
   ```

2. **Stratified sampling instead of flat top-500**:
   - Top 100 overall
   - Top 50 by client state
   - Top 50 by client city/county (if available)
   - Top 50 by NTEE sector
   - Top 50 by grant size range

3. **Add minimum viability filters before outputting**:
   - Has granted in past 3 years
   - Has website or contact info
   - Total giving > $50K/year
   - Not invitation-only

4. **Create filtering UI/script** - If expanding to 500, need a way to filter:
   ```bash
   python filter_foundations.py --geography "San Diego County" --sector "Human Services" --grant-size "25K-100K"
   ```

## Critical Gaps Not Addressed

### 1. Client Intake Quality

**The Problem:**
- 5/5 clients had empty `mission_statement` fields
- 5/5 clients had empty `grant_size_target` fields
- Client priorities are vague ("senior services" instead of "meal delivery expansion in West Oakland")

**Impact:**
- Algorithm can't match on mission if mission is empty
- Can't filter by grant size if target unknown
- Manual curation relies on inferred needs, not stated needs

**Build Plan Addresses:** NO

**Recommendation:** Add required intake validation:
```
REQUIRED before pipeline runs:
[ ] Mission statement (min 50 words)
[ ] Grant size target (min/max range)
[ ] Primary geographic focus (city/county, not just state)
[ ] Top 3 specific priorities this quarter
[ ] Known funders to exclude (with EINs)
```

### 2. Foundation Data Freshness

**The Problem:**
- 990-PF data is 1-2 years old (2023 filings are latest available in 2025)
- Foundations change priorities, staff, contact info
- Deadlines from 2023 filings are expired

**Impact:**
- Contact info may be wrong
- Application status may have changed
- Priorities may have shifted

**Build Plan Addresses:** NO

**Recommendation:**
- Flag data age in reports: "Data from 2023 990-PF filing"
- Require web verification for enrichment
- Track foundation website last-checked date
- Mark unverified data clearly: "Contact: [UNVERIFIED]"

### 3. The "Discovery" Problem

**The Problem:**
How do you find foundations not in our 143K database?
- New foundations (formed after our last 990 import)
- Small foundations under 990-PF filing threshold
- Corporate giving programs (not 990-PF filers)
- Government grants (different data source)

**Impact:**
- Clients like PSMF wanting patient-safety-specific funders may not find them in 990-PF data
- Niche sectors have foundations we don't know about

**Build Plan Addresses:** NO

**Recommendation:**
- Acknowledge database limitations in reports
- Add manual research step for niche sectors
- Consider integrating Foundation Directory Online or Candid data
- Track "wish list" foundations that clients mention but aren't in database

### 4. Multi-Priority Clients

**The Problem:**
- FCSD has two priorities: bakery expansion ($100K operating) and community center (capital campaign)
- Current pipeline treats client as single entity, not per-priority
- Result: Only first priority addressed, second ignored

**Impact:**
- 50% of FCSD's needs unmet
- Client perceives low value

**Build Plan Addresses:** NO

**Recommendation:**
- Run pipeline per-priority, not per-client
- FCSD should get two foundation lists:
  1. FCSD-Bakery: Small/medium grants, operating support, food/nutrition focus
  2. FCSD-Building: Capital funders, larger grants, community development focus
