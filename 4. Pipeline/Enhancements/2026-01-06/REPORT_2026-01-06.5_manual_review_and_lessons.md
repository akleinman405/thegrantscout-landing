## What Was Learned

### 1. Curated Foundations Require Manual Comparable Grant Selection

**Problem:** The pipeline uses automated SQL queries to find "comparable grants" but these are based on recipient size/NTEE matching, not sector keywords. Result: A healthcare foundation shows a random grant to a similar-sized org, not a healthcare-relevant grant.

**Learning:** Curated foundations need curated comparable grants. The human researcher who selects the foundation should also select 2-3 relevant grant examples.

### 2. Pipeline Quality Gates Are Insufficient

**Problem:** The 08_render_report.py script has quality checks but only looks at structural issues (same-state representation, missing fields). It doesn't verify that comparable grants match the client's sector.

**Learning:** Need semantic validation - do the comparable grants actually relate to the client's work?

### 3. Foundation Geographic Focus != Grant Distribution

**Problem:** A foundation may be based in CA but fund nationally, or based nationally but fund only LA. The "99% in-state" metric hides within-state concentration.

**Learning:** Need city/region-level geographic analysis, not just state-level.

### 4. Template Language Masks Bad Matches

**Problem:** Every foundation gets the same "has a track record of supporting organizations like [Client]" regardless of whether that's actually true.

**Learning:** Templated language should be conditional on data quality. If no comparable grant found, say "potential fit based on..." not "track record."

### 5. The 02b_add_foundation Script Works But Requires Follow-through

**Problem:** Adding foundations to snapshots doesn't automatically mean they appear in final reports - they also need to be in the viable list with high priority.

**Learning:** Document the full workflow: add to snapshots → add to viable list → verify in final output.
